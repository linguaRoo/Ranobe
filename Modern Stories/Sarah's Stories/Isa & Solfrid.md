# Isa and Solfrid

> [!Author’s Notes]  
> This story is inspired by _The North Wind and the Sun,_ however it is an original story in which the Sun, Solfrid, bends the North Wind, Isa, to her serve her.

## Prologue

Long ago, there lived a beautiful woman by the name of Isa. She had eyes of piercing blue, and hair as fair as the mountain snow. She lived a life of freedom in the northern mountains, and learned the magic of the winds and snow.

One day, she saw a fair maiden in the valley below her mountain. Though the fair maiden had skin like the snow, her eyes were like the valley leaves and her hair like fire flowed long and wild from her face. She wore finer clothes than the tribe of Isa’s mountain and valley, and she wandered through the valley as freely as Isa did the mountain.

As the days passed, Isa watched the fiery maiden from afar, and relished in the joy this maiden brought to the people of the valley. Until, one day, Isa fell from her mountain, and broke her leg. This was the day her life changed entirely.

## Chapter 1

Isa cursed her fortune as she tried to rise to her feet. Clenching her jaw, she pushed up her left leg, raising her injured right, and then tried delicately to support herself.

A sharp, shrill cry of agony sent birds flying from the nearby trees, and beasts scurrying from the area. There was no way to straighten her leg, let alone to stand or walk with it.

She looked to the heavens and called upon the spirit of the north wind to numb her leg, to harden it, to bless her with the endurance to return to the snowy heights. And while the winds blew off the mountain, her calls were not answered by the one she sought.

A different sort of sounds came to her ears, sounds she had heard in the distance, but never known. One of the valley people 

Isa turned her head to the voice, and saw the maiden with the fiery hair. She raised her hands, balancing on only her left foot, and crossed her chest. She lived a free life, and had never had cause to learn the sounds of the valley folk. She spoke the tongue of the winds, begging for aid as the strange maiden approached her.

The maiden pointed to the ground, and the spread her arms wide. Isa stared, only knowing that the maiden had yet to harm anybody she had seen. She whispered to the winds, a prayer of safety from the stranger.

The stranger nodded and came closer, helping Isa back to the ground. Isa felt warmth from the touch, almost uncomfortable to her chilled skin, but the warmth brought calm to her mind. She spoke to Isa, but the only thing Isa understood was that the stranger was naming herself: “Solfrid”

“Isa,” Isa said softly. The only word she had claim to, the name the winds gave her.

For some time, Solfrid examined Isa’s leg, tenderly stroking and prodding the pained limb, and Isa made her own discomfort clear, unable to stay still or silent -- though she did not cry out again. The warmth of Solfrid’s hand was strange, and Isa wanted it even as it caused her to squirm and wince.

“So…” Solfrid began to speak, but Isa found that she could understand the maiden now. The winds must have heard her prayer for help. “You need help. You are too cold, Isa. The healers shall see to your leg.”

Isa looked up, uncertain. “Isa is not valley person,” she said softly. “Isa only knows valley from above, from mountain.” She raised her hand to show. “Mountain is free.”

Solfrid looked up and saw the mountain, the cliff from which Isa had fallen. “The mountain sends us snow,” she said softly. “If the mountain sends you to me, I protect you.” Solfrid placed a warm hand on Isa’s shoulder. “I will carry you.”

It took several moments as Solfrid moved to lift Isa upon her back, for Isa had never been carried by another, and knew nothing of how to hold the maiden, and the warmth of Solfrid’s body brought her a strange sense of both comfort and discomfort. “Come now,” Solfrid said, when finally Isa was mounted upon her back, and she held Isa’s legs to each side. “To my village.”
