## After School (Kadelya)

Three classes after lunch, and Kadelya was ready to meet up with Terra. All she had left was to meet her girlfriend, head to the atrium, and then see Sophie off before she met with the mousegirl. Sophie was a few rooms down the hall, so Kadelya headed to the nearest drinking fountain to wait for her.

While she did, she sent a text to her mom, letting her know why she would be home late this afternoon, and then another to her uncle Kharm. No need to have them worrying. A lock of her light red hair fell across her muzzle as she swiped the second message in.

She was vaguely aware as Sophie stepped up to the fountain and started filling her water bottle from it. “My, my, such a thirsty girl you are, love.”

Sophie giggled at that, but finished filling her bottle. “Well you’re all the sugar I need, kitty,” she said, screwing the top onto her bottle. After slipping the bottle into her satchel, she intertwined her left arm with Kadelya’s right, and gave her a quick kiss on the cheek. “So you’re really going to meet up with her?”

Kadelya reached her left hand up and smoothed the loose lock back where it belonged, her right hand gripping Sophie’s. They started to walk, and Kadelya nodded. “I know I’m not a therapist, I’m not like Miss Sarah or Doctor Miller. But I want to try to at least let her see that somebody here cares enough to see her do better.”

Sophie let out a low sigh, and Kadelya knew she was upset. Not angry, but upset. “You’re my light, Sophie. If it weren’t for how much you’ve convinced me that we can work to make everyone happy, I wouldn’t try with her.” She shouldered open the fire door so Sophie wouldn’t need to, and they emerged into the central area of the building, nearing the atrium. “Could you do a huge favor for me, love?”

“Your bag?” Sophie giggled and squeezed Kadelya’s hand. “I love you looking for the good in everybody. I want to see the good in her that you do, but I’ve been here six years, we’ve been dating for four, and nobody ever gets close to Terra, love.”

“Well, it’s not too late to try again,” Kadelya said, leaning over and licking the fox-girl’s cheek. “You know where I am. I texted mom and uncle Kharm. If I’m not home before dark, you all know what to do.”

Sophie blushed and nodded. She let go of Kadelya’s hand, then waited patiently for Kadelya to get her own water bottle out and pass her satchel over. “Love you, kitty.”

“Love you too, foxy,” Kadelya said, all but skipping off towards Terra. As she got close, Terra reached her hand out for a handshake. Kadelya stopped and reached her hand out too.

And then her head was swimming. She felt confused, then tired and calm and focused. “Sleep. Quiet. Obey. Follow me, Shadow. Follow me to the lecture hall, silent as can be.” She knew she was in a trance. Hypnotized in front of dozens of other students who were probably too focused on going home or to club rooms to notice that she was suggestible.

The only student who noticed was the one who put her in this state. The one who was still hugging her, holding her up. Gently, she was pushed into a standing posture again. “Right. Third floor, north wing,” Terra said, letting go of Kadelya. “Nod if you understand.”

Kadelya nodded slowly, and followed Terra. Terra had some sort of hypnotic patter going, something Kadelya could focus on, but not quite remember what she said. Keeping her relaxed after the surprising rapid induction, but not quite trying to deepen the trance yet. Kadelya was curious where this would go.
