## Hypnotic Lecture (Terra)

The third floor north lecture hall was Terra’s favorite. Unlike the others, it had only one door, and no windows. The lock on the door had been broken for a few months, but the room was used so rarely that nobody had gotten around to fixing it. And since it swung inward, she could just move the lecturer’s desk up against the door to lock it.

She had been careful to stay near Kadelya the whole way here. Speaking softly and keeping the rudimentary trance state focused. Now, she was ready to work. “Now, you’re going to listen to everything I say, you’re going to help me prepare my speech, do you understand?”

Kadelya nodded slowly, and Terra felt a rush of excitement.

“You are so attentive, taking notes on everything I say, that it’s easy to remember everything and save your questions for the end of my speech. Until I finish, you only speak when I ask you a question. In fact, you only speak, if I tell you to answer, do you understand?”

Another slow nod from the catgirl.

“Since I’m the valedictorian, I’m standing for my speech, but as my audience, you should make yourself comfortable, sitting in any chair you like, or on the floor.” Terra nervously bit her lower lip. She’d never done this offline before, and even online she always used text chat. “When you’re comfortable, you… you start purring. So I know how comfortable you are.”

She wasn’t entirely sure if Kadelya could purr, but she was a cat, right? Why wouldn’t she purr if she was comfortable. Kadelya looked groggily around the room, her tail swishing behind her as she looked over the seating. After about half a minute, she squatted down and sat herself on the floor, her legs tight together, bent fully at the knees, slightly to one side, with her long feline feet stretching out straight from the ankles.

“Good girl,” Terra said licking her lips softly. “You’re here to listen to my speech with full attention, right? Answer.”

Kadelya looked up at her and nodded slowly. “Yes, I want to help you, Miss Kelly.”

“Call me Terra,” Terra said. “When we’re alone like this, I’d like if you call me Terra.”

Kadelya paused and looked at her. “As you wish, Terra. I didn’t think--”

“You can leave the thinking to me,” Terra said suddenly. “I mean, you only need to think about my speech. Calm, focused on my voice and words for the speech.”

After Kadelya gave another nod, Terra started her speech. It was a guided meditation induction, and she watched Kadelya for her responses. Her eyes started to flutter, her breathing grew deep and calm, her purring even seemed to get louder. It took about ten minutes until Terra was comfortable that it had worked well.

“Kadelya, you are now in a deep trance, but I can take you deeper. It will make it easier to focus on me, easier to remember what I’m saying deep down, even though your surface mind might forget the details, your deep hypnotized mind remembers my words perfectly… You want to go deeper, you want to let your surface mind forget so you can remember better in your hypnotized mind.”
