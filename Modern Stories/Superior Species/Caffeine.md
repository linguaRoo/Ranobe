## Caffeine (Kadelya)

Kadelya’s mouth opened, but nothing came out. It was probably the kind of question she deserved for the one she had just asked. The most fucked up part of her life? She didn’t want to lie. She didn’t want to pass, even though it would only cost her a bit of her outfit. But she wasn’t sure she _wanted_ to actually admit this.

“The year you transferred in,” she said, taking a deep breath. “I found out the way my parents play in their bedroom. And that they do some really. Really, really. Like super duper fucked up edgeplay. Not just the stuff everybody jokes about the commune being a cult… but I accidentally caught them doing a pred-prey scene with mom… I know it was just a scene, but mom was threatening to put him in the pot and serve him to us, the whole commune, for dinner if he didn’t, y’know, submit.”

She let out a deep sigh and closed her eyes. The growling in her mom’s voice, the smell of blood and sweat, the fear she’d felt back then that it was a real threat. It all came back to her. The way she had ducked out of the doorway, and listened to her daddy scream, covering her mouth fearing that mommy would put her in the pot too if she was noticed.

Of course, mommy _had_ noticed. The next word her mom had said was, “Towertop.” And that meant playtime was over. She had come out to comfort Kadelya, to reassure her. But the young kitten was still scarred by it. And by other scenes she sometimes wandered into, after that point, after getting home from school earlier than mommy and daddy had expected. 

“When daddy’s on top, he and mom definitely do some cult-like play with him as a god. When mommy’s on top, they do predatory hunt play. And… they do it less now. But they both really enjoy rough sex, and they, they don’t always go to a sound insulated room for it. They said they always tried to do it only when none of us kids were home. Or after we were all asleep. But obviously, they didn’t always wrap up before we got home.”

She squeezed her eyes tighter. “We’ve had a _lot_ of talks about sex, okay? I knew about bad touches before I started school, because daddy wanted me to have agency. He wanted me to know he’d always believe me if someone touched me in a bad way. C-c-consent, it’s like s-s-super important to my family. And and n-n-n-now just ans-answering this… are you h-x-hhhappy?”

She looked over to Terra. The mouse’s jaw was hanging open. Probably not the answer she’d expected, Kadelya figured. “My turn, right? You made your point, let’s keep it light for now… what’s your favorite…”

“Hold up,” Terra said, sticking out her free hand. “Caffeine. Right? We can’t just keep playing around after you dump that shit on me!”

Kadelya stopped and looked at Terra. This was a surprise. Terra looked worried. Concerned. “O-o-okay,” she said slowly. “S-sorry. Sorry m-m-mistress,” she said, shifting to actually face Terra. “You probably thought I’d talk about some f-f-fucked up ff-ffantasy, right? Like getting off on noncon or lolis?”

“I, I didn’t know what I was expecting, but those are closer to it for sure,” Terra said not quite sure how to process what she had just learned. “So your parents just like, do this shit in the open at home?”

“Th-they were in th-their bedroom,” Kadelya said. “Miss Kelly… our cook, sh-she picked us up from sch-school. I wanted to show them my marks on a project. I think it was book report, but that’s n-n-not the…”

“Okay, but any normal kid who saw that,” Terra paused and Kadelya should see her thinking. “Therapy, right? Did you ever get therapy for it, or have you just been suppressing it all these years?”

“Oh, I g-got therapy,” Kadelya said. “And I, I g-get how fucked up things between loving partners are sometimes fine. With… c-consent. With… s-say-safeties like safe, safe words. Signals. And so I generally don’t think too much about the sp-spiff-spiffic scenes I’ve stumbled into ara-around m-my home. Because I know,” she raised her paw, and pointed up to the top of her head. “I n-know, they consent. And I can’t help the t-trauma, but I… I get help to p-process it. Because I know it’s just play. N-ne-negotiated. C-consensual.”

“You’re stuttering,” Terra said, sliding onto the futon mattress. “Which… is kind of cute. Maybe cuter if you had a lisp, but the point is this is real fucking trauma. Are you sure you’re okay?”

Kadelya took another deep breath in. “You made your p-point,” she said. “I sh-shouldn’t have a-as-asked you why you m-moved here. We’re n-na-not close enough for that.”

“Not my question,” Terra said, hissing her words out. “I asked if you are sure you’re okay. Because it’s no fun to break you if you’re already broken, princess.”

“I… I don’t know,” Kadelya said, admitting it to herself. “I prolly sh-shoulda just t-tay-taken my shirt off in-instead.”

It hurt to admit that. That it did still hurt her that much. But she still felt like she could trust Terra, even though the mouse had literally just admitted she wanted to _break_ her. But Terra wasn’t a therapist. She couldn’t fix what two therapists over the past ten years had only helped her bury the trauma of by working through parts of the symptoms by not thinking about it.

But maybe… maybe there was a part of her that was curious. That wanted to explore the edges of what could be okay. And maybe, she told herself, experiencing that would help her understand it even better. Maybe well enough to move past the trauma.

“Look, it’s not fair to you,” Terra said, her voice a bit grumbly. “You tell me something more fucked up than I could imagine about yourself. So look, I won’t tell ya the whole story, spill my heart. But the short version is my mom got sick. She and dad couldn’t look after me, so I got sent out to my uncle here. And when she got better… I decided to stay here.”
