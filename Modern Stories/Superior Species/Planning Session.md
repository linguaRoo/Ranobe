## Planning Session (Terra)

At twelve, she’d still just been a prototype of herself today. Dabbling into everything, memorizing all sorts of diagrams because she could only visualize things she’d seen before because of her fucking aphantasia.

Rocks, stones, machines, insects -- oh her memory was great if she had seen something before. But apparently her brain was hecking useless at _imagining_ anything she hadn’t seen precisely. So she devoured any source of knowledge she could, trying to fix herself back then.

She should have taken another advancement exam to skip ninth grade. Then she wouldn’t be in this whole valedictorian mess.

And it didn’t matter that she’d had the hots for Tessa six years ago. What mattered was that she was committed to enslaving and breaking the princess _now._

That meant that Terra was going to start lying her ass off, and those lies had to make a story that Kadelya believed easily enough to replace the truth. Put more comments in your code? Fuck off Mr. Gardner. Stop telling your teachers to fuck off? Fuck off Principal Reyes.

She didn’t need their rules, and as soon as she graduated, she was going to teach them all their proper place. Shadow was her final experiment, and Brown would be her masterpiece before she started taking over everybody who had tried to make her obey pointless arbitrary rules.

And to make that happen, she had three things left to test.

She hadn’t figured out how to speed them up any more than that. And they wouldn’t really do anything obvious until she came close with the remote.

Speaking accurately, she had, and had literally no recollection of the hour she’d been out, except that nothing had happened except for her blacking out while her breathing slowed and her body didn’t move until the machine powered itself down. She’d improved the insulation on it since that test, but she was pretty sure her brain would fight her own suggestions as much as it did anybody else’s rules.

Third: Proving that she could break someone as much as the porn manga and hentai and mc stories she spent all her time building ideas from. Figuring out how to shatter someone so much their mind was like putty to her voice all the time, until they’d never know the difference between real life and whatever she told them.

The last was what she wanted from Kadelya. But she’d happily test the other two on her in the process. Fuck, she needed to work out this plan. 

So, she looked over her plot file. From what Kadelya had said on their call, she might have had a crush on Terra back then, so making sure she still had a crush on her was an easy start. She’d invited Terra to a lot of things over the years, but it always seemed like polite inclusion bullshit, not really like she cared. After all, everybody got invited to those beach parties and bonfires and birthdays. 

Would it be better to try to just build up an enslavement, or should she try messing with Kadelya’s memories? If there was a crush, she could probably take advantage of that. Convince her that she’d been enslaved after the exam, before she went to Japan? Convince her she _hadn’t_ gone to Japan at all? No… she wanted to know more about that trip to Japan. Maybe she could convince Kadelya she’d been on the trip after hearing some stories about it. Writing, after all, was something Terra could do.

Brown, no, “Sophie.” She had to remember that name, even if she wasn’t going to let on that she’d already fucked with Kadelya’s memory to get information like that. It was testing, though, and as far as she knew, it had worked. Sophie had transferred into seventh grade at exactly the time Kadelya had come back from six fucking months in Japan. And the two were clearly already flirting with each other from day one. While the accent was less pronounced, everybody thought her British accent was sooo adorable. Of course, Terra didn’t mind the accent. It was the instant popularity that ticked her off. Teachers telling her she should be more like Brown and Shadow, make some friends.

She growled and looked away from the screen. “I don’t want friends, I don’t need friends,” she muttered. “What good would friends do me when I can make slaves?” She shook her head and closed her eyes tightly. Little glowing, and fading, blobs drifted across her eyelids, but that was just because her monitor was so hecking bright. She could _remember_ anything she saw. But she couldn’t visualize. Why was her brain broken like this?

She took a moment to breathe deep, to center and focus her thoughts where she wanted them, not on some stupid flashback origin story or future plot for Sophie Brown. She wanted to plan her conquest of Kadelya Shadow’s mind to the point she could change whatever else she wanted later. “Right,” she muttered, looking back to her monitor. She reviewed what she had already typed in, making a few small notes on a timeline -- a timeline that was partially relying on what she could find out on Saturday night.
