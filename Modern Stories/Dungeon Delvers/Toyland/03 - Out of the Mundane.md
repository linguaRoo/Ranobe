Monty had planted a few small battery-powered lamps, facing further into the campus. One pointed to each of the three buildings we wanted to explore -- the warehouse, the offices, and the factory. Prtty close to the three meter mark we’d established, too. All of our phones had access to the app to trigger them.

“We’re not splitting up here,” I said after assessing everybody’s readiness. “I know you want to try and find the warehouse basement, Tifa, but we’re a team, and we vote. Right?”

The four more experienced members of the team all signaled their understanding vocally, while Monty just nodded. I pulled out three red plastic cups, and then a permanent marker. I wrote on each cup, then set them down. Pulling out a poker chip, I dropped it into the cup I had marked for the office. “I vote for the office first. It looks furthest away.” I gestured off to the west, my right hand pointing towards it. “By going there first, we have the best chances of finding documents of what happened here. Not that I’m counting on docs. Go ahead and place your votes.”
