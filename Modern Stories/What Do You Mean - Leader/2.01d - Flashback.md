_“Aphrodite and Dionysus are greek gods, but maybe they’re just people, Dean. People who the Greeks mistook for gods, maybe? This is supposed to be a competition, and if it’s a competition between the gods, I’m sure we’ll see more. Athena and Ares probably, they were competitive. Artemis, Apollo, Hera… Zeus himself, even.”_

_Dean interrupted her. “What about Hades or Persephone?”_

_“Sure, the whole point is we don’t know who else might be involved.” She took a breath and slipped her diary into her pocket. “They want us to compete for them. I don’t think it will be violent, I mean Amber hasn’t trained us to expect fighting, has she?”_

_“No, she wanted us to be able to fit in. At least, she wanted me to,” Dean admitted._

_He probably would fit in, she noted. Sarah had trouble with the language lessons, she had figured they would be doing everything with translation magic or something like that, and she was more obsessed with learning German, which didn’t have much in common with the Adin language._
