Sarah had never associated that with being a psychic power or magic or in any way not natural. She’d always believed it was just a form of self-hypnosis that helped her commit to actions which really drove the changes she’d experienced in her appearance throughout puberty.

“I see you’re remembering something formative,” Harvey said with a lazy smile on his face. “The face of a girl lost in thought and recollection. I haven’t seen that face in quite a few years. Centuries even.”

Sarah turned to face him. “So is there a problem?”

“No, no,” he said, raising his hands as if to shield himself. “Just be careful with that self-image of yours. You are in stunning health, miss Mackenzie, and I hope you do very well in this competition.”

Sarah was slightly surprised by that. “But you have your own candidates,” she said hesitantly. “Why would you want me to do well?”

Harvey just shrugged. “I suppose I just recognize potential when I see it. Besides, you doing well doesn’t have to mean mine don’t make the cut.” He gave her a small box and then headed to the door. “Once you get your slate, you can check your medical profile for yourself, see what it says about your magic and psychic potentials. Probably easier to look all the information up for yourself.”

Sarah stood up. “Yes, I think I would prefer to read it for myself,” she said as she walked to the door. “Thank you, doctor.”
