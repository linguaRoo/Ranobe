# 01: Megan’s Plight

## I - Megan

Megan huffed as she walked into her room. It had seemed like such a sure thing, such a simple thing. Edmund was part of the computer club! He admired her programming skills and loved the game she’d been working on, even if it was still kind of a piece of shit. She slipped her backpack off onto her bed with a sturdy THUD and then walked over to the window.

The sky was red with the late summer sunset. Wispy clouds were painted in pinks and lavenders by the backlighting, and the still-green leaves outside rustled in a steady breeze. In a few days, it wouldn’t even be summer anymore. And it was her senior year, too! 

She let out a deep sigh, then flopped out on her bed. Her purple-dyed hair got everywhere, covering her glasses and face. She tried several times to blow it away from her mouth at least, muttering and mumbling to herself. Maybe she should change her style? She loved her hair, but it was a hassle sometimes. But changing her hair wouldn’t fix her immediate problem, would it? After a few minutes, she brushed the smooth, long locks to the sides of her face, then pulled her glasses off, closing her eyes and raising up her right hand to write in the air above her.

> Topic: What is wrong with boys?
> 
> Case: Edmund. Computer club, OS development, command line whiz. Senior. Brown hair, tight curls. Cute face. School honor society. Good sense of humour. Skinny, tall. Tall was important, because Megan was too. They both enjoyed electronica, dubstep, and dance mixes. She liked nightcore, he preferred nerdcore.

Honestly, she was relieved that he had asked her out at first. Everything she could think of suggested compatibility, so she’d agreed to today’s movie date. A week ago, she’d thought she finally had a chance to _get_ what a high school romance really was, but after this afternoon, she wondered if she was made for romance at all. Edmund had treated her on their date, even let her pick the film. A scary one, with the kind of monsters that don’t get shown off without a ton of shadows until late in the second act.

At first, he’d been a gentleman. Sure, she drove, because she’s a modern young woman and her dad let her drive the family sedan to school. But he paid for the tickets. He paid for the popcorn. He even let her choose the movie, which she figured he probably wouldn’t have chosen himself. She kind of figured he might have been more scared at some of it than she’d been, but around the end of the film, he had tried to just kiss her out of the darkness in the theater.

And then? When she had the apparent audacity to say that a first date was too early for that, he had dropped the nice guy act. He said how he’d paid for everything, so he was supposed to get a kiss, and maybe more, and she hated how that seemed to be the goal of every boy she had tried to date for the past three years. Three years, since entering high school, every boy that had asked her on a date had that one goal in mind. A few lasted two dates before they said it. There was only one who hadn’t, but he’d told her that he thought they were better as friends…

And they were good as friends. Sure, he was a bit of an airheaded jock, playing on the soccer team and the basketball team, he didn’t really get the computer stuff she loved, but he was genuinely friendly, and they had similar tastes in music and movies and… If only Josh wasn’t so stupidly honest about not feeling anything romantic for her!

She noticed that she had sat up at some point, and her backpack had been emptied out now. She wasn’t in bed anymore, and had let her habits take over for a bit while she though. All of her books were stacked up by her computer, notebooks on the left, textbooks on the right. Her penguin-themed pencil case was open, and she had even started to sharpen the pencils she’d used today. All on auto-pilot. She glanced to the window again. The sky was even deeper red now.

She didn’t need to start her homework yet, she told herself. She was top of the school rankings. Hell, she was top of the district rankings. Kevin from the tech school was the only person who even came close to her…

Case: Kevin Prince. He was cute. Straight black hair. He was half-Asian something, but it was mostly visible in that hair and his eyes. But he was also her rival, and maybe, she told herself, not having a proper love life is why she was academically murdering even the biggest “prodigy genius” in the state right now. Besides, she only knew Kevin by reputation, so how was he supposed to ask her out?

She needed to talk to someone about this.

She had two people she could trust.

## II - Angela

Angela was eating pocky and reading a light novel, in Japanese, when her phone rang. “Hey there Meg,” she said with a lazy cadence to her words. She knew Megan didn’t care for the nickname, but she also knew that Megan was too nice to complain about it. And she only used it on the phone anyway. And phone-like media.

“Hey Ang,” came the response. Megan sounded preoccupied, but she usually sounded that way. Always up in her head, head in the sky, sky getting thin. Which was to say, she was a classic anxious Type A. “It was a wreck.”

Angela sighed and slid her bookmark into the book. “Yeah, you always make these dates a wreck,” she explained. “You overthink, you overplan…”

“Just listen a minute!” Megan interrupted her as she often did, but Angela just felt herself smile. “So, he wanted to move too fast again. This had nothing to do with me overplanning or overthinking, he tried to kiss me while a monster was killing the survivalist.”

“Gross, horror movies are for cuddling, not kissing,” Angela interjected. “He should’ve waited until… Sorry.” She pulled out another pocky stick and stuck the sweet end into her mouth, thinking. Megan was quiet too.

“So…”

Angela took the moment to shift her posture, sitting cross-legged on the bed. Her mousey-brown hair spilled down to her shoulders, and she looked around the room.

“Ange, you got anything?”

Angela shrugged. She did have one thought, but Megan probably wouldn’t like it. “What about trying girls?”

The line was silent for almost a whole minute. “What about trying girls? I mean, I can appreciate our beauty, but I really don’t think I’m into _that._” There was a stiffness, an awkwardness as she spoke.

Angela wasn’t entirely sure what to make of it. She turned to her window. The sky was getting dark, indigo already through her eastern view. “Well, then maybe just accept that you’re a femcel,” Angela said, surprisingly calmly, even to herself. “But… I know a girl who wouldn’t mind going on a date with you. Just one, just to try it out. No kissing. No hand holding. Definitely nothing nasty. And… no pressure. Think about it.”

“Like you have any friends I don’t know,” Megan said, laughing awkwardly. “Besides, Skye is your brother’s age, they’re both freshmen. Candace graduated last year and is in Paris for some international university now. What lesbian friend do you have that you’re hiding from me?”

Angela shrugged, fully aware that Megan couldn’t see the gesture. “I have neighbors, Meg. And I know that one of the girls who lives around here has a crush on you, and she’s a senior too.”

“So why doesn’t she ask me out?”

Angela paused. This was such a stupid idea, not being honest with Megan about her intentions. She rolled her eyes and let out a sigh. “Fine, you know what, forget it. You’re probably right, and it wouldn’t go anywhere.” She bit her lower lip slightly at that. it was a gamble, Megan might choose to let it go now.

“Fine, I’ll think about it,” Megan said after a tense ten seconds or so. “So, er, have you gotten your college applications in yet?”

## III - Josh

Josh let himself crash onto the futon in the basement theater his dad had set up. “That was a good run,” he said out loud to nobody in particular. He was alone, after all. But he liked to talk, and he liked to do it out loud. Senior year, varsity teams were kicking his ass. But he still loved the game, and he was still living for the action.

DING.

His phone. A text message from George. George… George. “Dude, u left ur shit in lockers.” George, his rival. The basketball player. Rival felt right to Josh, even though they weren’t, like, enemies or anything. They just both wanted to be captain of the basketball team. And the same girl, Angela Sparks.

Josh’s eyes narrowed. He never left anything in the locker room after practice. What the hell was George on about? It didn’t matter, the school was closed and locked up now. The sun was almost down, he’d just have to get it tomorrow. He typed quickly: “Fk it cn wt.”

The response was a bit delayed. “dw, droppin off”

Josh’s jaw went slack. He sent another response. “fy” So it had to be serious. Not just about his gym shorts and jock. He supposed he got on alright with George, couldn’t think of anything he’d done to piss him off. Guess he just had to wait and find out what the blond boy wanted.

He didn’t have to wait long. When he let George into the house, he was handed a clear trash bag with shorts, tanks, and his jock. He squinted a bit. Was it his jock? “Fuck, sorry I spaced on these,” he said, humble enough to admit he’d made a mistake. “Thanks. Want a beer? Ale?”

George nodded. “Dude, are you stoned right now?” His blue eyes were squinting at Josh. “No, sorry, I don’t mean that. Come on, dude. What were you up to when I texted?”

Josh hefted the bag and shrugged. “Come on in. We should watch a movie. I’ll throw your shit in the wash too, bro.” He knew his manners, at least. “And you know I mean pop. We got that Sprechers shiz.”

George visibly relaxed at that. “Oh, fuck, sorry dude. I just been pushing myself, y’know? Got distracted.”

Josh laughed. “Don’t worry. Gimme your sweats, I’ll get laundry and munchies.”

It didn’t take long to start the laundry. People always assumed Josh was slow or dense, but he was just wired different. He liked to clean things. He liked to feed his friends, and rivals. He liked to just zone out with a TV on in the same room.

“Yeah, so I don’t think we’re rivals no more,” he said as he brought the nachos into the theater. “We can just be bros. I’m not that into her.”

George rolled his eyes. “We’re not rivals,” he said firmly. “We never were. But I guess if it makes you feel better, then sure, whatever we were rivals for, I guess I accept.”

“Bro, you know what I mean,” Josh said, popping the tops off of two bottles of ginger ale. “Alright, I’m not that into Angela. You can have her, I won’t fightcha.”

George took a chipful of meat and hot peppers, eating it slowly. As he did, a smile came across his face, and Josh wasn’t sure if it was the food or surrender. He’d never been good at guessing how people felt. “Dude, these are lit! Come on, pick a movie.”

## IV - Megan

Megan wasn’t into girls, but she supposed she did need some more friends. Friends who were girls, not just the boys who made up most of the computer club. Okay, all of the computer club except for her. Of course, she was still dubious about Angela happening to know a girl who already wanted to date her. It was obvious that Angela was talking about herself.

Megan thought about it all evening. Dinner came and went, some casserole using leftover potatoes and chicken and gravy. During the meal, she was thinking about calling Angela’s bluff. Afterwards, with her stomach contented, she found herself inclined to at least consider it. She worked through her assignments, starting with the easy calculus assignments, then working on her first essay, and so on until she finally had thirty minutes left for herself.

To game, or to make a game. She took a deep breath as  
she looked at her computer screen. Tonight she would game. And maybe, just maybe, she would take Angela up on this game she was suggesting they play together. As long as Angela could be a real gentlewoman about this whole lesbian act, not forcing Megan into anything, it might be worth playing pretend again. For old times’ sake, if nothing else.
