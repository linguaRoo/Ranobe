# 02: Boy George

## I - George

George stretched out and sighed. Josh was as thick as ever, but it was nice of him to stop chasing Angela. And he was such a nice guy at heart, he’d made those great nachos and put on the new superhero movie, and even shared some pops.

They weren’t rivals. They were now, and had long been, friends. They’d even played in Angela’s old tabletop campaigns. Of course, Josh was a bit of an airhead -- he never took notes, and mostly just wanted to play a heroic knight that was, well, himself in fantasy, or anime, or sci-fi every single campaign.

George smiled as he thought about the last campaign, but ever since sophomore year, Ange and Megan had been too busy with college prep for either of them to run the games. Of course, joining sports had cut into his own time for the games too, but he still missed the regular get-togethers, usually at Megan’s place since it was the most accommodating to the group.

And of course, he’d bombed his own plan for today too. Sometimes, that dumb face and dumber brain Josh had made him forget what he really wanted to do with him. Besides, he had no idea if Josh would be okay with it. Not like in an abstract sense, just in the immediate, present, George sense of things.

George wasn’t sure if he really wanted it either, or if he was just lonely. Josh was popular. A lot of girls wanted to date him, and the only reason he didn’t date them was that he’d has his eyes set on Angela. If he didn’t now, maybe he’d rather be with a girl.

George pulled his pillow over his face and grumbled into it. It wasn’t a yell or a shout, but it was a mood. He needed to find his voice when it came to Josh, to tell him what he wanted, no, what he needed to say. To ask him. He knew in his head what he wanted to say, and what he needed to communicate.

“Look, Ange is too busy for us. We’ve been friends for a long time, and I…” Even in his imagination he paused here. He was curious, but not as confident as the himbo he had a crush on. “I don’t know if you’d be interested, but maybe we could go on a date sometime.”

He wasn’t thinking straight, and he grimaced as the pun tried to roll over in his head like a puppy, but he did know Josh was cool with gay people. Two of the guys on the soccer team were dating each other, and Josh had always supported them. So why was George so conflicted about actually telling him?

It was just the same as it had been last night. And the night before. And the night before. Since the start of the school year, about two weeks ago. He wasn’t really interested in chasing Angela anymore, and he wanted to try being with Josh. If only he could get the stupid words out his mouth.

Finally, he pulled the pillow from his face. This was not going to be easy, but somehow, he had to talk to Josh about his curiosity. He felt the moisture in his eyes spilling over. This was his problem to settle, and somehow, he would.

## II - Josh

Josh knew what other people thought about him. That he was dumb, or stupid, or whatever. But he wasn’t. He knew a lot of things besides soccer and other sports. He just didn’t care about most of those other things.

But he did care about George. George Rogers had been his first crush, back when they were both still in second grade. The first time he knew he liked boys. But it wasn’t right to like boys, not that way. He didn’t even know where he learned that, but he couldn’t stop himself from finding the words in the corners of his mind every time he tried to think about George as anything except a friend or a rival.

“Fuck, bro,” he muttered to himself, stepping into the shower. The hot water brought some focus. “Tony and Keith are fine. They ain’t creeps or nothing, just two guys who love each other. Or whatever they got goin’ on. Why can’t I just, try it?”

He knew George was cuter than him, all the girls who were into cute andro-boys said it. The ones who liked muscleheads were more into himself, Josh thought. And of course he knew Ange was into both of them, personally, but she never had time to date anymore. She’d turned him down for the Homecoming dance coming up next month, even though she didn’t seem to have any other plans… that was why he’d given up on chasing her for now. Maybe someday, when her life smoothed out.

He grumbled as the familiar stiffness appeared when he thought about Angela. He didn’t want to deal with this again, he wanted to get over her. George had been the one who brought his smelly shit over. George had stayed for a movie like a real bro. George had those piercing blue eyes, and the really short buzz cut right now that was one of the only things making him look like the man he was.

The stiffness stayed, and Josh felt the warmth inside of himself spreading, only to find it beat down a moment later. You’re not gay, he heard himself shouting. You could probably share Ange with him if they both wanted it, but you’re not a faggot.

Wasn’t he? The stiffness didn’t change, but the heat inside of him started to feel overwhelming. It wasn’t the heat that made him stiff, it was turning into the heat that made him aggressive. He clenched his jaw and growled wordlessly in the shower. “You’re not a fairy-fucking homo,” he heard the voice in his head. “You’re a red-blooded straight man, and you want to fuck pussy.”

He tried to ignore it. But the voice kept yelling in his head. Being a fag was fine for other people, but Josh had a legacy to protect, a lineage to carry on, and the voice inside of him kept saying he’d never make David proud if he was gay, he’d never have a son of his, no, two sons and a daughter, if he was a fag.

But he knew that David, his step-dad, had never talked to him like this. He knew better, but he couldn’t stop the voice. He didn’t know where he learned these hateful things. At the gym? In school? From soccer coaches? From YouTube?

He didn’t know, and it was tearing him apart. “Shut up,” he said, collapsing to his knees in the shower. “I don’t like you talking to me. Just shut up. Shut up. Shut up!”

A few moments passed as he tried to silence the hateful voice in his head. He did the breathing exercises his mom had taught him. He stared at the floor, shower water dripping down his face like tears. Maybe hiding tears. He didn’t know right now what he was feeling anymore.

He calmed himself enough to finish showering. He toweled off and looked in the mirror, his dark red hair sticking out and his brown eyes looking a bit pink around the edges. He hated the voice. He didn’t know where it had come from, and he was afraid if anybody ever knew about it, he’d scare them off.

He stuffed the casual clothes he’d been wearing since practice into the hamper and rushed to his bedroom, then put on the white noise player in his room. Camping in a forest. He breathed easier as he dropped his towel and climbed into bed.

As he closed his eyes, he thought about his rival. No, not his rival…

His crush.

## III - Angela

It was a long night. Another late night for Angela Sparks, reading the books she kept hidden in her closet, to keep her little brother from finding them. To keep her mom from finding them. Sure, she was an adult, and was legally allowed to own them, but mom was strict about even normal entertainment materials. These imports… well, mom might not know what they say, but the images were detailed enough.

Tonight’s book was some original story. Two boys caught and blackmailed at school into being sissies so their parents and the school wouldn’t find out. For an amateur author, it was pretty good. Of course, when she was a teacher, she’d never let something like this happen to her students… but she was more like the fembois here than the blackmailer now, and sometimes, the idea of being forced turned her on. This dark fantasy stuff was a way to let her inner demons have their fun without trying to do bas shit to others.

But she had sort of lied to Megan on the phone earlier, hadn’t she? Of course she could argue that she knew herself, but… she had suggested that she wanted to set someone else up with Meg. Was that too dishonest of her? She sighed and closed the book. 

Ethics. The question of whether it was permissible to lie… to setup a date. Obviously, it would be wrong to lie any further. She couldn’t hide that Megan was dating her if they had a date, after all. And it was more like diplomatic language than actual dishonesty. Selective revelation of the truth… which didn’t entirely reassure her.

Maybe she should be honest as soon as Meg took her up on the offer. If Meg took her up on it, that was still in the air, she reminded herself. But she understood Meg’s concern. Boys were too obsessed with sex.

Says the girl reading amateur smut, she reminded herself, and grimaced a bit. But she read this so she could keep her libido more controlled outside of her bedroom, so that meant she wouldn’t be trying to make Megan take advantage of her publicly, or so any of the messed up things she enjoyed reading about until she knew her partner was in it for her and for herself.

She reached for her pocky, and found the foil empty. No more snacks tonight. She opened the book again and her head was swimming. Probably not good to keep reading now. Yes, she was a perv, but she had principles. And letting Gordon find her sex stuff would be a huge mistake. She covered the books and toys with one of the tamest print blankets she could have chosen, a Hello Kitty design, and then closed the closet. It was bedtime. But first…
