# Digimon Adventure Side Trek

## 1-2. Why So Boaring?

### I - Ben

“I’m so sorry, it just a case of mistaken identity!” Ben shouted as he ran through the foret, the rock monster that called itself Geomon close to his side as a giant burning boar chased them both.

“Trespassers!” The voice was deep, rumbling, and crackling, almost like a blazing bonfire.

Geomon yelled just to be heard. “What did you do, Ben? Boarmon is normally a peaceful guardian!”

“I didn’t do nothing,” Ben responded with heavy breath. “I just, maybe, thought it was Kim.”

Another roar of “Trespassers!” echoed behind them.

“Quick, turn left! That tree is a blind spot!”

Ben took a deep breath and turned to the left moving around the tree indicated. He pulled himself up close to the trunk and… fell backwards into the tree. He could hardly hear anything from outside, and it looked like he had just fallen into a gleaming metal room. After a few seconds, Geomon rolled in as well.

“I’m sorry I scared you,” the rock said as it settled on the floor of the room. The room was cylindrical, and unlike the slightly blocky appearance of the outside of the tree, the interior was perfectly smooth. “But I’m here to protect you, Ben. I’m your Digimon.”

“I don’t really know what that means,” Ben said, looking sideways at the rock creature. “And I don’t know how you can move or talk. This doesn’t make any sense, unless I’m having some kind of dream.”

The room was silent for a moment.

“So… how will we know when it’s safe to leave?”

“Well, just wait until it’s quiet,” Geomon said. He had a laid back drawl in his voice, not like the cowboys around Bozeman, but like someone from the Caribbean, maybe? Ben couldn’t quite place it. “Anyway, I feel like we got off to a bad start. I’m your partner, Geomon.”

“Right, whatever that means,” Ben said, sitting down on the smooth and surprisingly comfortable floor. “I’m Ben, Ben Cohen. So, what exactly is a _Digimon_?”

“I’m not sure I get your meaning,” Geomon answered. “I’m a Digimon. All of the living things in this world are Digimon.”

“So like animals?”

“What’s an animal?”

Ben let out a deep sigh. “I guess you’re not an animal, though. You’re… like a lump of clay, or something.”

Geomon’s face moved up and down as if it were nodding, though its body didn’t really move. “I’m a lesser Digimon, but my data is mineral type.”

A sharp rapping sound came from outside of the tree.

“That doesn’t seem like Boarmon,” Geomon said.

Another voice came from outside. “Geomon! I can smell ya in there, it’s safe!”

“Koinumon!” Geomon’s eyes lit up. “Come on out, Ben. I trust Koinumon if he says its safe.”

### II - Kim

Kim shaded her eyes as she looked out over the jungle. She was on an island, with impossibly tall mountains scattered along it, and an especially tall peak in the center. There were different landscapes beyond the jungle, some dry and dusty, others looking even more humid, and even what seemed like a factory far to the left of the central mountain.

Kim was about to climb down when she noticed a glint a ways to the west. She reached into her backpack and grabbed her camp telescope, focusing on the spot she’d seen it. There was a boy over there, another human, in brown canvas shorts and a blue t-shirt, with wild brown hair and a pair of goggles. He was almost looking at her, but a bit off. And she could hear the buzzing of powerful insect wings.

Tracking his view, she spotted a giant red stag beetle. And by giant, she figured that it was probably the size of a semi—and it was swooping towards the boy. She decided there was no point in wasting any time or risking herself being spotted by the giant insect. Tucking her telescope into her hoodie pocket, she started to make her way down.

“Hey, Caprimon,” she said, nearing the bottom. “I think we’re on an island. And… do you know anything about a giant red beetle?”

She sat on the lowest branch and looked down into the area where she’d left Caprimon. She saw two penguin-like creatures with similar markings, one with coppery-red feathers, the other with a bright tropical violet. And they were cornering Caprimon against a tree.

“Hey! Leave my friend alone,” she shouted.

The penguins turned around and there seemed to be a mischievous glint in their eyes.

The red one spoke first. “Mucho mucho! What kinda Digimon is this here?”

“I dunno-pen,” said the other. “Seems a little stringy, though. Maybe it’s a Stringmon?”

“Stringmon? Mucho makes me think of beans… she kinda mucho lookin’ tasty to you?”

“I-I… I’m not food!” Kim protested. “I’m a human!”

“Huemon-pen?” The purple one looked confused. “Ah right, she matches you-pen! Look how red she is!”

“Mucho red! So mucho red, I couldn’t eat that,” the red one replied. “Hey, you know mucho jokes?”

Jokes? “We-well sure,” Kim said as she thought about it. “But come on, I can’t just tell you a joke without introductions. I’m Kim, a human being.”

The red one laughed hard. “A Huemon bean? Well that must be why I feel mucho hungry right now! Call me Muchomon! And this clown here…”

The violet one held up one of its wings as if to offer a handshake. “Penmon-pen! So ya really are a bean. Never heard of a Huemon, but ya seem right friendly-pen.”

“And what were you doing with Caprimon?”

“Mucho playing! We just wanted to share some of our mucho jokes,” Muchomon said cheerfully.

Caprimon hopped over to Kim and nodded. “I’m not sure I really understood them. But I guess they’re good.”

### III - Genki

Ben emerged from the tree following the strange clay-like rock. His denim jacket was tied around his waist now, long hiking pants tucked into long socks, and his hiking boots digging into the uncanny ground.

Genki looked him over and crossed his arms. “So you’re here too.”

Ben nodded. “Wait, did you just actually talk to me?”

“We’re stuck here together. Doesn’t mean I’m your friend,” Genki said.

“And you can speak English,” Ben observed.

“You’re speaking Japanese,” Genki retorted.

Koinumon bounced up on top of the rocky creature and the two Digimon whispered for a moment. “You’re both speaking the Digimon language,” Koinumon said bluntly. “So however it is, we can all understand each other, right? So let’s try to get along.”

Genki rolled his eyes. “Fine. So what should we do, Cohen?” He still wanted to keep some distance, so he used the surname.

“I think we should try to find some shelter,” Ben said. “And figure out where we are, and what these,” he nodded towards the Digimon, “really are.”

“I’ve already told you,” Geomon said, seeming to puff up as if he was breathing heavily. “I’m Geomon.”

“I’m Koinumon!”

Together they stated: “We’re Digimon. Digital Monsters.”

Then only Geomon continued. “And you’re in the Digital World, on File Island. We’ll be happy to help you find shelter, though, right Koinumon?”

“Yeah, the emergency trees are safe, but not very comfortable,” Koinumon said cheerfully. “And I bet you humans can build a shelter if we can’t find one.”

Genki nodded and started walking away from the tree. “Well then, let’s move.”

Just then, a sharp scream pierced the jungle. There were no words to it, just a girly scream of pure distress. Genki turned his feet and started running. “Koinumon, let’s check that out!”

He was barely aware of the deep sigh Ben let out as he sprinted through the foliage.

“Tanemon!”

A second scream helped lead the way, and Genki caught sight of a girl in a pink dress and a cowboy hat running with what looked like a pale green waddling sproutling.

“Mimi! He’s still coming!” This cry sounded more immature, no doubt coming from the smaller creature. It was all Genki could do to keep up with them until they burst into a clearing. From the other side of the clearing, a group of other children burst in and Genki skidded to a halt.

He knew those children. Well, most of them, at least. They all went to his real school, in Odaiba! Yagami and Takenouchi were calling out, “Mimi!” And the girl’s screams were turning to sobs as she kept running towards them, tripping and falling to her knees. There were expressions of shock on their faces, and then a crashing through the trees.

A giant red stag beetle appeared and Yagami shouted, “It’s Kuwagamon!”

The beetle swooped aggressively over the group and Genki swallowed. A group of kids from his own school. Should he introduce himself?

“Genki, what’s up?” Koinumon looked out at the other group. “Do you know those humans?”

Genki turned his head aside, not sure what to do. He’d missed the whole school year. “Do you know those Digimon?” he asked flatly.
