# Norma’s Night Out

> [!NOTE] **Refresh**  
> This is a story from the OzRoo [[Refresh Stories/Refresh]] series, based loosely on old fanfiction written in the 2000s. The stories may or may not closely resemble their originals, but they are intended to take 20+ years of writing experience to refresh these stories for a more mature audience.

“I’ve never been so tempted  
to bite my claws,”  
Kharm said as he approached Norma Lee,  
the head of the Osaka monster stadium.  
Offering a hand,  
he smiled.  
“You fought really well, Miss Norma.  
It’s been a privilege.”

“You cheated!”

The rebuke caught him off guard.  
Her stern glare locked on Kharm  
as she patted the Mootoro  
lying on the floor of the stadium.

“Excuse me? How did I…”

“I don’t have to give a tag to cheaters!”  
She huffed this time,  
her cheeks going plump  
as she walked around her monster.  
“The League policies say so.”

“I understand that,  
but how did I…”

“If you understand,  
get out!”  
She shoved her index fingers up against his shoulders.  
“Just turn around  
and leave!  
There’s no way Mootoro could lose  
to your measly little Ratty,”  
she said as if it was a law of nature.  
“So you cheated.  
Come back again tomorrow  
without your drugged up rodent!”

“Drugged up?  
You took out my strongest monsters  
with that Mootoro,”  
Kharm said,  
not wanting to deal with false accusations.  
“I won the fight,  
and while I may have been lucky,  
I have never cheated.”

“Then you won’t have any trouble  
rematching me  
tomorrow,”  
Norma said.  
This time,  
she seemed a little less demanding,  
as she looked Kharm over.  
“Or maybe  
you can do me a favor tonight.  
What do you say?”

Kharm swallowed.  
A “favor” would probably  
not be in the normal League guidebook.  
“I earned the tag,  
Miss Lee.  
If you want a favor,  
you can ask me  
after you hand it over.”

He could see her leg twitching  
and her nostrils flaring  
as she thought it over.  
He hoped he was coming off as confident  
and not creepy  
as he looked at her.

“Fine!”  
She turned around.  
“I didn’t bring any with me.  
If you’ll do my favor,  
I’ll have your tag at eight tonight.  
Take a bath  
and dress nice.”

---

It had to be the strangest way Kharm could imagine  
to be asked on a date.  
She had been blowing up his cell phone  
with text messages  
to make it clear that he was expected  
to be a gentleman  
and let her do whatever she wanted  
tonight.

He looked in the mirror  
and sighed.  
At least it wasn’t a full suit,  
but he hated collared shirts.  
And ties.  
Still,  
it was probably to be expected  
of someone going on a date  
with a Stadium Boss.  
He looked sharp enough.

“Kharm-san,”  
the voice startled him.  
“Other guests need the changing room too.  
Please leave  
in the next five minutes.”

Right,  
this lodge was a public accommodation for tamers.  
A clinic downstairs,  
common rooms upstairs  
with bathing and changing facilities.  
It wasn’t a lot,  
but he’d gotten used to the charm  
of the free lodgings  
in every town  
and city  
along the road so far.

“I understand,”  
he said taking stock of his outfit  
one last time.  
If only he had the time to get to a salon…

He picked up his bag  
and slung it over his shoulder.  
He still had about an hour  
before the start of the date.  
He should take in a bit of the city life.

---

Osaka was a lively city.  
A little bit noisy,  
but it was the biggest,  
most modern city  
in Kansai.  
An intersection of tradition  
and modernity,  
the new business towers  
and mansions  
sticking out in the heart of the region.

There were signs up for a performance  
at the HEP Five,  
some sort of upcoming Koppykat show  
running for a week.  
The posters featured three of the shapeshifting monsters  
in front of red curtains.  
Kharm thought it might be interesting to see,  
but on closer inspection,  
he would have to stay in town  
a day longer than he had planned  
to catch the first show.

Still,
he'd always been interested in theatre.
The last he'd heard from Stacey,
she was getting into it as well.
Maybe he could make up a day on the road,
put some hustle in,
just to enjoy a day for himself.

Maybe.

He resumed walking
along the outside of the building,
toward his destination.
A smaller,
local shopping centre.
He took in the atmosphere,
the scents of the city,
the restaurants he passed along the way,
the sounds of conversations,
and almost felt like
_this_
is where he belonged.

But in a few months,
he knew he'd be heading home.
Home to California,
to his twin brother,
to the relationship between Dean and his girlfriend,
to college,
to the house their parents had bought them.
Real adult life.
Real freedom.
Real responsbilities.

Unless he won the tournament.
Unless he could show that he
had the skill to be a real,
professional
monster tamer.
Then he could apply for an extended visa,
through his League pass,
and stay in Japan.
Travel to Kanto,
to Hokkaido,
to see more monsters,
to keep traveling until
_he_ felt ready to settle down.

That's all he wanted.
He glanced at his wristwatch,
noting the time.
19:35.
There was an arcade near
the entrance to the shopping street,
the lights calling to him.
Surely he could just step inside
and look around for a few minutes.
And he might not get another chance
to just enjoy the evening life
in a real Japanese city
by himself.

But Norma was holding his tag.
If he wasn't where she expected him
when she got to the shopping street,
would she deny him the tag?
Would she raise the stakes past just a date?
Was he _ready_ for that kind of move,
if it came down to it?
No.
Stop.
Think.
He tried to calm his racing pulse and thoughts.
There was no way she'd actually want _that_,
that was the plot of a bad fantasy.
In the real world,
he reminded himself,
people didn't actually use sex that way.

As a Stadium Head,
he told himself,
Norma is like an idol.
Not in a band like Zone or Morning Musume,
but a solo idol.
She had an image to maintain,
a demanding schedule,
and reponsibilities to the League.
And she'd basically announced their date
on her stage.
It had to be a professional thing,
something to cultivate her image.
Showing a tourist Tamer around the city.
That's all this was,
he told himself.
A big PR stunt.
