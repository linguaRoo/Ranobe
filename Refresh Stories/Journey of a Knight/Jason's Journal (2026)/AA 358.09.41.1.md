# Jason’s Journal (2026 Edition)

## 4. Morning Routine

Regularity is good in the pattern of a day. Being out of Darmi’s home doesn’t change that fact, even if it means I need to change some of the details. Guarn is still asleep. Tomo is rekindling the fire. In a few minutes, I’ll be practicing my forms. Tomo suggested we rely on the simmered sauce and some of our ration bread for breakfast.

The thread and the wards held up. We weren’t awoken, or killed, overnight. I didn’t want to admit it last night, but I was afraid. It’s my first time actually sleeping outside of a settled area. Karishé may be small, but it has walls. Camp Black’s wall may be crude, but it’s also run by a military expert. Even the one time we went to Orìnthia, we had inns and towns along the way there.

When the undead wake after sundown, it’s dangerous to be outside of walls. Tents with proper warding aren’t expensive, but they require attention when they’re set up and struck down. 

I know this is a short entry, but it’s still early. I’ll write more around midday.
