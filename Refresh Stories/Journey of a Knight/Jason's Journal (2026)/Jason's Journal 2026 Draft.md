# Jason’s Journal (2026 Edition)

## 1. Preparations

The heat is unbearable, but I still feel better wearing leathers over my tunic. I know today is just a practice run, but I can’t help but worry about Nethan trying to follow us out onto the road. Even though he’s my elder brother, he acts more like a child than I do at times. But, I need to make it clear to him before the new month breaks, this is a journey for myself. I want to prove my own strength and endurance.

I’ve been trying to plan rations for the journey. A pound of dried food for each of us per day should give us the necessary nutrients, and I know we’re heading into an inhospitable desert that is only a few hours east of Karishé. Tomodak gives me the side eye as I count the hard bundles of skypaper-wrapped rations.

“You won’t be needing those with me and Guarn along,” he told me earlier. “Leastways not so many.”

But isn’t it better to have more than I need? I’ve been training my endurance, my understanding of how much to drink and how often, how to survive hiking on restricted food. But I’ve _been_ in the Jargyn desert before, years ago. I nearly dried myself to death back then, and only really know what happened from stories Sara and Darmi have shared.

My course, our course, is already planned. Sara will be on a break from her studies, and I’d never want her to starve or suffer like I did in those first days. We need to ensure we’ve plenty of food and water for the entire journey, and so for tonight, tomorrow and it’s night, and the day after, Tomo and I are testing out camping gear.

We need to be certain it will hold up during the dark hour, after all.

---

## 2. Camping

The hike out to our campsite took about three hours. Guarn took the lead, acting as our our scout and wayfinder. We need to be in the practice of filling our roles, after all, as none of us have truly been on any quest before. We’re all rookies, after all. Sara isn’t even quite finished with her studies, but she’s joining us during the break in her magic school.

Guarn says that I’m underestimating the amount of food in the desert. He always takes Tomo’s side, but I suppose that’s to be expected. They knew each other before I knew how to speak with them. Even still, he agreed it’s better to bring more rations than we hope to need. He’s not that bad.

Before we headed out, I did have one last lesson with my sword master. He complimented by forms, but I feel like he was hiding something. I know I’m unproven. Duelling lessons aren’t the same as combat, I get that, but I’m confident I’ll be ready for the real thing. Not that I have any intention of rushing into my first battle.

Guarn did stop a few times, to make sure we knew some of the signs of water. Plants that have shallow roots or that wilt if there’s not enough water underground. He’s never really gone deep into the desert, but he’s confident we’ll be able to survive.

---

## 3. Evening Reflections

It’s the end of the day now. I set up our tents. Tomo got the firewood and prepared a perimeter of silver thread around the camp site. Guarn started the firepit and made us a simple meal of fried roots and semi-dried meat. Not exactly our travel rations, but a satisfying meal. I’ll rely on Guarn for the cooking.

I mean, anybody can make a passable meal on the road. I can make a decent meal in a kitchen. But Guarn knows more about dressing and sauces and how to build a dish, even in a relatively rough environment. I couldn’t have made these wild roots and meat taste so good without a kitchen.

Tomo is placing some extra wards around the camp. It won’t be long before the undead start to wake, so we need to be sure we’re laying our defenses well before the dark hour hits. I offered to help, but he insisted he would be fine. I think he takes his duties as a holy warrior too seriously sometimes.

But then he does something like the bet. A bet pool on finding a big kill, good meat, in the desert. 6 daimos to enter. Just a small wager among friends. Paid out when we reach Orìnthe, there’s a chance none of us come out ahead or behind. It will keep us all on our toes at least. Guarn already doubled the bid, which seems too confident for me, but brings the pool to 24 daimos. I wonder if Sara will join the pool…

When we leave, I don’t intend to return without proving myself. They say there’s a dragon’s hoard in the Mountains of Orìnthe, and rumors that it contains legendary arms and armor abandoned by the Semeric who used to live in the area. Some even claim that there are traded gifts from the Adin in the area. Bringing home even a single one of them would prove I belong. That I’m not just some fool’s dream, that… that I was worth Darmi’s time and effort.

I wonder if it will be enough to earn me a knighthood.

I’m going to sign off now and work on carving one the staves my lumbersmith master assigned me. If adventuring doesn’t work out, at least I have the steady hand for wood carving. And if I work hard, maybe the staves will even be good for sale or trade when I return to Karishé.

## 4. Morning Routine

Regularity is good in the pattern of a day. Being out of Darmi’s home doesn’t change that fact, even if it means I need to change some of the details. Guarn is still asleep. Tomo is rekindling the fire. In a few minutes, I’ll be practicing my forms. Tomo suggested we rely on the simmered sauce and some of our ration bread for breakfast.

The thread and the wards held up. We weren’t awoken, or killed, overnight. I didn’t want to admit it last night, but I was afraid. It’s my first time actually sleeping outside of a settled area. Karishé may be small, but it has walls. Camp Black’s wall may be crude, but it’s also run by a military expert. Even the one time we went to Orìnthia, we had inns and towns along the way there.

When the undead wake after sundown, it’s dangerous to be outside of walls. Tents with proper warding aren’t expensive, but they require attention when they’re set up and struck down. 

I know this is a short entry, but it’s still early. I’ll write more around midday.

---

## 5. Slow Morning

Guarn slept most of the morning. It’s fine today, since we’re not really hiking out further, but I’m still frustrated. It made the morning too quiet. I did my forms. I studied some of the local plants, and worked on my carving.

I reviewed some of the legends of the area. That’s my real passion, stories and seeking the truths hidden or buried within them. But studying legends doesn’t get me out on my own, it’s a noble hobby, not a practical one. But I suppose I can make soem notes on one of the stories I reviewed.

### The Legend of Kitasha

About a century ago there was an Adin Knight named Kitasha. She was a celebrity who originally hailed from the mainland, Vérnalorn. She fought in the international arena, a sword dancer who blended fire and lightning into her performance. She was assigned to a vampire hunt here on Atrusia, north of us around Zembrald. She was bold enough to try to fight her target during the dark hour.

But as we all know, the dark hour empowers the tainted spirits of the undead. It’s said that Kitasha fought a vampiress known as Vimora. And it’s said the battle ended in a bittersweet tragedy. Vimora struck, some versions say to turn Kitasha to her side, others say she sought to kill the Knight. Regardless, her strike was true and her fangs closed on the neck of Kitasha, as Kitasha’s mithral spear pierced the cold, black heart of the vampiress. But Kitasha had been preparing a vial of blessed water, and while it was raised over her head, it fell on them both, engulfing Vimora in silver flames and burning her to ash.

But when morning came, Kitasha fell into a deep slumber from which she would not wake. The people tried to rouse her, feeling the faintest beating of her heart and seeing the faint movement of her chest. But when she did not stir, they carried her to the temple, where a medical acolyte tended to her body until nightfall.

The acolyte was found in bloodied robes the next morning, and Kitasha has never been seen since, except in rumours and shadows. The people report that she was turned by Vimora and became a vampiress herself, and that the only trace of her was the vials of blessed water she threw from her belt in the temple.

### My Thoughts

Is there truth to this?

Well, it is true that Kitasha Lorébach was a well known Knight in the international arena around this time. She had a hatred of the undead, and readily accepted missions to hunt them down. Zembrald was scourged by a band of undead.

It’s likely that Kitasha would have accepted a mission to fight the leader of the undead around Zembrald. But it’s unclear what truly happened. I personally believe that if the people suspected her of being a vampire in the sunsleep, they would have burned her. But if they believed she was simply gravely wounded? They may well have taken her to the temple.

But the legends say she was unresponsive. That suggests the people should have suspected the sunsleep. They should have suspected she was turned, and either killed or imprisoned her. Silver threads, wards, even a head of garlic hanging over her should have rendered a newly turned vampire safe until they could verify if she was exhausted or turned.

And while they’re rare, some tales claim she returned home to Vérnalorn and was sent into the Outcast Caverns, an isolated part of the underground only accessible through teleportation.

One last point of truth, though: She never did return to the arena. So something must have happened to her, however accurate the legends of her battle are in other areas.

Guarn is calling me now. He says we need to be ready to hunt in the desert, and it must be time to go seeking some local game.

## 6. Exhaustion

It was too hot today! I don’t know how that wolf bastard Guarn can act like he’s not worn out by the heat, his fur _must_ be hotter than my leathers! And Tomo running around with those enviable pecs and abs. Okay, I get it, they’ve both been training longer than me, but this was probably the worst day of my life since Darmi took me in.

Still, we were able to catch a few hares. Guarn sent Tomo back to skin and dress them for dinner while he showed me how to forage for roots and local flowers for flavor. That always sounded so easy, but it’s not as easy as I thought. We aren’t even in the desert yet, and it’s hard to find a lot of edibles.

But we found enough. Along with the ration bread, we’ll have an even better meal tonight, and Guarn swears that tending it properly over the embers will make an even sweeter breakfast.

I should keep working on the staff. But I’m just too tired after all of that. I think I should nap until food is ready.

---

I couldn’t sleep. I was too tired to do anything for a while, but when I went out, Tomo asked me to help secure the silver thread again. We need to keep it taut to form the barrier against the undead, and it tends to get loose when people are walking around and over it all day. A single snap would be disastrous.

So I walked the perimeter. Checked the tension in the silver threads. Noted the other wards Tomo put up yesterday. There were some holy symbols made of silver or sacred woods. One I didn’t recognize. “Thrk’urna,” Tomodak explained. “Our god of might and power. He wielded a hammer of dense stone that could split the earth and the heavens, but when he isn’t fighting, it’s his job to carry the world in its course.”

I nodded slowly. “So the ward is like asking him to carry the undead away?”

Tomodak shook his head. “It’s more of a final defense. May we have the strength to survive if all the other wards fail us. For someone obsessed with legends, you don’t seem to know much outside of your father’s people. And Ferindilb.”

I sighed. “Well, I suppose he’s more interested in what makes a good story or song,” I said. “The Silver Dragon. The Silver Stag. Kitasha Lorébach. Even ancestral stories like Hyrafis, the hero of their old world.”

“Mmm,” Tomodak muttered. “Don’t forget you aren’t the only one adopted by an Adin. Papa told me so many stories about Anedhar, you know? Sometimes I feel like I need to live up that legacy.”

“I don’t have a legacy,” I admitted. “But I get it. I know how many people think Darmi was crazy for taking me in. How hard it’s been to fit in.”

Tomodak placed a hand on my shoulder. “That’s why we get along so well. Guarn is my best friend before you, but he grew up with his mother’s pack. We’re both orphans. So let’s hold each other up until the world can see our worth.”

I smiled and reach to take his hand, clasping it firmly. “Yeah. Orphans stand together. That will be our strength.”

---

Dinner was better than last night. Guarn didn’t let us waste anything except the small bones, but even that was an offering to the spirits. He asked us to take watch shifts tonight, so he wouldn’t sleep all morning again. I got the short straw, so my watch is first. I’m just checking in to confirm we survived the day. I won’t have the light to make a final record after my watch, and I’ll need the sleep anyhow.
