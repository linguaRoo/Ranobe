# Guarn’s Journal (2026 Edition)

## 1. Tired

We were lucky tonight. Nothing came too close to our camp site. Not the dead. Not the undead. Not the living. And the sleeping fire has made the stew much better. The dark hour has passed, and we survived. Still, the undead are active.

They do frighten me. The living I can see clearly by night. Even a mouse scurrying about, or a bird flying past, they are warm. The dead, and the undead, are cold. Perhaps as an adventurer, it is best I try to acclimate to our daytime schedule. Hunting by day may be harder, but fighting a corpse or spirit that looks little different from the air about us may be worse. Still, at least I can smell and hear them. At least some of them. There was a ghoul wandering around a hundred yards away. It caught a small animal, I heard its kill and feast. And then it moved further afield.

I don’t know if it’s worth waking one of the others for a watch. We’ve survived the worst part of the night. The undead will grow tired soon, returning to the dust of the field. Powerless after dawn, though we’ve still a watch or so until then…

I guess I should wake Tomo. Better if we not take too many risks.
