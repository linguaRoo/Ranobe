# Tomodak’s Journal (2026 Edition)

## 1. Preparations

Ignorance. It must be bliss.

Jésen is not ignorant of the dangers of the desert. But perhaps he is too aware? The desert is not as lifeless as he may think, and between myself and Guarn there ought to be little danger of running out of food or water. Even in the heat we’re facing, it’s summer, not winter. Animals will not be slumbering, and between Guarn’s knowledge and my own, we can use the animals and plants of the Jargyn to find water between oases.

He would have us each carry a child’s weight of food from the first day, just to be safe, and more water than is reasonable. I will always support him, but this need for excess confuses me.

“You won’t need those,” I told him as he was counting out a mass of rationed foods wrapped in skypaper. “At least not so much. Guarn and I are good enough hunters.”

“Better prepared than needy,” he muttered.

I took a deep breath at that. It’s difficult to argue with him sometimes. So, I moved to the table where he had mapped out our route. It is not a route following the main roads. He has insisted we should not travel too easily, and I can agree with challenging ourselves, but I do worry about how far off the main routes we’ll be at times.

While Jason visits his master, I’ll go get the final supplies I need. Perhaps a spare spool of silver thread would be worth investing in. It can be fragile, and will be our first line of defense against the spirits and corpses at night.

“I’ll see you both for lunch,” Jésen said as he headed out. “We’re heading out after we eat, right?”

That’s right. I’ll need to find Guarn while I’m out too. Not that I think he’d miss lunch, but perhaps he can help me convince Jésen to cut back on the amount of rations he’ll insist on bringing.

---

## 2. Security

The morning was uneventful. I did buy a second spool of silver thread, and gathered the materials I need to keep our wards maintained. I’m no priest, but I can channel enough power to keep the sacred wards operating each night. The silver thread doesn’t need any charging, but does need to be kept above the ground. The slightest amount of dirt will break the ring.

For this dry run, we’ll all be in one tent, though we’ll have a second for Sara while we’re on the road. Jésen is setting it up. Guarn played the mediator, too. We brought the rations for this test run, and Guarn swore that he’d fix us a meal once camp was settled. But we’ll be taking about a week of rations each on the road. Enough to be safe, but not enough to slow us down.

We spent about 3 hours getting out here. Not a trivial walk home, but not so far that we can’t signal if we need to. I’ve been assigned to gather firewood, and then I’ll be setting up the silver thread perimeter and other wards. I have enough stakes to give us a good 10 yards radius, and to keep it taut.

Guarn will be doing the survival side. Jason is doing the tent. I’ll go do my part now.

---

## 3. Morale

Dinner was good. For rations, that is. The roots were fresh. The meat was from our ration packs. And Guarn even used some of the fruits to make a sauce. Better than anything I ate as an orphan.

I decided to try and get Jésen into the spirit of genuine survival, not just preparation. I set a bit of my own savings into a pot. Six daimos to be split between anybody who can get us good meat. Two shares to whoever gets the biggest. I could see his reluctance, but Jésen did agree. He threw in another six. Skeptical, I’m sure, but it’s something. Then Guarn let out a barking laugh and grinned like a feral wolf.

“I’ll double you up,” he said with a booming voice. “I’ll match both of your wagers, and Sara’s if she joins the pool too.”

Jésen laughed at that. I laughed when I saw the mood was genuine. “I’ll pay out double if you win, Jés. Don’t assume we’ll starve, try to find us something worthy of the trip!”

I know my friend’s resolve. He’s looking for something. Something big, something meaningful. A legacy, perhaps. Why else would we be heading to the Orìnthe Mountains where they say that dragon is nesting? And if he thinks he can find something to make a legacy…

Maybe I can find something to support my own.

I can tell the undead are stirring outside. Guarn insisted on taking the watch until after the dark hour ends. As long as we survive that, we should be fine all night. But it wouldn’t hurt to do one last check on my wards.

Tomodak Ognar  
358, Nernemesen 40  
Karishé Flatlands
