Synopsis: Jason Knight, the primary protagonist of an ensemble cast, has applied to the League of Knights to try to prove that Humans have a place on the world of Atrusius. As a Fighter (Eldritch Knight) He is accompanied by his best friends Tomodak, an Orkish Paladin, Sarah, a human fire mage and mentalist, and Rraukon, a Gramurn Ranger. They have a rotating cast of support characters, but are eventually joined by Jed, the final human adventurer (a frontline Fighter), and Leyshell, a halfling Rogue and scout.

### I. The Point of Origin (The Root)

- **The Status Quo:** Humans are outsiders on Atrusius, with only a few even present. While they are not discriminated against, their younger representatives feel like they don’t belong, and need to prove themselves. Jason doesn’t want to be a simple Adventurer, he wants international renown -- so he applies for the League of Knights, and is given an assignment to complete 7 challenges to qualify for the tournament, a chance to rise from from Aspirant to Squire.
	
- **The Catalyst:** After an earthquake, the party encounters a vampire -- and one who fights with deep strategy against the initial band. A magebane arrow ends up stopping her as she’s about to kill (or turn?) Rraukon, introducing Farzhan, who reveals that Kirora is not entirely evil. She escapes during this explanation, and Farzhan, a Cavalier asks the novice party to witness him when he captures her and saves the good soul trapped within her.
	
- **The Threshold:** After saving Kirora and meeting Farzhan, they decide to pursue the lost chapters of the Book of Nightstalkers, which Kirora had hidden across the desert. This provides a home base and hub they can return to if lost, and keeps senior Knights Kitasha and Farzhan invested in their journey.

### II. The Journey Outward (The Stem)

- **Exploration & Navigation:** Farzhan ensures the party has access to transportation between the hub and their missions. The desert is a dangerous place, but where there are large enough oases, towns and villages spring up. There are also Dungeon sites near some cities. The easiest target is at a nearby Oasis with only a fortified inn, but it’s separated from the Hub by a ravine that opened up in the recent earthquake. They can go after it right away, or head to the second chapter and return to the first chapter after a bridge is built…
	
- **Encounters:** In addition to various encounters with citizens of all species during their travels, they become entangled in a conflict and rivalry with freelance treasure hunters $JOLLY and $SAMBA, who are pursuing the Book of the Nightstalkers as well, and will target both treasure sites and the Party to get Chapters. They win some and lose more, but are always competent as bandits and treasure hunters.
	
- **Minor Successes & Setbacks:** Over the course of the first five Chapter Missions, the Party fails to retrieve the Third chapter, and when they finally reach the location of the First Chapter they skipped over, they find nothing there except a calling card signed “J.D.” They missed their chance and resolve to find the final location as quickly as possible.

### III. The Heart of the Quest (The Bloom)

- **The Hidden/Lost Location:** The final chapter contains a powerful secret -- a way to purge the condition of undeath from a living victim, notably how to turn Kirora from a Vampire back into a free soul. But it’s guarded but only by puzzles, but by a Lich, a powerful skeletal Undead who has also retrieved 2 other chapters by this point.
	
- **The Core Conflict:** $JOLLY and $SAMBA capture the party at the hub, including taking all of the Party’s chapters with them to the Lich. While the Book could free Kitasha from Vimora, it could also allow the Lich to become even more powerful -- and if it needs to be destroyed, that is more important than any Knight’s life.
	
- **The Revelation:** The ritual that the Lich and Farzhan want are the exact same ritual -- a spell to drive one soul out of a body. The party’s final option is to either destroy the entire book and Kitasha’s chance of being free, or to allow the Lich to become an immortal spirit that can bring its power and knowledge into entirely new bodies and ages, but try to swipe the book before he can take over his next body and have a chance at saving Kitasha.

### IV. The Aftermath (The Harvest)

- **The Resolution:** Jed sacrifices himself by collapsing the passage out, but the rest are able to escape -- with the book. Was Kitasha’s freedom worth Jed’s life? Was the choice they made the right one, since the Lich can now live forever?
	
- **The Journey Back/Onward:** They’re able to save Kitasha, and having the book, she reveals that the book still wasn’t finished -- and she’s sure they can find a way to banish or even destroy the Lich’s spirit. 
	
- **The New Status Quo:** The Aspirants are recognized by the League as eligible for the next tournament -- but will be expected to engage in new adventures under Farzhan’s mentorship until the tournament.
