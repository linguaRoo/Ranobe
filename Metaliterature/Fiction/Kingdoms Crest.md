# kuLarijmo Shan (The Kingdom’s Crest)

> [!info] Information  
> - Author: Roman Danen (Adin, literary), Eren Nissar (Hol’tiin, meten)
> - Conceit: Epic Adventure
> - Genres: Adventure, Action, Morality

A two-year fictional history in which the displaced ruler of a kingdom is pushed to claim a sacred sword, renew his family’s covenant with a divine being, and use the divine blessing to defeat the Emperor of the conquering nation. The world of _The Kingdom’s Crest_ has developed in a number of works produced by various authors over the years, with strongly gapped continuity, often involving centuries between individual stories.

When the first story was published, the copyright term was on a 6+6 year scheme—rather than the modern 12 year flat scheme—however Roman Danen forgot to renew his copyright for the second 6 years, so the story title has been in the public domain since 239. Many authors have extended on The Kingdom’s Crest with their own nations, kingdoms, dynasties, and conflicts in different years and contexts.

## Literary Release

The Kingdom’s Crest was originally published in weekly literature anthologies. Each chapter was originally about 1,000 to 2,000 words, and explored the world of the characters (which was never given a name within this story, but has widely come to be called _Larimur_ since). The Adin prince Xarien is accompanied initially by a band of loyalists from his kingdom, Etlashé. As he seeks the resting place of his ancestor’s sacred sword, in a distant northern shrine in the holy kingdom of Jurliari, he fights the foes of the conquering Dorvamn Empire.

## Meten Adaptation

In the Silver Age, a Hol’tiin artist named Eren Nissar decided to adapt the original story into a folio series on a 4-weekly schedule. He used bold lines, strong colors, and heavy use of solid ink hatching and shading to create a distinctive feel. He embellished the story with lore from later stories by Danen and other authors working within the setting, specifically implicating two other stories from the mid-3rd century AA as being contemporaries which were also caught up in the Dorvamn Empire’s worldwide campaign of conquest.

## Primary Characters

### Etlashé Xarien Hirtal (Prince Xarien of Etlashé)

The story begins with an inciting incident: Prince Xarien is spirited out of his kingdom by night as his father and elder sister (the crown princess Etlai) stand their ground against what appears to be a bandit uprising by the Red Scarf Army. Over the following years, spies remaining within Etlashé communicate that the Red Scarves were paid to incite their rebellion by Dorvamn, and this motivates Prince Xarien to seek alliances to take back his kingdom.

He is a nervous but passionate youth, too forthright and nervous to play politics well, but he grows with the advice of his coterie, and is told by the King of Andon that Andon will support his bid if he can bring the Sword of the Silver Dragon from its northern resting place, and that he will also secure the aid of other kingdoms. Xarien agrees to the quest, and receives the aid of clerics, wizards, and the princess Elaure of Andon as he makes his way north.

On his journeys, he also recruits great support from the oppressed lower classes, especially in lands where Dorvamn has claimed influence: he develops a deep respect for the people who keep nations alive, from farmers to merchants to artisans and guildworkers. He uses the enlightenment of this empathy to endure the Trial of Silvering and earn the sword, and the blessing of the Silver Dragon on his quest.

### Andon Elaure Kairotal (Princess Elaure of Andon)

Princess Elaure is the second daughter of Andon and knows her duty is to be married for political alliance. However, she is no idle princess, and cares deeply for the suffering caused by Dorvamn. She has a sacred blessing and wields divine magic, and insists that she accompany Prince Xarien as a witness on her father’s behalf.

Over the course of their adventures, Xarien and Elaure grow fond of each of each other, and in the conclusion of the story they are the reigning King and Queen Consort of Etlashé. The plotline between the two never approaches their relationship as romantic, but as one of mutual support and political alliance in the aftermath of victory over Dorvamn.
