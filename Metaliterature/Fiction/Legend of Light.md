# Kendan i Lorek (Legend of Light)

> [!info] Information  
> - Author: Danel Laumen (Adin, literary), Miklos Laumen (Adin, meten)
> - Conceit: Epic Adventure
> - Genres: Adventure, Action, Morality

One of the early epics of the modern anthology era, written in the early 220s, _Legend of Light_ ran for four years and told the story of four heroes chosen by the Heart of Light and given sacred crystals to empower them with the echoes of past heroes. Tasked with finding the Elemental Hearts of the world, they set out from a small kingdom. As the story progresses, their crystals grant them new insight into historical heroes and unlock new abilities for them. They end their journey by stopping the Knight of Darkness from using the Heart of Darkness to overwhelm the crystals they’ve restored, and defeating the spirit of Darkness sending it to sleep until the next cycle.

## Literary Release

The Legend of Light was originally published in fortnightly literature anthologies. Each chapter was originally about 2,000 to 3,000 words, and explored the psychology of the chosen heroes, and their relationships with the historical heroes Beppa, Xaron, Tezjre, and Leyn. They overcome the Knight of Darkness and defeat the spirit of Darkness, though they are warned that the cycle repeats every 864 (600 in base-12) years. 

## Meten Adaptation

Roughly a century after the original story was written and published, Miklos Laumen (the son of Danel) used artwork produced by his father and his own fond memories of the story’s development (when he was still a young child) as a basis for a serialized meten adaptation that ran for four years and strove to adapt the original story in full. While some initial critics claimed that the artwork was inconsistent, the loose and flowing style grew on audiences and cemented the core of Danel’s later career as a watercolor folio artist with _Meten’twanu Orindau_ (Golden Comics). 

This adaptation cemented the visual identities of the heroes. The Gramurn Warrior of Light’s Heart wore a brilliant suit of red armor and a full helmet, the Hol’tiin Mage of Life wore a bleached white cloak with a rainbow of glyphs along the lower hem, the Kenyu _Nashix_ of Nobility wore a flowing blouse and braies as well as a silver diadem and other rings and bracelets, and the Orkish Sorcerer of Flame wore a wide-brimmed hat that cast shadows over his face, and a deep burgundy poncho with various pouches and pockets to conceal his spell-casting materials.

## Primary Characters

### Ferio

Ferio, the Gramurn Warrior, is often portrayed as a noble and righteous, if somewhat clueless, front-liner. He takes the fore and is most concerned with martial affairs. He is paired with the crystal spirit Tezjre, the ancient Semeric Paladin, and while Ferio wields a sword (and later two swords), their relationship guides the heroic soul of the Warrior.

### Urraxme

Urraxme, the Hol’tiin Mage of Life, is the heart of the team, providing supportive magic and a patient heart. Urraxme is paired with the crystal spirit of Xaron, a survivalist strider, and while they wield magic Xaron never did, their relationship grows into the provider and sustainer of the team.

### Selaio

Selaio, the Kenyu _Nashix_ of Nobility, is a royal from a Kenyu nation who was disgraced from his family for his reckless duelling and gambling. He wields light swords and daggers, and has developed the skills of infiltration and keen insight for danger. He is paired with the crystal spirit of Beppo, a Peratan noble who was noted for insight and invisibility. Though they have fundamentally different views on what a free life looks like, both agree that their place in saving the world trumps their desire for freedom while the threat persists.

### Darkam

Darkam, the Orkish Sorcerer of Flame, is an itinerant researcher from Yshkaren (the only real-world city named in the story) who stumbles into the sacred quest by finding the first crystal shard of Light’s Heart. He is seen as the most pragmatic and powerful member of the group: though he holds great power, he is reluctant to use it. He is paired with the crystal spirit of Leyn, a Kenyu strider, who initially distrusts the Ork, but grows to respect his tactical mind and restraint. They are ultimately the strategic heart of the team.
