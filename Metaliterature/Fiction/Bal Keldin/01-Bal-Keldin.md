# Bal Keldin—Bal the Superman

The earliest iteration of the Bal Keldin character was an alien figure who might pass for Adin at a distance, but had an elongated body proportion, and had only three digits per limbs rather than the five typical of Gaean life or the four typical of Atrusian life.

![](Metaliterature/Fiction/Bal%20Keldin/Concept%20-%20Bal%20the%20Superman.png)A rough drawing of Bal by **The Author** (signed as NKnight and JenSon); the text, written in a version of Adin script labels the absent colors of his design: _abse_ (white) hair, _shif_ (light grey) bodysuit, _azul_[^1] (blue) skin and the field of the crest, and _roha_ (red) for the shape and border of the crest and the loincover.

## Debut Medium

Bal Keldin debuted in AA 292, before the modern weekly _méten coritan_ publishing schedule was established. At the time, _méten_ were published primarily as monthly folio standalone chapters, usually with 24-30 pages of illustrated story, and extra content such as written fiction, standalone illustrations, or even _tantal_ (mini-tales) separate from the main monthly narrative. _Bal Keldin_ specifically was told in a series of these older standalone folios, using a 40-pages count format: 28-32 pages of the primary story, one or two pages of _luxamét_ (4-panel) strips, and standalone artwork or _tantal_ for the remainder.

## Debut Story

The character first debuted as a grown, adult, alien whose vessel had crashed on the then-modern Atrusius. As he made his way into the fictional city of Kandyur to seek assistance, he was surrounded by a group of bandits. Using a powerful empathic sense, he was able to dodge and disable the bandits as they attempted to rob him, learning their language through a form of psychic learning facilitated by his bare hands or feet touching the skin of the bandits.

Securing the bandits, he took them to Kandyur to face discipline. He explained his origins from a distant star (not named in this story) as he delivered them to the city’s police, and that he was confused by the concept of bandits and crime’s driven by desperation or necessity—among his people, there was no need for such behaviours. The police contacted a skilled magitech engineer to assess the damages to the visitor’s ship, and asked if he had a name.

Introducing himself to the police and the engineer, he simply said, “You may call me Bal.”

## Continuing Stories

Over the next 12 years, Bal fought a mixture of local crime, bandits, and even international threats, monsters, and other aliens throughout a 144 issue run. The climax of his story came when another of his people arrived and demanded the world surrender to the imperial governance of the Kyrellan Empire. Bal stood up for Atrusius, fighting the General Porun, and driving him off. Nethan, the engineer he had met a dozen years ago, was finally able to repair Bal’s vessel, and Bal swore he would keep the people of Atrusius safe from the Kyrellan army before he left.

## Reboots

The original series has been rebooted and modernized several times over the 6 decades since, sometimes as continuations where Bal returns, and sometimes as reboots from the very beginning. There is currently no actively running iteration of the Bal the Superman continuity, though there is talk of one in development by Luati Morlan, the author of a recently ended Dungeon Delver series.
