# I Love Bal Keldin!!

Set in a modern academy school, Bal Kalish is a normal superman with moderate empathic and mana burst powers. He’s been harboring a crush on his childhood friend, Liyer Taramos, and uses his lunch break to compose a letter of confession. He tries to slip it in her locker at the end of the first day, but on Day Two discovers that somehow, he put it in the wrong locker, and now has the earnest devotion of a girl he only met yesterday -- Burna Detterk, an Orkish athlete with fire powers! As his empathy interferes with his attempts to let her down gently, he gains the interest of Hol’tiin water-powered girl Riva Lahan and Gramurn earth manipulator Lo’on Maura Rashaan, as well as Liyer’s own interest. Other girls also join this dynamic relationship web, starting with the Kenyu lightning-wielder, Lakora Neliel, whom Bal calls “Koko” (his childhood nickname for her).

This romantic comedy explores how deep empathy affects a traditional lovable everyman harem dynamic, with an ever-growing web of supermen who may homage and tribute to the entire history of the Bal Keldin mythos. It is written by Miss Jerin Romaren, noted for her cutting romantic stories from her directorial debut in _The Pollan Household Tales_, to her most recently completed _Why Can’t I Hate the God I Love!?_ in which the protagonist laments that she can’t find it in herself to hate the person she loves after finding out that he’s actually a god -- and finds a support group of similar men and women who have wound up in romances with immortal beings without realizing their lovers were immortal.

## Fan Theories

### Liyer is a Vampire

Since the beginning of the Dark World arc (around chapter 84), there has been a theory that Liyer Taramos is a vampire, or half-vampire. The primary arguments in favor of this are that none of the Dark World suitors seem interested in her, her emotional void could represent being undead or emotionally dulled, and that vampires (on Atrusius) are widely associated with wind elemental powers and being effortlessly charming.

Opponents of this theory note that her powers do not grow weaker during the day, her three favorite foods all include garlic, and she is never shown as being unusually pale. Liyer has almost always had a wind affinity (as wind is the element most associated with healing) and been Bal’s emotional support (explaining why her emotional power counters his). They also note that Liyer’s power could be the reason other suitors aren’t actively pursuing her, if she can transmit some sense of “disinterest” towards them.

The theory ebbs and surges as the arc is currently developing, and Romaren refuses to comment on the direction other than stating that “the ending is already set, the only variable is how long I’ll get to enjoy telling the story.”

### Burna is the Protagonist

This theory is fairly straightforward -- because Burna was the first girl to fall for Bal, she’s the real protagonist and Bal himself is a side character. The title of the series is “I Love Bal Keldin!!” which suggests a perspective other than Bal as the protagonist, so it must be one of the girls. Burna may have already had a crush on Bal when she called out to him on the road in Chapter 1, since she recognized him as an athlete for a sport she can’t play.

This theory is somewhat popular, as Burna also seems to get the most page time out of all the girls, whether it is with Bal or the other girls, and seems to have the most emotional development to date, from explaining to Bal early on how the Orkish concept of love is really different from love in other cultures, and more recently that she’s starting to consider all of the other girls to be _ngarak_ to her.

Romaren has commented about the theory in an editorial that she’s, “…glad so many people can enjoy this story and find so many ways to understand it.”

### The Girls are Gender-Flipped Supermen

Romaren has confirmed that every character in the series draws on past characters from throughout the mythos. However, it is not always clear exactly which superman, or supermen, go into each of the characters, as elemental connections alone are repeated both across different past series and sometimes even within series. With that is mind, the major characters are most often associated with:

- Burna from Burmon (Magical Doppelganger) and Dompen (a TerrorNet Operator from Cyber Soldier)
- Riva from Sa’lé (Magical Doppelganger) and Twar (sometimes localized as “Flow”) (a TerrorNet Avint from Cyber Soldier)
- Maura from Janoc (Magical Doppelganger) and Stríbo (a criminal sorcerer from the original Bal Keldin)
