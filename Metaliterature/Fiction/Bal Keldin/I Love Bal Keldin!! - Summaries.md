# Summaries of _I Love Bal Keldin!!_ Chapters

## Year 1 - Feb. 372 — Feb. 373 (Chapters 1 - 75)

### Chapter 1 (pub. 372-Feb-14)

We meet Bal Kalish and his family — his father Luko, his mother Aneu, and his younger brother Gi. He is a fairly average 10 year old starting his 6th year of Academy at a superhero school called the Kangyur Keldin Academy, and intends to confess his crush on his best friend, Liyer Taramos, through her locker at the end of the day. On the way into school, he meets Burna Detterk and her friends, Parka and Bel. The chapter ends with Liyer revealing that she and Bal are assigned to separate classes.

### Chapter 2 (pub. 372-Feb-21)

Bal is waiting for his seating assignment between two female students—the Hol’tiin Riva and the Gramurn Maura—and he is immediately aware of their pre-existing rivalry with each other in academics and popularity. Vowing not to get between them, he is assigned the seat in the center of the class—while Riva and Maura end up on either side of him. A lecture on the known science of superpowers is taught by Mister Elz, a middle-aged Kenyu with ice powers and a fittingly chilly classroom style. The chapter ends with Bal feeling like he’s being stabbed from both sides by the girls sitting next to him and struggling to pay attention to the lecture.

### Chapter 3 (pub. 372-Feb-28)

Bal is eating lunch at the rooftop garden of the school, under a lightly shaded pergola where he’s isolated himself from the other students on the roof. As he writes in his notebook, a lock of hair comes into view: Liyer asks him what he’s up to. Quickly closing the notebook, he tells her he’s just trying to calm down. He explains the situation in class, and how he feels like he could become a casualty in the rivalry between Riva and Maura. Liyer smiles and pulls out her lunch, offering Bal some of her garlic-oiled rolls. She talks to him about her morning, and how Miss Mikti seems to get along with the more physical students better. She mentions that the “girls from this morning,” are all in her class, and mentions their powers — Burna is a spontaneous fire wielder, Parka can weave silken threads into shields and weapons, and Bel can turn her body into different metals. Bal admits he was too distracted to remember his classmates’ powers. The chapter ends with Liyer hoping that whoever ”that note” (the one Bal was working on) is for takes it well, revealing that she had time to at least read it a bit.

### Chapter 4 (pub. 372-Feb-35)

During the afternoon classes, Bal discovers that Maura and Riva will compete over pretty much anything — maths, volunteering for questions, and even new friends. Maura confronts him about his garlic breath before they switch to elective courses, asking him not to eat so much of it while she has to sit next to him. Using aquatic sports as his first elective, Bal finds that Riva is in the same elective and she quickly challenges him to a 10-lap race. She reveals that she was on one of the teams Bal’s Gryphons had beat last year, but was ill the day of their game, and so she has a personal grudge. But when Bal wins the race, she lets it go: The chapter ends with her saying, “Well, I guess you are better in the water than me.”

### Chapter 5 (pub. 372-Feb-42)

The day is over and Bal slips his love letter into Liyer’s locker when he’s sure she isn’t around. He walks home having a daydream about her reaction to the letter in the morning, and smiling about the successful operation when he hears Burna’s voice. “So that sunflower’s yer sweetie, huh?” They have a short conversation about Bal’s childhood with Liyer until they reach the corner where Bal has to turn towards his own home. “Well, I hope we get a chance to compete on the field sometime, blue.” The chapter ends in the morning when Burna and Liyer open their lockers (directly next to each other), and Burna sees the envelope with Bal’s confession letter.

### Chapter 6 (pub. 372-Mar-05)

Burna opens the letter and reads the message. Liyer glances at her and asks what’s up. “I got my first ever love confession!” She notices the name at the bottom and grips the letter tightly, crumpling it in her hand. “And I’m gonna show him I’m worth it!” Liyer wishes her luck and they head to their morning classes. During a mandatory fitness course, the class all get to test and demonstrate their powers. Riva reveals that she can control any water around her and has gills so she can breathe normally under water. She loses the ability to manipulate water if it freezes, but can control it as vapor. Maura has the ability to control earth and stones around her by dancing, but only within a short range, though the range is greater for things on the ground. Bal has to explain his empathy and how he can feel all the strong emotions around him, and demonstrate that and his ability to focus his plasma energy into mana bursts. The chapter ends with Burna looking at Bal on the rooftop during lunch.

### Chapter 7 (pub. 372-Mar-12)

Bal is eating a greens and berries salad when his eyes go wide and he looks up. Burna is standing there, holding the wrinkled letter from her locker: “Thank you for this letter! I’d like to get closer to you as well, Bal!” Confused by the letter, Bal is overwhelmed by the intensity of Burna’s emotions. It takes a moment for him to realize what’s going on, and he tries to explain that he got the wrong locker, when his powers flare up and he clutches at his stomach. All he can bring himself to say is that he hopes they can find time. The scene cuts to the other side of the roof where Riva and Maura are competing to eat their lunch faster, but Maura points out the big Ork girl over by Bal. “If he’s that popular, I bet I can win him over faster than you.” Riva refuses to be goaded into an emotional battle, but expresses interest in whether Maura can beat Miss Detterk.

### Chapter 8 (pub. 372-Mar-19)

Burna offers to bring lunch to share with Bal tomorrow, and asks if he enjoys spicy food as they head back inside. During the afternoon session, Miss Mikti covers history, discussing the heroism of Bal Kyrlas and how he had defended the world from a powerful magical incursion, bringing together other supermen from across the world with his hope for the brighter future that “we all now live in.” But not everybody wanted to be heroes, so the Kangyur Keldin Academy was established to train heroes to protect civilians from the inevitable monsters and villains of the world.

Bal heads to the athletic building for his aquatic sports elective, but is stopped in the hallway when a furry paw taps his shoulder.

### Chapter 9 (pub. 372-Mar-26)

Maura smiles as Bal turns to her and invites him to ditch and join her at the botanical garden. Bal’s eyes narrow and he reveals he can tell she’s as much playing as she is serious. He admits that the garden sounds nice, but he also really needs to keep his core discipline up as a diveballer. Maura offers that they can cut after their first electives. Riva comes up and asks Maura what she‘s up to, then reminds Bal that he’ll need time to change into his swimwear, shooing him off. She walks with Maura down the hall, then continues towards the athletic building. She competes for number of laps with Bal, winning thanks to her gills. As Bal leaves the locker room, Maura is waiting for him, holding out her paw as her ears perk up.

### Chapter 10 (pub. 372-Mar-33)

Maura reveals a deep love of flowers and trees at the botanical garden, and Bal has a good time, though he feels guilty about cutting school early. As they leave the gardens, Maura thanks him for joining her and leans in, sniffing him lightly. Bal blushes and thanks her for inviting him. Then, Maura’s eyes narrow and she leans in, licking his cheek. “See you tomorrow,” she says, walking off briskly.

Liyer calls out to Bal and asks who she just saw him with. Bal explains that one of his classmates invited him on a date, and he couldn’t bring himself to turn her down. “But didn’t you confess to Burna too? Playing the field while you’re young, Bal?” Bal awkwardly protests the idea that he doesn’t care about either Burna or Maura, he just barely knows them and without Liyer around it’s harder for him to say to no. “Oh, so you wouldn’t have any problem turning me down, hmm?” The chapter ends with Bal’s face in an amazingly rendered screwed and flustered expression, thinking that it would actually be even harder to say no to her.

### Chapter 11 (pub. 372-Mar-40)

At dinner, Aneu notes that she saw Bal out with a gramurn girl during the afternoon. Luko notes that he’s glad to see Bal showing an interest in relationships, but that he really shouldn’t skip school. Gi grins and teases Bal about how easily flustered he gets during the scene. Bal spends the evening doing his homework and dreaming about Liyer, Maura, and Burna. As he goes downstairs for a bedtime snack, Aneu takes his hand. “I’m really glad to see you growing up. Don’t mind Gi, you know he just likes to rile you up. I thought you looked really handsome with your girlfriend.” He thanks his mom and gives her a hug, heading back upstairs with some sliced fruits and a cup of isa milk.

### Chapter 12 (pub. 372-Apr-3)

Bal realizes that he’s in a tricky position when Burna is waiting at the corner for him. She’s acting a bit awkward and shy, admitting that Bal is her first boyfriend, but she really wants to talk with him on the way to school. While she stutters a bit today, she explains she’s not really good with feelings, because orkish feelings are usually seen as so intense that they put people off. “Basically unless we’re fighting, y’know, people think  
we’re not in control of our feelings. And when we are fighting, I guess, they just think that’s alright.” Bal pauses, feeling her sincerity.

“You know, I can feel what you mean. It’s like a boiling hot pot, right? Everybody is happy when all the food comes out, but you need to cover it up to let it cook. And you feel like you’re always covering it up, letting your heart cook until it’s what everybody is going to love. Well… I don’t want you to hide from me. I promise I won’t hate you for showing me what’s in your heart.”

Burna’s face is full of deep emotion, looking somewhere between happy, afraid, and about to burst into tears. “By Kirkedz, I don’t know how I earned your interest. Yes. I promise, I won’t hide from you, partner.” The chapter ends with Burna grabbing Bal’s hand, looking down at him, in front of the school gates.

### Chapter 13 (pub. 372-Apr-10) 

Burna asks Bal to meet her in the courtyard for lunch as they head into the building. They part ways to their lockers and for morning classes. Between classes, a gramurn boy from a different class barges into Bal’s classroom and points at him. “Blue-haired Adin boy, I challenge you to a duel after classes! The prize shall be the right to court Rashaan!” Bal shows deep confusion, then his eyes go wide. “Rashaan? You mean Maura?”

After the challenger leaves, and noting that Maura isn’t around, Riva explains that Taerrmon has been trying to court Maura since they were in junior academy, and asks what happened between Bal and Maura yesterday. Maura returns and asks why everybody is staring, but then Miss Gilda comes in and starts their economics course.

### Chapter 14 (pub. 372-Apr-17)

As economics ends, Maura apologizes to Bal for Taerrmon’s challenge. She notes that he’s a very traditional Gramurn, a year ahead of them, and that she’ll try to make him back down during lunch. She notes that if there is a duel, he’s got a combination of wood and wind powers, and practices swordplay.

On the roof, Bal finds that Burna has already claimed the pergola and set out four dishes of food. He offers her some of the salad his mother gave him, and some grilled chicken skewers. Burna’s heart starts to race, and Bal smiles. They discuss growing up, and Burna says she’s always wished she could just be honest about her feelings outside of her house. She admits that she‘s always wanted a small pet, and that someday, she just wants to wear a dress and feel pretty. Bal talks about his cousin’s cat and how even though it’s tiny, it’s always trying to attack him, and that he wants to be a superhero like Bal Kyrlas, how he’s always believed in his greatness because his parents named him for such an important hero.

The chapter ends with both of them reaching for a piece of spicy garlic chicken, both blushing as their hands touch.

### Chapter 15 (pub. 372-Apr-24)

Burna pulls her hand back and takes the last of the grilled skewers. “I’m glad you like the spicy food. I know some Adin struggle with it.” Bal nods slightly. “Well, I know it means a lot to you that we had this chance, Burna. And I’m glad for it too.” They finish eating silently, both smiling, and Burna starts to pack up the dishes she’d brought her food in. She gives a playful grin and asks, “I don’t think you intended that letter for me, did you?” Bal admits the accident, but also says he’s glad with how it turned out. And he’s pretty sure that’s not just because Burna is glad. They hold hands briefly while covering up the last dish, smile, and then part ways back to class.

The afternoon session is skipped in the narrative, with Taerrmon standing at the lockers to make sure Bal doesn’t turn coward. Bal says he thought they weren’t supposed to duel, but as the chapter ends, his homeroom and science teacher, Mr. Elz, notes that it’s fine as long as a faculty member supervises — and Taerrmon had already asked him to witness.

### Chapter 16 (pub. 372-Apr-31)

Burna steps up behind Bal and says he has to defend himself whatever this is about. Bal grimaces, not wanting to explain how he seems to be dating two girls, but also acknowledging internally that he needs to. “Fine. As long as it’s okay, we can duel, green-maned Gramurn. By the way, my name is Bal Kalish.” Taerrmon finally introduces himself as Taerrmon Laira Mi’arlaen. The scene shifts to one of the sporting fields with Elz giving the instructions: Three connecting blows or surrender. This is an academic environment, so inflicting any serious injuries will disqualify the offender. As the challenged participant, Bal is given the choice of free battle or staggered by connection: He chooses staggered.

Maura and Burna are both in the crowd watching the fight. After four connections show that both of the students fighting are evenly matched, Riva steps up to Bal during the staggered reset, offering him a cup of water. “I recommend you let him win. Maura‘s not a prize, and if she likes you she won’t care that you lost. Taerrmon’s pride will be satisfied. If you win, he’ll keep coming after you.” Bal narrows his eyes. He can feel the Riva is concerned about him, and this isn’t about her rivalry with Maura. “You too, Miss Lahaan?” She turns her head and just says, “I’ve just offered you advice, Mr. Kalish. Whether you apply it is at your discretion.”

### Chapter 17 (pub. 372-Apr-38)

Riva walks away and the duel continues. Now feeling the weight of three girls all interested in him, Bal struggles to maintain his fluid motion, but his own sense of honor won’t let him give up or throw the fight. “You know, Taerrmon, you can’t choose how other people feel about you.” He almost gets hit by a wooden dart, but brings both of his hands together, focusing his plasma. “If Miss Rashaan isn’t interested, you should try to find someone who actually likes you.” Performing a combat roll to dodge another dart, he pulls his hands apart and tosses two mana bursts with an arced trajectory. When Taerrmon tries to dodge, he gets hit, losing the duel. Elz announces the results and Bal approaches Taerrmon. He offers him a hand to get back up, but Taerrmon glares at him, his lip curled up to reveal his fangs.

“I have no interest in your friendship, Kalish. I will defeat any suitor of Maura Rashaan until she acknowledges my fitness and chooses me for herself.” He pushes himself up and turns away, but pauses. “We Gramurn don’t mate for life like you apes. One season, I will earn her affection. But will she tolerate your ork or dragoness?”

Bal is greeted by Burna, Maura, Riva, and Liyer after leaving the field: They all congratulate him on his victory, especially against the upperclassman, and Burna clasps his hand. “I didn’t expect you were so strong outside of the water. I hope we get a chance to fight together someday.” She lets go of his hand and then turns to Liyer. “Can I pick your brain on the walk home, sunflower?” The scene continues with just Bal, Riv, and Maura.

“Congratulations on your victory. And on earning my curiosity,” Riva says. “So I guess that means you have four girlfriends, doesn’t it?”

### Chapter 18 (pub. 372-May-1)

The chapter begins with a journal entry indicating that it’s been about a month since Bal’s duel with Taerrmon, and Bal has been struggling to juggle the demands of his three girlfriends and Liyer—and that a few other girls have started flirting with him too. He reveals that he and Riva are both on the diveball team, Liyer is hanging out with Burna, Parka, and Bel, and Maura has shot down Taerrmon weekly since the duel. Despite the intense relationships, Bal is happy. The story picks up on a weekend, with Maura and Riva taking him on a joint date, which the narrative notes means they will be competing for his attention fiercely.

The date is to an arcane museum, Bal jokes that Riva must have won the choice of location, and Maura grumbles playfully. As they’re in the museum, they explore the exhibits of magical history, dragons, and then they enter the Romarin exhibit, and the lights go out. They turn towards the exhibit entrance just in time to see the door closing, locking them inside, and they hear the clanking of metal. Maura growls and says the room is too cool to make anything out. Riva takes a deep breath and tells Maura to listen if she can’t see. While there are two pages of almost complete darkness, the characters’ eyes are drawn and dialogue suggests they’re being attacked by armored soldier displays.

Riva lets out a sharp hiss and a fourth pair of eyes appears. “Aren’t you a little short for a Rak’shao trooper?”

### Chapter 19 (pub. 372-May-8)

The scene is illustrated white on black for several pages, repeating the quote from Riva (itself a reference to the _War of Ages_ story) and showing that she is holding the throat of another Hol’tiin who is shorter than herself. The lights return and the young male Hol’tiin hisses out a surrender. Asked why he attacked them, he says he wanted to prove himself to Riva, but clearly his ambush skills are insufficient. He slinks to the floor and apologizes, introducing himself as Neffel. He admits that he has the power to shroud an area in darkness, but he can still see clearly inside of his power. He wants to earn Riva’s respect, and says he’ll keep trying — Bal takes the girls aside and asks if they would mind letting Neffel join them for the rest of the museum, at least. Maura says it would mean Riva has to split her attention, making it easier for Maura to win Bal over. Riva hesitates and explains that she doesn’t want to lead the other boy on when she’s not interested. But she says that as long as he doesn’t try to hit on her, she doesn’t object since they’re already all here and together.

Bal nods. “That’s understandable. He’ll probably follow us around anyway, won’t he?” Neffel _does_ follow them for the rest of the date, but he doesn’t attack again. Riva obsesses over the displays of the Xardon Expedition Team’s equipment for most of the chapter, trying to get Bal to pose with her for Maura to sketch in the interactive display. In the end, both girls leave holding one of Bal’s hands, and Neffel is staring at the trio with a jealous expression.

### Chapter 20 (pub. 372-May-15)

Bal finds three duel challenges in his locker on Monday morning. One is from Taerrmon, demanding a rematch to prove himself worthy of Maura. The second is from Neffel, demanding a one on one battle against Bal. The third is from a Kenyu named Lakora Neliel demanding the privilege of a date if she wins. All three have faculty endorsements and are scheduled for Tuesday after the last class ends.

Bal laments the challenges, but resolves to meet all of them: he genuinely likes Burna, Maura, and Riva, and if that makes him have to fight, they’re all worth it. As he passes by Burna’s locker row, he notices her staring at a letter, and asks her what’s up. She just offers him the letter, but it’s written in Orkish script. “A guy challenging you for a date?”

“Enkidu,” Burna says. “Ragnar Enkidu, and he’s calling out my honor. If I refuse… he’ll say I’m not fit when my seÿlb comes. If I lose, he demands I stop courting you.”

“And you’re…”

Burna shoves the note back into her locker. “I don’t need you to say it, Bal. But I’m glad you know. I need to win this duel. Tomorrow.”

Bal takes a deep breath. “I believe in you, Burna. I know you can win, if you just follow your own heart.”

The chapter skips to lunch, with Liyer meeting him on the roof. “I heard you got three challenges,” she says, opening up a lunch of garlic-fried mushroom risotto with whole cloves. “You’ve really grown since academy started.” She expresses how happy she is to see him starting to rely on her less for emotional stability, and then says she’s glad he got the wrong locker, but she still cares about him. Bal smiles and offers her some iced kafa from an insulated jug. “Even with three girlfriends, you’re still my best friend.” The chapter ends with Liyer smiling in a mysterious expression, only showing her nose, smiling lips, and a hint of her teeth, softly whispering: “Four.”

### Chapter 21 (pub. 372-May-22)

Bal and Gi are wrestling in the greenyard. Gi is teasing Bal about being so popular with the girls, but Bal lets it all slide off of him, getting Gi a bit worked up. “Hey, maybe you’ll be popular too when you catch up to me.” This chapter focuses on a quiet evening with his family and doing his homework — practicing mental focus for his empathic sense and increasing the speed he could focus for his mana bursts. After Gi goes inside, Luko joins him for practice and compliments his improvements in form and conditioning over the last season. He promises to be at the diveball game on Saturday, and then sits down on a bench behind the house. They talk about how hard it is for Bal to balance everything, and Bal admits that the girls aren’t the hard part: the duels are. Luko asks if Bal will choose one, and the chapter cuts off with Bal looking up to the sky, not saying a word.

### Chapter 22 (pub. 372-May-29)

Bal and Burna are both off to the same side of the dueling field. Their combined rivals are seated on the other side, as the four different faculty sponsors sort out the order of the duels. Bal rests his hand on Burna’s clenched fist. “Relax. I know you can handle this.”

Burna clenches her jaw breathes out heavily through her nose. “I’m glad one of us does,” she answers. “I don’t even know what his powers are.”

Miss Mikti approaches the center of the field. She holds up a projection stone to her mouth and says simply: “Kalish versus Loshre. Bal Kalish and Neffel Loshre, please come to the center of the field. Kalish will choose the manner of this duel.” Bal chooses an all-out battle, assuming that Neffel’s powers will make it more difficult to judge individual connections, and offers a respectful handshake to Neffel, who hisses while returning the gesture.

The field is quickly enveloped in darkness, though in this battle the art only uses heavy halftone masking to indicate the darkness. Neffel, however, is treated as being invisible except when attacking or being harmed. Bal, however, relies on his empathy to narrowly dodge key blows, manipulating the confidence of his opponent and allowing several blows through on purpose. When he turns the tide, he delivers two strong counterstrikes to Neffel, eventually landing a throw similar to a Gaean judo throw, and pins Neffel. “I know how you feel about Riva,” he says. “But she’s not just a prize to win. Maybe you should try to befriend her first.”

Neffel hisses, but yields, dropping the field of darkness and calling out his defeat. Unlike Taerrmon in the previous duel, Neffel accepts Bal’s hand to get back up, but he doesn’t offer any words. The chapter ends with the Gramurn coach of the cross team standing in the center of the field. “Enkidu versus Detterk.”

### Chapter 23 (pub. 372-May-36)

“Enkidu versus Detterk. Ragnar Enkidu and Burna Detterk. Detterk will chose the fashion of the battle.” As their paths cross as they move across the field, Bal and Burna offer each other a deep glance: Bal confident but winded, Burna uncharacteristically uncertain, but proud. Burna chooses to duel without powers, staggered by connection. Ragnar is a big, beefy, masculine Ork, with bushy hair and two unevenly sized swords at his hips.

Burna takes up a worgulbÿr. She assesses her chances as Coach Garon holds the flag between them. Neither duelist shows a sign of respect, and the coach raises the flag, starting their duel. Ragnar moves quickly and precisely with his two mismatched swords, a style of swords known as _daz magul_ in Orkish. Burna holds him off with great effort, deflecting his blades with her worgulbÿr, but ultimately suffers the first connection. She looks to the sideline and remembers what Bal said as she prepares for the second movement. Bal has faith in her: and she adopts a more nimble, evasive style, striking the butt of her Worgulbyr into Ragnar’s chin in the final panel.

### Chapter 24 (pub. 372-May-43)

Ragnar grunts but is thrown off his balance and falls to the ground. Burna prepares for the third movement. Ragnar broadens his stance. He begins to swing wildly but with great force: Burna finds it difficult to get in close enough to use her worgulbÿr as a staff, but still has the staff-like grip. Ragnar taunts her, claiming that he deserves her attention more than the alien. Burna can barely sustain her defenses and her mood at the taunts, but lets out a roar as she tries to turn the crooked bident end into a polearm. Ragnar drops one sword and catches her by the arm, twisting it up and holding her lightly elevated until Coach Garon calls the connection.

Burna resolves to use her worgulbÿr as a spear now. She shifts her stance and battle style for the fourth movement. Using only the larger heavy blade from his set, Ragnar parries and blocks her attacks, but her aggressive stance makes it difficult for him to take the advantage. He is quickly hit for the fourth connection — a draw with only one movement left to decide the winner. A bolt of lightning crosses the sky as Garon holds the flag between them.

### Chapter 25 (pub. 372-Jun-06)

“It sounds like rain, sweet fireblood,” Ragnar says, smirking. “And I know your moves now.” The flag rises and Ragnar moves quickly, knocking the worgulbÿr from her hands and attacking relentlessly — but still missing consistently. Burna recognizes that he’s not trying to hit her, only to humiliate her. Once she’s broken he’ll claim the connection… and she hears the sound of rain starting.

Taking a sharp breath, she resolves to win no matter what. If she’s got to be reckless, she will, but she won’t break her promise: She’s going to be worthy of Bal’s confession even though it was an accident! She starts to dodge and counter, almost dancing, and starts to smile as she turns the tide, getting Ragnar off balance. Crouching down, rolling, and rising into an uppercut, she shouts her defiance at the top of her lungs: “I LOVE BAL KALISH!” The uppercut lands, but so does Ragnar’s blade on her shoulder. 

The duel is declared a victory for Burna. Ragnar is bitter, but honorable: he assures her that he won’t challenge her honor. The next teacher comes up, a Kenyu woman who hasn’t been introduced before. “Kalish versus Neliel.”

### Chapter 26 (pub. 372-Jun-13)

Burna holds her scratched shoulder as she walks past Bal. “I meant it,” she says softly as they pass. Bal smiles and nods. “You’re worth it,” he replies. They continue to walk and Bal reaches the center of the field. Bal is given the choice of format. He chooses staggered by contact. Lakora reaches the center of the field and flashes a wide, wild grin at Bal. “Long time no see, bestie. You have a promise to keep to me.”

Bal’s eyes narrow in shock. While we don’t learn much, he acknowledges her: “Koko. If I promised you something, you don’t need to fight me for it.”

“It’s been 4 years, Babal!” She strikes a wide aggressive stance and a flash of lightning strikes behind her. “I was supposed to be your first girlfriend!”

The fight is sharp and decisive: Bal can’t keep up with her inhumanly fast movements or lightning strikes. Lakora wins and smirks. “I won’t make you dump anybody, Babal, I just want a proper date with the boy I promised myself to in second year.”

Bal nods and accepts Lakora’s hand to stand up. We find out that four years ago, they were in the same neighborhood school (along with Liyer) and one summer afternoon, Bal had told Lakora that whenever he was big enough to go on dates, he wanted to date both of them. As the flashback scene ends, Bal sighs. “I don’t know if I meant that as a promise, but if you took it as one, I owe you, Koko. And that was a great fight.”

Lakora grins and then narrows her eyes: the chapter ends showing Maura marching to the middle of the dueling field.

### Chapter 27 (pub. 372-Jun-20)

Maura stands in front of Mr. Elz. She demands to substitute for Bal in the final duel and simply states that she has the right of choice in courtship, so dueling over _her_ choice is illegitimate. Mr. Elz thinks for a moment as Taerrmon approaches. “I will have to offer that choice to the challenger,” Elz says. Taerrmon accepts readily. Mr. Elz gives Bal and Lakora time to leave the field while Maura chooses the battle format.

Elz announces the battle. “Substituting for Kalish, Rashaan. Rashaan versus Mi’arlaen. Rashaan has chosen a continuous battle. Duelists to your positions.”

The duel begins and Maura raises a clump of dirt from the field, dancing to manipulate it like a shield. Taerrmon manifests three wooden darts, dancing in a similar fashion to manipulate their movement, but taking the offensive stance. The two appear to be evenly matched in skill, blocking and dodging each other until Taermon challenges her: “I don’t know which of you is cuter to think of as the pet.” The words break Maura’s calm—as she tries to strike back, she misses one of Taerrmon’s darts, taking a distinct sideswipe to her left arm. Finding her left arm difficult to move without pain, she drops to a low stance and then rises along with raising her right hand, lifting a pillar of earth around Taerrmon’s feet.

She quickly reshapes her shield clump into something like a whip, lashing at Taerrmon, and takes another hit, now to her right leg, but holds her focus, wrapping Taerrmon in a restraining coat of earth. Unable to move, Taerrmon’s power loses precision control over his darts. Maura lets out a howl and leaps towards Taerrmon, stumbling from the pain in her right leg. 

As she lies on the ground, one of Taerrmon’s darts lands in the ground by her face. A sharp whistle sounds and Elz declares the duel over: Neither duelist is fit to continue the battle, so it ends in a draw.
