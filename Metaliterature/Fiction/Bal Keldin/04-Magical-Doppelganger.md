# Magical Doppelganger: Bal Keldin

The third iteration of Bal as a character was produced by a young Kenyu adult named Baeron Lassalen, who wanted to write a story for children that didn’t talk down to them. The story ran for four years with 300 chapters in total.

## Synopsis

### Introduction

Bal and Liyer are teenaged siblings working as lab assistants and apprentices to the magitech engineer Professor Luko. They are working on a new device designed to focus plasma[^1] more efficiently when the lab is suddenly attacked by Burmon (translation: Slag), a superman who wields power over the element of fire. Grabbing the prototype device, Bal attempts to protect Liyer and Luko from the superman by focusing a mana burst from his left hand. He is easily swatted aside and injured.

When he wakes up, he is in a hospital, and told that he’s been gravely injured. Professor Luko reveals that Burmon was a superman he had helped to empower with his former associate, Rák Shao, but Profesor Rák had parted ways with him several years ago. Burmon stole the latest research on the Superman procedure -- but Luko knows it well enough to replace the stolen data. More importantly, Luko is upset that Bal was injured trying to protect him, and offers to give him superman powers: There’s no guarantee of what powers Bal will receive, but he will be stronger, faster, and will even recover more quickly from injuries. Bal accepts without hesitation, then closes his eyes to sleep.

Bal is wearing a cast on his left arm and right leg. Liyer is pushing him to the lab in a wheelchair, and they’re talking about the procedure Bal agreed to. He says he wants to protect everyone, and Liyer says she wishes he wouldn’t be so reckless. She wonders if she should become a superman too, just to keep up with him, and he just laughs. He explains: “It’s a guy’s job to protect his family, y’know.”

Bal is strapped into an infusion chamber. Professor Luko explains that the procedure will be like going to sleep, and waking up like a butterfly emerging from a cocoon. He asks one last time if Bal is sure about this, saying that it can have negative effects on the body and mind, and after receiving the “Yes, I’m sure,” from Bal, he begins filling the tank with liquified mana.

Bal wakes up with a hexagonal crest on his bare chest. He is still in the casts, but he says he feels clearheaded and pain free. He asks what his powers are, and the Professor says he isn’t sure yet: “They don’t seem to have affected your appearance.” Liyer comes into the room and offers Bal something to eat. When their hands touch, Bal’s eyes go wide and his crest changes to heart shape.

“You didn’t!”

“It’s a sister’s duty to look after her family too,” Liyer responds.

Luko raises an eyebrow. “Fascinating!” He suggests that Bal has the power to “doppel” or copy the powers of other supermen, and confirms that Liyer insisted that if Bal was going through the treatment, she would too. Because she wasn’t injured, she recovered earlier, and her power is the ability to infuse mana into things. Bal tests the new power by infusing mana into his drink, giving him a burst of energy and a claim that he can feel his arm and leg healing.

### Main Storyline

After finishing his recovery and getting a feel for his powers, Bal dons a suit of magitech armor designed by Professor Luko. The armor is made up of azure and cyan materials, a cyan flexible chain mesh underlayer and an azure sturdy breastplate, boots, and gauntlets incorporating the plasma focuser from earlier, along with an armored athletic headguard.[^2]

Professor Luko says he and Professor Rák created eight supermen together. Burmon (Slag), Elzjonk (Glacier), Migdul (Mightier), Chamtax (Exploder), Anesh (Lightning), Tembar (Timer), and Alborén (Tar-setter) were created and trained to help serve utility functions in city management and research. All of them had been doing their own jobs very well for several years but they all went missing about a year ago. Burmon’s attack has Luko worried that they’ve all gone rogue, or worse. But he doesn’t have any leads.

Professor Luko asks Bal to go out and help people until there’s another attack--to become known to the people nearby. Bal proceeds to engage in a lot of small helpful heroic activities for several chapters, getting to know the people of the area better, until a sudden, unpredicted lightning storm rolls in quickly, directed by the superman Anesh. As the power in the neighborhood fails, Bal asks Liyer to lend him her power, and he stands off with his first superman opponent. When he defeats Anesh after a difficult battle, he also comes out knowing the scope of the threat: Professor Rák Shao has convinced the supermen that they deserve to be rulers of the world, not servants of society, and this city is just the first stepping stone.

The series follows a similar rhythm throughout its run, often dealing with several slice of life heroic chapters between the arrival, or pursuit, or other Supermen. Eventually, another superman arrives in a dark grey outfit with bright red trims and two swords in his belt. He defeats Tembar (the fourth enemy) when Bal is unable to do so, then introduces himself as Enkidu, a “free agent” superman. When Bal tries to talk to him, he flees.

Over the course of the series, Bal develops the ability to doppel multiple powers and even synthesize powers together when he has more than one. New supermen start appearing after the initial eight are all defeated and Professor Rák’s fortress is destroyed -- but Rák Shao (who stops using the Professor title after his first defeat) himself always seems to escape justice. Over time, Bal is able to convince some of the other supermen to return to the side of society, but Rák Shao has the research that allows him to make more supermen.

The conflict eventually peaks when Rák Shao sends an army of 100 supermen to attack the City all at once, establishing the Central Tower as his fortress.

### Finale

Borrowing the powers of Liyer, Enkidu (who is as opposed to Rák Shao as Bal, but also distrusts Luko), and several other supermen who have become friends or allies over the series, Bal rushes the Central Tower while trusting his allies (and Enkidu) to fight the supermen throughout the rest of the city. Using a teleportation beacon near the floor, he returns to Luko who has been developing a synthetic superpower for Bal that might end the war -- but at a severe cost:

The Super Splitter power can, in theory, send its targets to a reality where they can’t hurt anybody except themselves, but he can’t promise that it will be properly tuned to only affect the supermen who are loyal to Rák Shao. It could send _every_ superman to the new reality, including their allies. He asks Bal not to use it as long as there is any hope for a peaceful resolution with Rák Shao, because he couldn’t imagine going on without Bal and Liyer.

Rák Shao is beyond negotiation even after pushed to utter defeat. Insisting that the only way to stop him is to kill him, he forces Bal to see the battles happening throughout the city, where the heroes who work with Bal are all being overwhelmed by the sheer numbers of his army. Finally, Bal stares at a display showing Liyer being stepped on and lets out a heavy sigh. “You’re wrong, there is one other solution.” He activates the Super Splitter, his crest transforming into the sharp angular classic crest of Bal Keldin, a mirrored dual “K” that makes the crest resemble a shattered crystal. “For one or both of us, this is goodbye.”

He raises both hands to the sky and focuses all of his energy to create an entirely new world, a world where Rak’shao and the Supermen who aligned with him can rule, while keeping the original world for humans and Supermen who align with humanity. The final page of Chapter 225 is a striking image of a utopian future city with the bold text: “To a Brighter Future”

### Continuations

There were no continuations of this story until A.A. 371 when an anonymous circle calling themselves Team Enkidu published _\~ETERNAL\~ Enkidu Keldin_ beginning in November 371.

The side story _Lone Wolf: Enkidu Keldin_ by Baeron Lassalen debuted in April 372 retelling parts of _Magical Doppelganger_ from the perspective of Enkidu. _Lone Wolf_ was directly followed up by _Lost in Time: Enkidu Keldin_ in October 372. _Lost in Time_ is still running as of August 373, with a second year planned for that story.

## Footnotes

[^1]: In Atrusian cultures, _plasma_ is the magical energy of living beings, which interacts with _mana_ in the environment to produce magic. It is not the same thing as blood plasma.

[^2]: This headguard is a common aesthetic choice for helmets in meten, as it allows the character’s hair to remain exposed to help keep that aspect of their visual design apparent.
