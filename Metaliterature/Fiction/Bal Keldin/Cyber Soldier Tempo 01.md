title: Cyber Soldier Tempo 01
---

Deep gray and red, the colors of struggle and bloodshed, were his trademark, and he was definitely failing in this struggle. The man was surrounded by silvery pillars of light which marked the boundary of the battlefield. “Damn it,” he said, leaping as a female shape attacked him. The fight was close, but he had already used up all of his special techniques. All he had left was his cannon arm. “Timed Burst\!” He pivoted as he called the attack and took aim at the enemy. Her body was dangerously close to the edge of the ring after her slash had failed, and all he needed to do was knock her out.  

A buzzer sounded just as the cannon arm’s shot was let out. The battle was over, and he had lost again. “Lyre, you did a great job.”  

“You should have won,” she said, walking over to him. “You know, we’ve never met face to face, but you look so handsome with this avatar. Maybe we should meet offline someday, Tempo.”  

“Maybe,” he said. “Mail me about it and we’ll see. I’m logging off now.”  

“Well you be good. And we’ll definitely e-mail you, so you just watch that mailbox for us.”  

\>\>Net Avatar, security mode deactivating. Prepare for Avatar disconnection in five, four, three, two… \<\<  

Removing the helmet, he looked at the computer screen. Tempo was there, the avatar program was being scanned for damage while the headset made sure no damage had been suffered to him during the connection. “Enter password for Bal Felorn.”  

“Bal Felorn, net hunter child.” The computer processed the password and then disconnected from the internet, allowing Bal to get up and find his dinner. Papa was working late at the Administration for Net Security, and mama was in the hospital with a broken leg. For a while more, Bal had to take care of himself.

---

Dinner was over. Dogart, the butcher, had dropped by with bacon for the morning, and Bal was just relaxing before he went to bed. “Practice for Dive tomorrow… I’m not going to have much time for studying. I may not even be able to meet Lyre online tomorrow.”  

He picked up the mail and looked through it, hoping to spot something interesting he might have missed earlier. There was nothing except… He paused as he caught his breath, flipping back a few packages… It was there. The red envelope was from the ANS, and it was addressed to him. He opened it and read through the letter before opening the package that it contained.

> Bal:
> 
> I know you have a busy schedule these days, between Dive-Ball and your schoolwork, so I thought you might appreciate this prototype we’ve been developing. The disc that I sent with this is an AI and Armor program for Tempo, so he can become more than simply an Avatar. We’re also developing portable units to hold the AI-endowed avatars, but until then, this should help you plenty.
> 
> Because of our current line of research, I also won’t be home for at least a month. Remember, this AI is a prototype, and so it may act unpredictably. Keep a backup of the Tempo avatar in case that happens, and I’ll keep up with you through e-mail. Remember, you are the first person to get this, and I can’t afford you telling people. I wasn’t even supposed to send this out to you, so keep this as our little secret.
> 
> \>\> Roman Kieres, Doctor of Programming \<\<

His own father had written the note, and the program… He grabbed the package, a square package like a disc case. Obviously it was a disc case, and he ran up to his computer. Loading it up, he opened the package and slid in the disc. “Run setup program,” he said, scanning the discs contents on his screen. “Install intelligence program, battle upgrades. Run and project time.”  
His computer was audibly processing the commands, beginning the install and estimating the install time. “Install projection complete,” it droned. “Four hours to completion of install and updates.”

---

“Tomorrow is the first big game of the season,” Coach said, looking squarely at Bal. “We can’t afford for any of you to miss the game against Sunbridge Girl’s Academy. They had the second best team in the region last season, and we were almost at the bottom. Kieres, I’m talking to you\!”  

“Wha? Oh, sorry coach,” he said, blushing. “I was just thinking about this girl I met the other day.”  

“Well you better hope those thoughts don’t interfere with your game tomorrow,” Coach said, slapping Bal. “Come on, suit up, we’re going to try some formations practice.”  

“Alright,” Bal said, sighing. He headed over to his locker and took off his shirt, changing into the weighted shirt he used for training. Swapping his pants for the loose shorts of the Dive field and then his shoes for the tight and smooth shoes that were meant for the field, he closed his eyes. Leah had sent him an e-mail, including a picture of herself. She’d been a bit mysterious about where in town she lived, and Bal had been watching for her in school all day. He slipped on the goggles, keeping the lenses over his forehead, and looked at the uniform hanging in the locker still. Blue and gray were the team colors for the Gryphons, and he still had the red headband that indicated he was the captain. He grabbed it and tied it around his right ankle.  

A shrill whistle called him back to reality, and he ran out to the pool for practice. “All right, coach, I’m here.”

---

A dozen attempts for a dozen formations seemed to have been coach’s goal, but when sundown came the kids couldn’t be happier. “Still captain, huh?”  

“Of course, Gi, I am the star player and the quickest planner.”  

“Yeah, well let’s hope those girls tomorrow don’t take any of your planning away from the game,” another guy said, rubbing Gi’s shoulders. “This was sure a rough practice though, you really think Coach thinks we have a chance?”  

“Of course we have a chance,” Bal said, smoothing out his half-dry hair. “You’ve got me as captain, and I’ve got all of you as my team. The only problem is if they get the ball.”  

“Aw, cut it out, Tesh,” Gi said, turning to his friend. “I don’t need my muscles relaxed that much, need to be in good form for the game.”  

“We all need to be in good form, Gi,” a fourth said. “The way you acted you’d think he was forcing himself on you.”  

“Whoa, Fel, Geyir, keep it calm,” Bal said, slipping his normal shirt on. “Look, we’re going to play to win tomorrow, and if we lose, we will do twelve perfect runs on all twelve formations coach set us to today, alright.”  

“No need to threaten us,” said the last, quiet boy, his black hair was slicked back but still somewhat untamed from practice. “We’ll do our best, and if we lose, we lose. This is the best team in the region, Bal.”  

“Well, I’m going to win to prove myself to my girl,” Bal said. “I don’t care about the rest of you, but we are not playing for our best. We are playing to win.”

---

“Sir.” The room was completely dark, except for some slight light, directly around a throne. “I have managed to intercept the location of the Avatar Intelligence program. I can retrieve it before anyone will know anything, and we can begin our plan.”  

“That will be excellent,” the man on the throne replied. “Towar… deliver the program to Towar, he will know how to handle it. Then bring it here, to me. Terror Net will conquer the networks of the world by use of the new Avint software.”  

“As you wish, Lord Enkura.” There was a rustling sound, and then the sound of a door closing. Enkura simply smiled a he looked at the list of operatives he had in place. Only one had failed to report in, however it was only thanks to him that the Avatar Intelligence program had even been known of by Enkura. Even if the Avint software was incomplete… he could refine it. He wasn’t considered the genius of programming for nothing, after all. “Towar, prepare yourself for a delivery. I want you to install the Avatar intelligence program off the disc that Tren is bringing you, and then make certain he gets the disc back so I can receive it. Tomorrow, I want you to attack the opening game of the Dive-ball season, between the Stone Valley Gryphons and the Sunbridge Academy Mares. Are you clear?”  

“As you command, Lord Enkura, it shall be done. Flow will disrupt the game at my command.”  

“Excellent. Make no mistakes, Towar.”

---

“I’m going to be at the Dive-ball game tomorrow,” Tempo said, looking at Lyre. “Will you be there?”  

“Of course I’ll be there,” she said, laughing. Her red gauntlet rested on his right hand, and she looked over at him. “How will I be able to recognize you?”  

“I’ll be wearing a red headband,” he said, smiling. “And I’ve got your picture, so I’ll be able to see you.”  

“All right. After the game we can get together for something to eat, then.”  

“All right. I’ll see you then.”  

“Okay,” she said, smiling. “Ah, it’s getting late. I need to leave for dinner. Tomorrow, Tempo.”  

“Bal\! My name is Bal\!”  

He stayed in the network for a while, looking up information for his history paper and anything he could find about the Sunbridge Girls Academy team, not that there was much to find. “Bal, I think you should log off now.”  

Bal turned, surprised at the voice. “Who’s there?”  

“It’s just me, Tempo… I haven’t had any need to talk until now, Bal.”  

“Well I didn’t ask for your advice. Look, I just need some more info for this history paper, then I’ll log off.”  

“I can find that for you,” Tempo said, the voice hollow, echoing in Bal’s head.  

“Then you can write my report too, right?”  

“Don’t push your luck,” Tempo said, a smirking tone in his voice. “I’ll find the information, but you have to write the paper yourself.”  

\>\> Security Mode deactivating. Disconnecting. \<\< Bal saw flashing lights and blinked. He was still in the Net world, but Tempo was standing in front of him. “Tempo, come on\! I just need a few more minutes\!”  

“Disable helmet connection,” Tempo said, giving the voice command. “Log off user Bal Felorn, net hunter child.” He looked back at Bal. “I’ll have the information by the time you finish dinner.”

---

Bal swerved left, caught the ball from Gi’s pass, and dove to dodge the red-haired girl coming for him. He felt his foot slide through her grasp and he pushed, rising and passing to Tesh. Tesh caught it as the girls seemed to suddenly converge on Bal, he expected to hear a penalty buzzer, but it didn’t come. Their arms were all around him, and all he wanted to do was find Leah.  

He tried to look past the girls team, but it was useless, his face was between the breasts of one of them and they were feeling him all over. He gasped, then blinked. He could breathe? That couldn’t be right… but he couldn’t seem to think straight at the moment either.  

Suddenly the green-haired girl who had been smothering him with her breasts moved and he saw her. Leah was in the stands, cheering him on\! Pulling himself together, he broke free of the girls and headed for the goal, spinning his body to catch the ball as it passed above him, and then releasing the ball and giving it a solid head-butt into the goal, the dark haired goal-keeper missing the block by a mile.  

The buzzer sounded, but it seemed dryer than usual. “BAL\! YOU NEED TO WAKE UP, BAL\!”  

Bal awoke with a jerk and turned to look around. “Tempo? Where are you?”  

“In the alarm clock system,” came the response. “I’m going to log on and head to the Dive-ball systems, I’ll be there to watch your game. Your dad wants me to send progress reports on my functions and your progress.”  

“Damn it all,” Bal said under his breath as he looked down at the stain on his bed-sheets. “All right Tempo. But remember, you’re not supposed to exist, so keep things quiet in the network.”  

“Roger,” Tempo said, his voice shifting in location. “You’ve only got half an hour until game time, so hurry it up.” This time the tone shifted, to match Lyre’s voice, “I’ll see you after the game.”  

“No fair teasing me,” Bal said, jumping up and looking at the computer. But Tempo had already logged into the networks, and couldn’t hear him. Bal quickly threw on his clothes and grabbed some bacon, stuck it between a few slices of bread and cheese, and ran out of the house. “Damn it, if only Tempo had woken me up earlier I wouldn’t be getting to the arena out of breath and without time to eat breakfast\!”  

He watched the town clock as best he could while he ran towards the Arena, taking every shortcut he knew. He made it just in time, as the locker room gates were closing for preparation. There were still fifteen minutes until the game, plenty of time to catch his breath and suit up.  

“Thought you weren’t gonna make it,” Tesh said, watching Bal move towards his locker. “We were afraid Gi would have to take your place as captain today.”  

“Hah, I can’t be late today,” Bal said, ducking under Gi’s arm. “I’ve got a girl to impress\! He took the uniform, making certain to avoid the temptation to use his weighted shirt underneath it. The blue sleeves on the gray shirt seemed odd, all of a sudden, but he knew it was necessary for protection and identifying the team. What colors did the opponents have? The shorts had a black trim and the team emblem on them in black. The shoes and headband followed, the headband firmly secured around his forehead, his bangs all the more pronounced hanging in front of it.  

Goggles in hand, he went to the arena door, where Coach was watching the field in shock. “What’s up, coach?”  

“The field… the lights are flashing, and the water is boiling away. We can’t play in there like that, Bal.”  

“But they know we have a game,” he said, looking over. “We should go talk to the maintenance staff.”  

“I already sent a second-stringer for that, Bal, you need to stay right here.”  

An image flashed on the display panel across the field from them. “I am Flow,” the figure said, a male figure with heavy armor on. Somehow he seemed a bit unreal, though. “Terror Net will not condone the senseless energy expenditure of you humans in ‘sport’. And so I, an Intelligent Avatar, have been sent to stop this waste. Your energies will be better spent on the coming war.”  

“Intelligent Avatar?” Coach shook his head. “There’s no such thing, Avatars need an operator to be useful.”  

“No, the ANS is working on a system to make Intelligent Avatars,” Bal said. “Papa sent me a letter about it the other night. He called that kind of an Avatar an… ‘Avint’, for Avatar with Intelligence.”  

“Da… Bal, you mean this is an ANS agent?”  

“No way,” Gi said, looking up. “That’s a Terror Net Avatar… He’s been trying to bully people into joining Terror Net for a while…”  

“He can’t be an Avint,” Bal said. “The project is still in prototype, and… Papa said they’ve been trying to work out the bugs for months. And he said I’d be the first person to get an Avint, to make up for him always being gone.”  

“Wow,” Tesh said, looking at the field. “That Flow tried to push me into joining Terror Net the other night. Me and H-X taught him not to mess with a Dive player, though.”  

“I need to talk to papa… I need to find a terminal station.”  

“Up in the announcer’s booth,” Coach said. “And tell him to have the ANS send help.”

---

“I knew it was too dangerous,” Roman said, watching as the systems reported the failure to respond of the Dive Arena’s systems. “A call? Hello, Net Safety Administration.”  

“Papa, it’s horrible\! Apparently these goons called Terror Net…”  

“I know about the situation, and we’re working to fix the situation as quickly as possible, Bal. Terror Net apparently has developed their own Avint system.”  

“No, I just had Tempo check the drives at home, they stole the one you sent me\!” Bal seemed terrified of the punishment he’d receive for being so careless, but was shocked at his father’s response.  

“Tempo… Right\! I’m authorizing Tempo as a Cyber Soldier Avatar, he’ll be allowed to battle Terror Net Avatars and Avints without interference from the government… But remember, he’s only a prototype. Don’t push him too hard.”  

“Roger,” Bal said, forming a V with his hands. “Tempo and me are on the job\!”  

“I heard that, and Flow seems a bit too dangerous… Bal\!”  

“Tempo?” Roman was shocked to hear the Avint being so reliable. “Alright, Tempo, I’m sending you an Electrical shotgun weapon to fight him with, but be careful. The program can only be fired four times.”  

“We only need one shot,” Bal said, watching the display list the data program promised. “Keep in touch, papa\!”  

“I’m ready Bal, but I’m worried… what if the Avint software malfunctions?”  

“It won’t malfunction, and I won’t lose this fight or the game today,” he said to the Avint. “Cyber Soldier Tempo, load weapon program, ThunderShot\! Activate Armor units, and prepare for combat\!”  

“Loading ThuderShot,” Tempo reported, his right gauntlet appearing and shifting to a dirty yellow shade. “Activating Armor units, J-Boots, Special Arm. Activating Software compression and backup, Helmet online… Backup to system memory complete. Alright, Bal, let’s go\!”

---

A wicked laugh was the sound of Flow’s voice in the Net world, as he threatened the maintenance software into doing whatever he wanted. “Come on, make the arena even hotter,” he said, striking one of the programs into the firewall of the system. “Faster, you need to listen to my orders and follow them now, not in a minute\!”  

“Stop what you’re doing,” Tempo shouted, launching a basic shot from his cannon arm. “I’m a proper Avint, and I won’t fight dirty like you, Flow. Can you stand a challenge from a Cyber Soldier Avint?”  

“Cyber Soldier Avint? You don’t know what you’re saying, little Avatar. A human mind will never compare to my strength\!” Flow turned to face Tempo, the gray suited Avint wearing blood red armor. “I wouldn’t risk your deletion by fighting me, human.”  

“I’m not a human,” Tempo said, running at his taller and bulkier foe, delivering a punch that sent Flow into the firewall. “It’s like I told you, I am an Avint as much as you. Tell me why you’re here\!”  

“To destroy human recreation… a superior being such as you should realize the fruitlessness of such activities as these ‘sports’. All that matters is working hard, and working for TerrorNet lets me do the best work I can.”  

“Sports are what give humans the drive to perform to their best,” Tempo said, narrowing his eyes. “Without recreation, human energy would burn out too quickly to produce anything, and they would not have dreams to push the limits, to do the best they can\!”  

“I see it’s pointless to talk to a human mind,” Flow said, disappearing for a brief instant, then reappearing behind Tempo. “Water Pressure\!”  

Tempo screamed at contact from the attack, only activating the jets in his boots before the firewall. “You play dirty, attacking from behind like that\! But a true warrior can still beat your tricks\!” Landing on the ground, he charged again, and met Flow’s foot.  

“Dirty? Humans pollute water when they make it a field for sport, and you call me ‘dirty’\!?” Flow spun too rapidly for Tempo to dodge the kick, and Tempo flew into a software barrier.  

“Tempo, charge and use the ThunderShot as soon as he stops you,” Bal said, looking down at the fight. “Do you think it’ll work?”  

“For now, at least,” Tempo said, holding his gut. “But he’s strong… and fast. If it doesn’t work, we don’t have a second chance.”  

“It’ll work, then,” Bal said, nodding. “Go, Tempo, take him down\!”

---

“Isn’t that Bal?” Geyir said, looking up at the screen. “How could he be plugged into the net, though?”  

“He must be using a remote program to operate Tempo,” Fel said, watching as Flow grabbed Tempo by the face. “This is a shame to watch, our star player being beaten by a net bully\!”  

A flash of light filled the room, the announcer’s cabin, and both of the locker rooms. Nobody was spared as Tempo unleashed the ThunderShot in four rapid shots.  

“I can’t believe it,” Flow screamed, his vice metallic and charged with pain. “To lose to a human mind? This won’t be forgotten, humans\! We, the operatives of Terror Net will be back, and we will not fail\!”  

The brilliance faded, Tempo sighed, and Flow was gone. “All right you programs, get back to your real jobs, there’s a game that should’ve started ten minutes ago.”  

They chirped a binary ‘Thank you’ and went to work immediately, setting the systems to cool the water enough to start the game as soon as possible.

---

It was half an hour late, but the game was about to start, and Bal couldn’t see Leah anywhere in the audience. Then again, he wouldn’t have been able to spot his parents in the audience, so big was the turnout today.  

“And now, the Stone Valley Gryphons versus the Sunbridge Girls Academy Mares, each team will be led out by its captain, for pre-game formalities.”  

“Her,” Bal muttered, watching as Leah herself led the Mares onto the field. He could see her muttering the same curse, that he was the captain of the Gryphons, as if she didn’t know the meaning of a red headband on a Dive-ball field. “So you didn’t know that I was the captain, Leah?”  

“I thought you might be on another team, Bal,” she said, smiling. “But just because I like you, don’t think my team will play any easier against you.”  

“I wouldn’t respect you if you did,” Bal said. “And no hard feelings, but I have to impress you by winning this game.”  

“Yeah, you try that.”  

“And you do your best too, Leah. Ready?”  

“Mares, take the field, Formation Alpha, variant 3\!”  

“Gryphons, defensive Formation Beta, let’s learn their moves first.”

---

The start buzzer sounded and Bal was staring down Leah, the star player of one of the worst teams against the captain of one of the best. The ball flew into the field, and Bal pushed himself firmly up to catch it, just keeping it from Leah. Rolling backwards, he passed it to his best offensive player, Fel, and followed through with a tackle on Leah, to slow down their team.  

Fel was keeping her own as Tesh and Geyir stuck around her, holding off the defenders, but once a fast enough girl got past them it was over. They tried to correct their mistake by catching the girl’s tail, but she hurled the ball towards another girl, who had slipped right past the defense, and she sent a shot into the center of the Gryphons’ goal, scoring the first point within a minute of play.  

The Gryphons had the ball now, and instead of giving it to offense, Bal sent it to Geyir, who pushed through the opposing team, crossing most of the field. Geyir handed it off to Fel who passed it to Bal. Rising to avoid incoming blocks, he pulled his arm back and unleashed his secret weapon, the Timed Burst: two hand thrusts and a kick, meant to give the ball optimum momentum as it sped into the goal. Another thirty seconds to the first break, at three minutes. With three periods of three minutes and a two minute break between each period, the game was going to be a good one.  

As the buzzer sounded for a break, Leah was about to use a ball tackle to get past Bal, but instead they both broke surface together. “You put on a good game, for such a low-ranked team.”  

“We’re only warming up, Leah. We spent the first period learning your strategies, not putting on an offensive show.”  

“Well I know your techniques plenty well, Bal, so don’t expect to get any more points against us.”  

Taking the chance to breathe for a few minutes, Bal also considered changing the lineup. But Leah would do the same thing, to alter her team’s strategies… except those couldn’t work unless every player was able to take whatever role was needed. He knew the Gryphons weren’t up for that, but he had to think of something.  

“All right, I had a new idea for a play. Kuna, I want you to take the goal, you’re the best at catching the ball. When you get it, pass it to one of the defenders, and when they get it, they’ll take it to the end. Once there, Fel or me will be waiting to make a shot. But switch it up. Pass it when you need to, send it to Fel if I’m too close to their goal, and to me if Fel is. We can win this, guys\!”  

“I’m with you,” Gi said, smiling. “Besides, we do need more than one good goal-keeper, huh?”  

“We’ve got it,” Tesh, Geyir, and a girl said. “Give the Mares the run-around, right?”  

“If they have no problem, I’m in,” Fel said, rubbing her shoulders. “Are we set, then?”  

“I hope so.”  

“Hey, where’s that girl you’re trying to impress,” Geyir said, nudging his friend in the ribs. “I bet she’s sitting in the front row, huh?”  

“Oh, she’s got one of the best seats in the house all right,” Bal said, not willing to tell the team that his new girlfriend was the captain on the opposing team. “But to be honest, we’ve only met on the net before today, so I can’t say what she looks like.”

---

For the next ten minutes, the game was all that was on anybody’s mind, and with a stalemate from the beginning of the second period, it seemed like there wasn’t going to be a winner, until Bal signaled a shocking move, pulling his entire team to offense, abandoning the goal, and finally scoring with only a handful of seconds left on the clock.  

“That play was almost impressive,” Leah said, grabbing Bal’s arm. “But don’t think your skills on the field are going to win me over, alright? Because I promise you, they won’t.”  

Unmoved by her words, Bal just laughed and looked over at her. “Well, I guess now would be as good a time as any for that date, then, huh?”  

“Who said anything about a date,” she shot back, her palm landing flat on his chest and pushing him back. “I’ll call you later, though.”  

Wincing from the force she’d managed to put into the shove, Bal couldn’t help but grin as he turned towards the locker room. “At least she didn’t say ‘No.’”
