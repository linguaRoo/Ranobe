# Keldin Adventures: Bal the Mercenary

The fourth iteration of Bal as a character was produced by Baeron Lassalen, and began running during the third year of _Magical Doppelganger_. Lassalen wanted to write a story for adolescents that didn’t talk down to them, specifically for the fans of _Magical Doppelganger_ who felt they were growing out of the more childish tone.

According to Lassalen, the story is set across the steppes of Xardon (this is made explicit during the second storyline), though it does not feature any real world settlements. Bal in this story is depicted ambiguously as either a Kenyu or Adin with a delicate nose, his ears are inconsistent, either rounded or pointed, and his jawline often fluctuates between the two species’ shapes.

## Synopsis

### Introduction

A hero wearing grey padded armor and a red duster jacket is deep in a cavern, standing before a magitech door. He uses a crystal to jump the mechanism, but the door gets stuck only halfway open -- however it leads into a pristine hexagonal corridor, with a giant shinign crystal floating above an altar. As he walks down the corridor, he raises a hand to his ear and holds a bauble earring.

“The readings were right, Liyer. The power crystal is still active.”

The response is uneven, but the words “Bal” and “careful” are clear.

“I think the mana is disrupting the sending pearl,” Bal says. He looks around.

After a moment of examination, he finds a magical tripline and lets out a sigh. A thought is shown to the audience: “If I tripped that, I might never see Liyer and gramps again. But I don’t see how to disarm it… just need to remember it’s here on the way out.”

He pulls out a piece of black stone and sets it to the sides of the altar stairs, then approaches the crystal. Unable to get any response from his sending pearl, he taps the crystal, producing a clear chiming sound effect, and them closes his eyes, reaches out…

And takes the crystal from the altar. The lights flicker for a panel, but recover. With a smirk, Bal begins to descend the stairs, but the high camera angle shows what he misses: the altar is retracting into the floor.

After escaping the cavern and emerging onto a clifftop, Bal meets up with the armored wagon bearing the insignia of a pistol and pickaxe -- and gives Liyer a warm hug as she greets him outside the wagon. He sets the biggest piece of loot on the ground, or as close as it will set, as it floats and begins to rotate, creating a field of restorative magic. Liyer examines it using some sort of slate-like device, she says that a crystal this size could make some settlement very prosperous. Their grandfather, Luko, come out and says, “Our crystal is wearing down, we might need to keep this one.” 

Liyer nods and takes the Crystal inside. “I’ll install this one to carry us to the next town,” she explains. Luko and Bal are able to have a conversation while they examine the map. They note a settlement about half a day away called Bludoe, and another called Millamak. Luko says either one should be able to give them a good haul from the loot, but as he offers Bal the choice, a monster emerges from the cavern:

A giant two-headed serpent golem roars and rears its heads up. “A dragon!?” They’re both taken off guard, but Bal grabs his pistol and focuses his plasma to charge it up. “Take cover,” he tells Luko, but the old man is picking up a pair of swords.

“I may be old, but I’m not dead,” Luko says, gritting his teeth as he channels plasma into the blades. “This is a bidra.” He explains that its heads will use different elements, and unless they can sever both heads at the same time, it won’t go down. He tosses one of the swords to Bal. “Together.”

### Main Storyline

Due to damage from the bidra attack, the family goes to Bludoe to try and find repair materials for their wagon. While there, they find themselves entangled in a raider plot to excavate all of Bludoe’s ancient ruins by the Saidyur Raider Clan. Luko offers the family’s mercenary services to defend the city in exchange for a proper digrun permit and a promise that anything the city needs will be delivered to them.

Bal and Liyer take on the Saidyur family in a series of increasingly convoluted battles against repurposed ancient golems and novel golems created by their genius artificer Kybrai. Between fights, Bal is able to explore into the depths of the ruins, with Liyer using her own skills as the team artificer to support Bal with enhancements to his gear, scan data, and communications. For several chapters, when Bal is injured, Liyer even takes on the digrunning role while Bal offers support from the wagon.

Deep below the modern city, they eventually discover a chamber with four stasis chambers containing humanoid forms. When Bal approaches the control station, it comes alive and reveals that due to overpopulation of the surface, the Enkidu Protocol is being activated. One of the stasis chamber control panels changes from “Sealed” to “Unsealed,” and a giant beastly superman, looking like a human with golem parts, is revealed. Liyer tells Bal to get out immediately -- his current weaponry and armor are no match for the monster coming out of stasis.

After Bal’s final confrontation with the Saidyur Clan, Enkidu announces that the Human Population is Out of Scope, and he will be destroying the region of Bludoe. When confronted by Bal, he explains that Bal is a descendant of the supermen, and questions why he has sacrificed his enhancements. Bal is holding his own against the massive figure, but is partially captured in a stasis bomb explosion, causing him to lose control of his legs (temporarily). Enkidu tries to capture the crippled Bal, but Liyer launches a magical artillery shell and makes a bold rescue run. Enraged, but confident in his victory, Enkidu warns them: There are 2 days until Human Collapse, and if Bal does not align with the Supermen, he will be part of the collapse.

Bal is ultimately able to defeat Enkidu, return him to stasis, and cancel the destruction of Bludoe, but begins to grapple with what it means to be recognized by the ancient superweapons as a superman who can authorize, and cancel, the destruction of the world.

The next story begins a few months later, when a superman who looks suspiciously like grandpa Luko’s daughter announces that due to the failure of Bluedoe’s Reset Protocol, other Supermen have been awakened and will be purging the world of the overpopulated humans. Four supermen are sent to distant areas of Xardon, and Liyer uses the family’s airship (upgraded from the wagon between stories) to take Bal between te four regions being prepared. The Saidyur Clan returns, along with two other Raider Clans, to make things harder for Bal as they attempt to loot the technology of the ruins before the Supermen make their final move.

At the end of the story, with all four of the other Supermen returned to stasis, Liyer assembles an access key to the northern island where the supermen control is centered -- but only Bal, recognized as a superman, can enter the facility. From the facility, he is transported to a space fortress, and engages in a debate with the master computer of the system, created by the supermen to monitor the world, keeping humanity from overpopulating, and awakening supermen as needed to execute population control measures. Bal asks how many supermen there are, and is told there are over 50,000 supermen in various vaults and ruins around the world, with another 100,000 in stasis within the fortress, awaiting a safety signal to be awakened.

The next story reveals that it has been a year since Bal entered the facility, and Liyer and Luko have made a truce with the three Raider Clans from the last story -- Liyer and Kybrai are trying to develop an airship that can reach the space fortress with help from the engineers of the other clans, but suddenly six beams of light fall from the sky, followed by a message containing Bal’s voice.

“The system has sent six supermen to test the best of humanity. Please, show them mercy.”

This story follows the four engineers supporting each other as digrunners trying to overcome the six newly awakened supermen, and to showcase the potential for humans and supermen to exist peacefully without conquest. They are often challenged by local militia forces which see the supermen as an existential threat to humanity, but they are ultimately able to succeed, showing the six Superman “knights” that humanity can accept differences without terror or oppression.

### Finale

After Liyer calms the sixth superman, Bal returns from the Fortress, but now has golem components, marking his as a true superman. He has not lost his heart, however, and greets Liyer, and Kybra, warmly. He explains that the master system of the fortress will be awakening, and he has been designated as the King of the Supermen, responsible for guiding them into peaceful coexistence with humanity.

With the six now-peaceful supermen from this story at his service, he tells Liyer that he’d really like to return to Bludoe.

### Continuations

There were no continuations of this story until A.A. 371 when an anonymous circle calling themselves Team Enkidu published _\~ETERNAL\~ Enkidu Keldin_ beginning in November 371. In this story, the Team posits that the Enkidu of this world is possessed by the consciousness of _Magical Doppelgänger_‘s Enkidu while trying to get Bal to destroy humanity.
