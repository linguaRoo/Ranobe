# Keldin Girl - Little Witch Liyer

The most recent Keldin series to enter publication is _Mornai Keldin—SHOXECHIN Xáiti’tal_, formally translated as _Keldin Girl—Little Witch Liyer_ and localized into English as _Little Witch Liyer_. It is written by a private husband and wife team and is being published on a fortnightly schedule in the anthology _Everybody Read!!_, an all ages mixed-gender meten anthology magazine. _Everybody Read!!_ is published weekly on Tuesday (every _romjia_ and _eljia_ on Atrusius).

The husband and wife team uses the circle name _Sug Dandé Réjo_ “The Family Circle” working on the project publish under the aliases _Cici Almen_ (translating into English as “Sweet Cream”) and _Anak Popo_ (“Never a Papa”). They are known for being very good at writing and illustrating cute girls of all sorts, having primarily worked on other _xáiti’tal_ series.

## Broad Summary

Liyer is a young girl who just wants to hang out with her friends, but when a mysterious creature calling itself a “Familiar” approaches her, she can’t leave it to the mercy of the cruel beasts chasing it. She accepts its hand, repeats the magic chant, “Become ye mine wand,” and transforms into the Little Witch _Lyric._

With the power of her new Familiar, Lyric uses the _Song of Heart_ “Tender Voice” to calm the aggressive beasts and remove evil spirits from them. After the fight, the Familiar introduces itself properly -- Melody the Songstress. Now that they’ve formed a bond, Melody is _Liyer’s_ familiar, and will be by her side, but Liyer needs to protect her secret identity while also seeking out other Familiars and girls who can bond with them.

## Chapter Summaries

### Chapter 1

(published 2026-Feb-24)

The story is set up with Liyer, an 8 (Atrusian) years-old girl[^3] taking care of animals at her mother’s veterinary practice before her afternoon session at school. As she makes her way back to school, she sees a mysterious spotted cat-like animal grooming itself on a wall. She meets back up with her friends Relya and Tari as they all head back to school. The chapter ends with a dark figure sending out two dark spirits with a simple command: “Capture Qonshoxem.”[^2]

Liyer parts ways with Relya and Tari on her way home, and is approached by the cat-like creature from earlier. It introduces itself as a Familiar (in Adinjo _réjolav_ “family spirit”) and asks Liyer to help protect it. As soon as the Familiar makes the request, two stray dogs appear chasing after it -- Liyer scoops the Familiar up and promises to protect it at her mother’s clinic.

### Chapter 2

(published 2026-Mar-10)

The stray dogs seem to grow larger and work together to corner Liyer and the Familiar in an alleyway. The Familiar nuzzles Liyer’s palm and tells her to chant the words, “Become ye mine wand.” When she does as instructed, the Familiar transforms into a silver wand with the Familiar’s face, and pink spinels for eyes -- and Liyer herself is transformed into an armored school uniform, and declares herself “Little Witch Lyric” (in Adinjo _SHOXECHIN Xáiti’tal_ “LYRIC the Little Magic Girl”).

Able to feel the disharmony within the stray dogs, Lyric fights defensively until the Familiar’s voice in her heart tells her to sing the song of love in her heart. Using the Tender Voice song, two dark spirits are driven out of the stray dogs, and Lyric uses her wand to purify the spirits. The purified spirits take the form of fireflies and merge with her wand. She reverts out of her Little Witch form and the Familiar thanks her for assistance, then asks Liyer to look after the stray dogs, who were hurt by the fighting.

### Chapter 3

(published 2026-Mar-24)

Liyer brings the stray dogs to the clinic and after telling her mom that she found them limping, she goes upstairs. The Familiar enters her bedroom through a window and admits that she is married to Liyer now. Her name is Qonshoxem, or Melody, and she has the power to sense and heal the hearts of other living beings. She has a mission to find and purify corrupted Familiars, and Liyer must help her now that they are married.

Liyer pauses at the repeated use of the word “married.” She admits that she hates to see animals or people suffering, but isn’t sure she can take on the weight of being Lyric so easily… until she hears a scream from downstairs. Rushing downstairs, she finds that one of the birds downstairs has grown almost as big as herself and her mother has passed out.

### Chapter 4

(published 2026-Apr-7)

The chapter opens with Liyer reaching out to Melody, chanting the incantation, and transforming. The bird lets out a piercing screech and grabs Liyer’s mom in its claw before bursting through the wall and hopping up to the neighborhood roofs. Lyric chases it, and Qonshoxem tells her that this bird is not possessed by a spirit, but a corrupted familiar. If they can learn its name, they can bind and purify it, but the Tender Voice will be useless.

The fight against the pet bird crosses the rooftops of the whole neighborhood, with Lyric singing to release sound wave attacks and the bird countering with bursts of wind to disrupt the sounds. Qonshoxem eventually says she’s figured out the spirit’s name: Arrozom.[^3] If Lyric can use the Song of Heart “Calling Home” with that name, they can draw Arrozom out of the bird. Lyric protests the idea of using this immediately -- her mother and the bird would be hurt if they call Arrozom out while it’s flying.

### Chapter 5

(published 2026-Apr-21)

Lyric repeats that she can’t put her mother or the bird in any more danger by trying to stop the familiar Arrozom. Qonshoxem is understanding and helps her try to lure the bird back to the ground. They are able to get it to the ground in a park, using the bird’s need for food to force Arrozom to the ground. As it eats suet, Lyric chants the Calling Home and draws out the sparrow-like familiar Arrozom. As the bird shrinks back to its normal size, Lyric uses the Tender Voice to purify the Familiar, who thanks her and offers to support Qonshoxem.

Lyric reverts to Liyer and wakes her mother up. Jenais, her mother, says she remembers seeing a shadow out of the corner of her eyes, then got attacked by a giant bird. She doesn’t remember what happened until she woke up, but she’s happy that Liyer is okay. When they return home, she is upset about the damage to her ground floor clinic, but happy to see that apparently none of the animals escaped. Up in Liyer’s room, Qonshoxem and Arrozom tell her that she can only be married to one Familiar, so Arrozom needs the protection of another person as soon as possible. The chapter ends with Relya drawing a picture of Lyric fighting the giant bird.

### Chapter 6

(published 2026-May-5)

Liyer is sitting in her classroom at school when Relya sits down next to her. She asks why Relya was running late, and Relya just smiles and taps a rolled up piece of paper. “I’ll tell you at break.” Class starts. On the next page, the teacher announces the end of history class, and Relya eagerly pulls out the rolled up paper -- showing the picture she drew last night to Tari and Liyer. Liyer recognizes the fight immediately, but knows she needs to keep her secret. She says Relya has a great talent and should think about submitting a meten to the school’s newssheet. Tari says it wasn’t imaginary, she saw the bird from her neighborhood too. Liyer suddenly excuses herself. In the washroom, she asks Melody what they should do.

At lunch break, Liyer invites her friends to join her for lunch at her house. Melody follows the trio as they head to Liyer’s house together and Arrozom lands nearby. “I like the artist,” it says. “Please?” Melody says they don’t know yet if she has the potential. Relya notices the repair workers at Liyer’s house and asks what happened. Liyer explains that the giant bird damaged their house. She claims she was so scared when she saw it that she fainted, so she doesn’t remember anything, but Relya presses her on that… until all of their stomachs grumble. Liyer promises a delicious lunch. A shadowy figure watches the girls enter the clinic…

### Chapter 7

(published 2026-May-19)

Liyer and her friends fix leftover soup and wrapped sandwiches for lunch, with Jenais joining them to eat. Jenais says that she heard one of the pet birds being very loud but passed out from fright and doesn’t remember what exactly happened until she woke up in the park, with Liyer sitting next to her. Liyer explains that she ran downstairs and passed out before she could recognize anything, but woke up with a hole in the wall and tried to find her missing mom.

Jenais says she’s going to offer the repair workers some of this food, and heads downstairs. Relya presses Liyer for absolutely everything, insisting that the giant bird had to have come out of this very house. Tari agrees, and asks if they can check the clinic to see if there’s any signs of how the bird grew giant. Liyer says they can do that after school, and encourages them to head back from lunch early.

As they are on their way back to school, a figure who appears to be Kenyu steps out of the shadows and blocks their way. She says, “The familiars belong the queen of spirits. For every familiar taken from us, we will take someone dear to you.” She then uses shadows to bind Liyer and Tari, though Relya (the most active), is able to run away. As she hides behind a stack of crates, Arrozom introduces itself to her. The chapter ends with Arrozom asking Relya: “Will you marry me to save your friends?”

### Chapter 8

(published 2026-Jun-2)

> [!info] Cover Illustration
> 
> The cover of this issue of _Everybody Read_ features the new transformation, Little Witch Válkri, showcasing a jade green and silver design for her armored school uniform, with an armored headpiece and eyeguards and aesthetic wings on her boots, gloves, and covering her ears. Arrozom’s face serves as the binding between the handle and blade of her spear, with jade eyes.

Relya is staring at Arrozom and nods slowly. Arrozom holds out it wing. “Hold my wing and say, ‘Become ye mine spear.’” Repeating the incantation, Relya transforms into Xáiti’tal VÁLKRI, the spear of the heavens. Her armored school uniform incorporates a motif of winged armor, including a headpiece with protective visor for her eyes. Arrozom transforms into a spear, its body extending into the haft, its head as the binding, and the blade emerging from its mouth, while the wings serve as a handguard.

Válkri uses blades of wind to disrupt the magical shadows binding Liyer and Tari, and faces down the Kenyu Woman. The woman notes that this affront to the Queen of Spirits will be remembered, and vanishes. Válkri checks on her friends and realizes they’ve both fallen asleep. As she moves to wake them, she hears Arrozom’s voice: “We need to keep this a secret from civilians. I wish we could hide it from the Queen, too.” Válkri nods and ducks back behind the crates. Arrozom instructs her to say, “Spear of Heaven, release,” so she can dismiss her transformation and wake her friends up. Arrozom disguises itself as a necklace after reverting.

Relya wakes Liyer and Tari up, and asks if either of them knows who that scary lady was. The woman is watching them from the rooftops as the make their way back to school. After their day ends with a physical fitness field session, Relya invites Liyer to the park where the giant bird was yesterday. Once they’re in the park, Arrozom returns to its sparrow form and apologizes to Qonshoxem, but reveals that Xáiti Weyra, Thailaxin’s partner, forced its hand. Qonshoxem reveals itself and Liyer lets out a deeply relieved sigh.

“I’m sorry I couldn’t tell you about Lyric.” Relya is understanding, but as she hugs her friend, the earth shudders and shakes. From under the ground, a mole-like monster arises, and Liyer and Relya stand together, hands out for their familiars.

### Chapter 9

(published 2026-Jun-16)

Liyer and Relya transform into their Little Witch forms and Qonshoxem assures them that this monster is just corrupted by lesser spirits. Lyric insists they still can’t hurt it too much, the mole is innocent, after all. Válkri agrees and promises not to injure it. Over a few pages, they manage to get it still and calm enough for Lyric to sing the Tender Voice, and release six corrupted spirits. As they’re purified, half of them merge with each of the weapons. Lyric digs a small soft patch of earth and lets the mole go back into the park ground, then Válkri transforms back into Relya. Lyric looks confused, but Melody tells her to say, “Wand of Heart, release,” to control her own transformation.

“So we need to find more spirits and familiars,” Relya says. “And the bad girls are going to come after us and our families. Can we do anything to protect them?”

Melody and Arrozom converse quietly for a few panels, then nod. “Silver can make a barrier that spirits can’t go through on our own. You could carry us through, but you can protect your homes with a simple coin nailed above every door and window.” Melody hesitates, but continues. “We’ll be more tired inside the house too. But it should keep your families from being attacked at home.”

Relya grumbles. She says her allowance isn’t enough to stick daimos everywhere. Liyer sighs and agrees. “That’s a lot of money. How much do we really need?” Relya starts counting on her hands. “I think you’d need about six at your house, and mine would need another six. That’s like a whole month of allowance!”

“We could try hiding outside of your homes at night. But then we might not be there if you need to transform,” Arrozom says. “And we’d be sitting sparrows for any dark witches!”

As they try to think up another idea, they hear a jingling purse and scurrying feet. They look around but can’t find anybody—just a cute coinpurse with a hurriedly scribbled note: “Gift for you two.” Inside they find exactly twelve coins. Relya points to the purse. “Isn’t this…” Liyer quiets her with a finger up to her lips. “That’s a little too convenient. Let’s make sure we pay this favor on, right?” Relya nods and they split the coins -- every single one of them a simple one-daimos featuring the familiar horse-head mark and the number “1.”

The girls split up to go back to their own homes, and the last scene shows Tari—with no coinpurse attached to her bookbag—watching her best friends split up. A small corgi-like spirit nuzzles her hand. “It’s not time yet, is it, Kijtal?”

### Chapter 10

(published 2026-Jun-30)

The story suggests that some time has passed since the end of the last chapter, though it is unclear how long it may have been. Liyer and Tari are working together on maths in their classroom at lunchtime at the start of the chapter, with Relya sipping on a bottled juice and talking about how she can’t decide whether she’s more into drawing or cross. As they enjoy this quiet life, we also get a glimpse into each of their inner lives.

Liyer feels the most burdened by her own role as a little witch, reflecting on an evening with Qonshoxem. After defeating a mutated animal, Liyer asks why the lesser spirits merge with familiars. Qonshoxem explains that the lesser spirits are a form of food for familiars, similar to certain animals and plants for humans. While spirits, including familiars, don’t need to eat to survive, they do need to eat to grow more powerful… and defeating the Queen of Spirits will require a lot more power than they have right now.

Relya eats a bacon, raumesh, and leafy green sandwich and says she’s sorry she’s had to let Tari walk home alone lately. Tari observes that she’s glad Liyer and Relya are spending more time together, teasing that there’s nothing as pure as the friendship between two young ladies. We see that Válkri has become very invested in Lyric—the framing even uses floral motifs common among young romance—and she feels like she’s become Liyer’s knight is shining armor. Arrozom explains that it’s only natural for their marriage to bring about a unity of purpose: As one of the Royal Knights of the Familiars, it’s only natural for it to feel strongly about defending its lady, and for that feeling to influence Relya’s attitude towards Liyer and Qonshoxem too. It does apologize if that’s making Relya uncomfortable, but notes that all she has to do in that case is talk to Lyric about her feelings.

Tari excuses herself from lunch for a bit. Finding a shady part of the schoolyard, she brings Kijtal out. “I feel so bad having to hide from them,” she says, sipping from a bottle of herbal tea. “But how much longer do we need to wait?” Kijtal tilts its head and sighs. “We’ll both know when the time is right.” We see a recollection of Tari in a little witch outfit with a canine tail and a dog-head helmet. She is standing with a dark shadowed figure looming over her. Four other little witches lie on the ground around her: Lyric, Válkri, and two shadowed figures (though the silhouettes suggest an Elressie and a Semeric girl), and the figure calls out a command to the spirits of Qonshoxem, Arrozom, Dhonchark, Lobyachi, and Kijtal renounce their unions to these humans. Tari, the only one still standing holdings up her paw-gloved hands and calls out, though the words she says are not revealed. As the chapter ends, she looks to Kijtal. “How many times?” Kijtal just cocks its head again and climbs onto her lap.
