# A Glossary of Atrusian Terms

## Foreword

The glossary uses a strictly lower-case format for words being defined and identified. They should be properly capitalized when used as proper nouns.

Where not otherwise noted, these terms are in the _Adinjo Journalist_ language. 

## A

- **abse:** white, light, bright; this word is considered archaic and refers to “sky-like colors”, the preferred modern nomenclature for “white” is _vic_
- **abso:** black, dark, deep-coloured; this word is considered archaic and refers to “sea-like colors”, the preferred modern nomenclature for “black” is _pazo_
- **adin:** a species of humanoids originally from the world of Gaea, descended from the residents of Atlantis, the adin mature at the same rate as other humans on Atrusius and the current broad generation has an estimated lifespan of about 360 years, though this is gradually declining
- **adolescent:** a sapient Atrusian who is in a developmental stage between juvenile _childhood_ and full _adult_ maturity, for most sapient species this biologically begins at around 8-9 and ends around 12-13; this is a localized usage of the English word
- **adult:** a sapient Atrusian who is in the mature developmental state of their life, generally 12-13 years old or older; this is a localized usage of the English word
- **anèdjia:** Lightningday; Friday of even-numbered weeks
- **arzol:** blue, lazuli; borrowed into Adinjo Journalist from the Latin _lazuli_ or Arabic _lazuward_, 
- **asenkier:** the eastern-most continent on the giant landmass of Atrusius, east of Hyarem and Xardon the landscape is largely dominated by various sorts of wetlands and wastelands, and is the most densely settled area by Hol’tiin states; the name of the continent is of unknown provenance, but resembles an Adinjo Journalist construction “_asènt kier,_” which would mean something like “the east is wooded,” however the continent name _may_ be derived from a Hol’tiin language
- **atrusia:** a continent on Atrusius, this continent is cursed to spawn undead beings every night
- **atrusius:** the fantasy world on which the vast majority of this documentation’s publications are produced
- **aureb:** a continent on Atrusius, this continent has a warm Mediterranean-style climate in the south and European-style climate in the north, and is the home to primarily Kenyu and Elressie settlements, though it is also the continent where Kuharashi, capital of the Adin Imperial States, is located; the continent name is derived from a Kenyu language
- **aurelg:** a Gramurn mythological monster of shadow and earth, often depicted as a giant bipedal monster cloaked in armor and supernatural darkness
- **avint:** English localization of a term from _Cyber Soldier: Bal Keldin_, short for “Avatar with Intelligence;” (META: because _Cyber Soldier_ was developed in English before establishing the proper vocabulary, the original Adinjo form is currently unclear)

## B

- **blueyard:** a courtyard defined by the inclusion of a water feature, usually in the most central location; while these features are generally not fit for swimming, they are pleasant places to socialize
- **blutwar ejis:** diveball; a rugby-like sport played underwater in special pools; literally “underwater chasing”

## C

- **chan:** (numeral) seven
- **chanjia:** week, literally “seven-day”
- **child:** a sapient Atrusian in their juvenile stage of development, usually from about 3-4 years old until adolescence; this is a localized usage of the English word
- **ch’kier:** a continent on Atrusius, located south of Mermag, with a high degree of passive volcanic activity that gives it a tropical-style climate despite its southern latitude, largely uninhabited in the modern era, and only lightly explored; the name of the continent is of unknown provenance, but resembles an Adinjo Journalist construction “_chon kier,_” which would mean something like “the ocean is wooded,” however the continent name _may_ be derived from a Hol’tiin language
- **cici:** an affectionate form of address similar to English “my sweet” or “sweetie,” though it is also sometimes used as a description for sweet, gentle, and tender people, stories, and ideas
- **coci:** nighttime, midnight, bedtime
- **courtyard:** the central communal area of a residential block which does not belong to any individual lot, confer with _greenyard_ for the standard forma used by most city blocks, these courtyards are the center of block-community life, and are common places for children to play and adults to socialize

## D

- **daimos:** the silver tier of currency on Atrusius, a silver coin or a price listed in silver; serves socially as the equivalent to a pound, dollar, euro, or 100 yen; economically roughly equates to the buying power of USD 2.50; this word is derived from a Semeric word meaning “payment”
- **dime:** (locally _daim_) any individual silver-tier coin; this is an English variant of a local shortening of the term _daimos_, used for coins in the silver currency tier

## E

- **elressie:** a species of anthropomorphic feline originally from the world of Gaea, they arrived on Atrusius with the adin; little is known of their private isolated cultures outside of their own species, except that they are highly communal and patriarchal, and extremely protective of their females; they have life spans of around 60 years on average with a peak around 80
- **elzjia:** Iceday; Tuesday of even-numbered weeks

## F

- **falkena:** the northernmost continent on Atrusius, north of Mermag, and the ancestral home of the Cel’vark species, the continent has limited arable land but dense natural growth tundras, it is largely unihabited by the modern civilizations of Atrusius; the name is from the Classical Kenyu language
- **farnell:** a county and major city on the Millak peninsula of Hyarem continent; the name is from a Kenyu language, it is technically governed by the Lord of Farnell, Tamario Ellis

## G

- **garbling:** a small anthropomorphic primate species that is starting to be widely recognized as sapient, though still considered of lesser social and intellectual capacity than the other species, often likened to the human conception of gremlins, imps, or goblins, and long considered a pest to be hunted by adventurers; they do not appear to have an adolescent stage, reach adulthood in 6-8 years, and when living in their natural state live 15-20 years, though a few garblings who have lived with Greentenders have reached nearly 40 years old without obvious health complications; the word itself is of uncertain provenance
- **gaurin:** a Gramurn mythical monster, _gaurin_ appear similar to an ape or gorilla with heavy demonic or draconic wings. They are classified as dangerous beasts with little reason, however some myths also depict them as messengers of the gods
- **gerjui:** the silver moon of Atrusius, it orbits Atrusius in about 29.28 days
- **gifjia:** Fireday; Friday of odd-numbered weeks
- **gìl:** any individual gold-tier coin, this is a shortening of the term _gìldèn_, used for coins in the gold currency tier; cost, price, the listed price of any object in a market or catalog; originally a small flat or flattened shell, or a coin resembling the same
- **gìldèn:** the gold tier of currency on Atrusius, a gold coin or a price listed in gold; serves socially as the equivalent to 100 pounds, dollars, euros, or 10,000 yen; economically roughly equates to the buying power of USD 360; this word is derived from the Adinjo words _gìl_ (a small flat shell, a coin) and _orind_ (gold) meaning “gold coin”
- **gramurn:** a species of anthropomorphic wolf-like beings from the world of Atrusius, among their special senses is an involuntary visual shift to perceive thermal radiation in the darkness, using a second set of cones when their primary cones are understimulated, they mature at a similar rate to other humans and live an average of 174 years, with a maximum of around 204
- **grat’shrin:** (sometimes _gratshrin_) a heavy stone often worked for orkish tools, these tools are often relegated to ritual use in modern society, though they remain the basis for iconic orkish weapon designs in artwork; the word is Orkish and refers explicitly to the type of heavy, durable stone
- **greentender:** (usually Kenyu) ecologists and druids, biologists, researchers of nature and natural magic, ecological engineers; this is an English translation of a variety of terms often translated into Adinjo as _veroloktax_ “green-maker”
- **greenyard:** a courtyard defined by the specific feature of plantlife, this communal space may be used to grow herbs and garden plants for the sake of the community
- **grey-yard:** a courtyard defined by a specific _lack_ of vegetation as the primary feature, often more appealing to Hol’tiin, Semeric, and those who live by certain meditative philosophies
- **grossury:** (Adinjo: _quonyore_) 144 years, often the set of years with the same 144s-place value (much like Gaea, the “first grossury” is the years from 0 to 143)
- **gurion:** the slow moon of Atrusius, it has a pinkish-red color and orbits Atrusius in about 41.86 days

## H

- **hao’gnerk:** an orkish settlement in the eastern steppe of Xardon, generally described even by its inhabitants as having a “hellish” climate; the name id Orkish
- **hol’tiin:** a species of anthropomorphic lizard-like beings from the world of Atrusius, they are ectothermically reactive despite being primarily endothermic, they have thermal vision in darkness (though it is through a slightly different path than Gramurn), they mature at a similar rate to other humans, and live around 156 years on average, with a peak of around 180
- **howlstirrer:** bard, skald, poet, a composer and teller of epic poetry in the Gramurn traditions; this is an English localization rather than a native Atrusian term
- **human:** for the sake of this documentation, _human_ refers to all sentient, sapient species; this is an English term with a specific added definition within this documentation
- **hyarem:** (sometimes _hiarem, hiarèm,_ or _hyarèm_) one of the continents on Atrusius, east of Atrusia, the most densely populate continent with the most metropolitan and diverse populations overall, including major cities like Kamelon, Farnell, and Yshkaren; the continent name is from the Orkish Unified Tongue
- **hyfel:** the fast moon of Atrusius, this moon has a blue color and orbits Atrusius in about 16.11 days

## J

- **jaldin:** the Adinjo Journalist term for other humans from Gaea; while there are very few such individuals on Atrusius at present, the term is important as there are expected to be a large number of jaldin colonists arriving around the year 400; these jaldin age at the same rate as other humans with middle age around 27 Atrusian years and senescence setting in around 48 atrusian years
- **jalujia:** Earthday; Thursday of odd-numbered weeks
- **jon:** (numeral) three; trio
- **jongu:** triple, triplicate
- **jurabi:** midday, afternoon, mid-afternoon

## K

- **kamelon:** a major port city at the western end of the Millak peninsula on the continent of Hyarem, one of the first truly intercultural cities on the world built around A.A. 20 - 30, maintained and grown continuously since; the name is from Adinjo JOurnalist and roughly means “carved from stone”
- **kangyur:** a fictional city loosely inspired by Yshkaren and Kuharashi, originally created for the meten folio series _Bal Keldin_ in A.A. 292; the name is Adinjo Journalist and roughly translates as “biggest city” (cf. D.C. Comics’ “Metropolis”)
- **karadu:** phoenix
- **kenyu:** a species of anthropomorphic primate-like beings from the world of Atrusius, they are culturally diverse like humans, but have many other traits that liken them to the gaean perception of elves, notably after reaching adulthood at the same pace as other humans, they have extended periods of adult developmental stages, typically entering middle age around 288 (276-300) and beginning to experience senescent decline no earlier than 504, and they seldom speak of the death of their elders as a result of age; biologically related to _ork, peratan,_ and _semeric_ species; this word is from the classical kenyu language, the Adinjo word is _kenyudin_
- **kími:** (numeral) grand gross, twenty-thousand seven-hundred thirty-six; (measurement) ten-thousand, multitude; (scoring) over the top, breaks the scale, beyond reproach; may be shortened to _-mi_ in compound numbers, but usually only if they exceed the scale of _kími_ squared
- **kolmen:** evening, golden hour, early night
- **kom:** (numeral) five
- **konjia:** Metalday; Thursday of even-numbered weeks
- **kosij:** (numeral) eight; (meaurement) two-thirds, sixty-seven percent; (scoring) six out of ten, sub-par, not up to snuff; usually shortened to _kos-_ or _kosi-_ in compound numbers
- **krestu:** morning, dawn, before midday
- **kros:** cross, lacrosse[^1]; a field sport involving manipulating an airborne ball with a stick and net
- **kuhajia:** Sunday; Sunday of odd-numbered weeks
- **kuharashi:** (sometimes _sunbank_) a city on the continent of Aureb, the capital of the Adin Imperial States on Atrusius, in a land rented from a Kenyu nation; the name of the city is in Adinjo Journalist and roughly means “sunny bank” or “sunny beach”
- **kybrau:** cybernetic, electronic, digital

## L

- **larn:** (numeral) two; couple, pair; twice (as a prefix to another number or numeric word)
- **larngu:** copy, duplicate, clone, double or “doppel”
- **lav:** elemental, spirit, sprite; _usually_ a free, independent spiritual being that is not of a particular sapient species, often used in stories with unique appearances
- **leap day:** an intercalary day between 44 December and 1 January of leap years
- **leap year:** every third year, specifically years which are a multiple of 3, minus 1 (ie: 2, 5, 8), excluding every 10th leap year (ie: 29, 59, 89) but including every 50th leap year (ie: 149, 299, 449)
- **lekajagin:** battle card game, a class of card games in which each player uses their own personal deck to battle against an opponent, the specific rules of such games vary, and may be likened to Gaean games such as _Magic: The Gathering, Yu-Gi-Oh!!, The Pokémon Trading Card Game,_ and other such battle card games. The earliest such game used standard arcana-style (or tarot-like) decks, and so there are methods of playing battle games with standard playing card decks
- **leng’eni isa:** (often simplified to _isa_) a hard shelled fruit with sweet milk and flesh on the inside, a particularly hardy and resilient crop that grows well even in hard areas; the name is originally from Classical Kenyu, but has been borrowed into most every modern Atrusian language
- **long week:** a fortnight or fourteen day period, most typically one starting specifically on _kuhajia_
- **luanjia:** Moonday; Monday of odd-numbered weeks
- **lum:** (numeral) six
- **luxan:** (numeral) four; often shortened in the leftmost part of compound words to _lux-_ or _luxa-_

## M

- **magigame:** magitech games, often equivalent to Gaean video games; this term is an English localization
- **magishow:** a recording of a performance (initially of live theatre) captured by the use of magical crystals and played back through either a spellcaster or a piece of magitech, these are typically expensive to produce and are subject to copyright; this term is an English localization
- **magitech:** a form of technology which incorporates the use of mana as a power source, sometimes also creating programmed magic effects as output; this term is an English localization
- **mana:** the external energy field of the magic and the elements, _typically_ inert unless acted upon by plasma
- **meme:** (sometimes _mémé_) mama, mommy; a diminutive form of _medra_ “mother,” sometimes also applied to a generic motherly or auntie-like figure in a social group
- **mermag:** a continent on Atrusius, the primary homeland of the Gramurn species and all of their cultures, located in the northern tropics far to the west of Aureb and east of Asenkier; the name is synthesized from many Gramurn tongues and roughly means “growing land” with a sense similar to “motherland”
- **millak:** a peninsula that juts out of the southwester part of Hyarem, home to the independent citystate of Kamelon, Farnell, and the Southgate into the Atrusian underworld; the name is of uncertain linguistic provenance
- **mithral:** (sometimes _mitral_, _mitrahl_, _mitar_, _medwal_, etc.) a tree, which uses manasynthesis in addition to photosynthesis, the wood from such trees, especially treated through a meticulous process of sun-drying to crystallize the mana within the structure; charcoal made from such wood or coal from the fossilization of the same; _mithral steel_ is a steel made using mithral charcoal or fossilized mithral coal as the primary source of carbon; the word itself seems to appear in similar form across the Atrusian language families to refer to the same sort of trees
- **month:** a month on Atrusius may be referred to by the English names of Gaean months, each month is 44 days long (6 weeks and 2 days) and there are 12 months per year; this is an English localization of various local words used to refer to this period
- **mu:** (numeral) nine; (measurement) three-fourths, seventy-five percent; (scoring) seven out of ten, three out of five, acceptably average

## N

- **neimjia:** Knightsday; Sunday of even-numbered weeks
- **ngarak:** squad, team, unit, force, comrades; refers to a group with which one has broad shared struggles, including victories and defeats; this word is Orkish

## O

- **ōraiganon:** a gramurn spirit associated with sacrificial altars for burnt offerings, usually manifesting as a stone elemental with burning oil or pitch
- **oralen:** a vibrant orange-red berrylike fruit that is highly juicy and has very high levels of capsaicin, the juice and oil of oralen is sometimes used as a pesticide to deter mammals and insects, though it’s scent can attract birds; the word is of Classical Kenyu origins, but is widely used across most languages today
- **orinthia:** a major seaport on the southeastern coast of Atrusia, protected from the wandering undead by powerful magical words and silver circuits built into the city walls, the western counterpart to Kamelon; the name is from Adinjo Journalist and roughly means “golden port,” though the city was originally established under a kenyu name before the Adin arrived on Atrusius
- **ork:** a species of genetically modified anthropomorphic primate-like beings from the world of Atrusius, they are culturally diverse, and have the shortest lifespan of native Atrusian sapient species, maturing like other humans and living 60 years on average, or 80-90 at maximum, with a drive similar to Gaean humans; biologically related to _kenyu, peratan, semeric,_ and _tsel’vark_ (the species that genetically modified them); this word comes from the Orkish United Tongue, the Adinjo term is _orkdin_

## P

- **peratan:** a species that was originally the result of _kenyu_ and _semeric_ intermarriage, but has proven to be a stable and fertile species. They are the second-shortest lived sapient species on Atrusius, with an average lifespan of about 84 years, though those who keep good health can reach as long of a life as 120 years. They are less adventure-seeking than the other species, but those who do adventure have an uncanny sense for abnormalities and hidden danger; compare to J.R.R. Tolkien’s hobbits or Dungeons & Dragons’ halflings; this word is from the classical Kenyu language, the Adinjo word is _peratandin_ or _peradin_
- **plasma:** the natural magical energy field of living beings, must be cultivated to interact with the external _mana_ field to produce magic; this is distinct from _blood plasma_ or the state of matter, it is more analogous to _ch’i_ or _ki_
- **plati:** a platinum “coin” roughly 1 rod in diameter and 5 kg in weight, a physical hold of great wealth which sits idle in vaults, a reserve currency against which other economic values are derived; the social equivalent of a novelty check or a display of gold ingots, economically valued similar to a 500,000 pound, dollar, euro, or 50-million yen security or bearer bond, technically valued around USD 622,080
- **popo:** papa, daddy, dada; a dimunitive form of _poren_ “father,” sometimes applied to a paternal figure within a social group

## Q

- **qin:** (numeral) eleven; (measurement) ninety percent, almost total, almost complete; (scoring) nine out of ten, great, almost perfect
- **qori:** one-hundred-forty-four (144), gross, “hundred”; serves the numeric role of _hundred_ in social contexts because it is the square of the base value of the traditional Adin and Kenyu numeric systems; often shortened to _-ri_ in compound numbers

## R

- **rejjaku:** family cards, a card game of hand construction which may be played with either traditional playing cards or specialized cards using various illustrated characters with specific professions or roles; the game is similar to poker, especially when played with standard playing cards, but can get much more complex when using the character styled cards
- **rezolav:** familiar, familiar spirit, family spirit; a spirit which is bound (or capable of being bound) in a contractual relationship with a human
- **rknary:** a giant predatory bird trained as lifelong partners by certain Orkish tribes, large enough to carry two orks on its back when properly traind anad handles; the word is of Orkish origin, and often includes an epenthetic vowel when adopted into other languages (Adinjo: reknarii, Classical Kenyu: rìc’nari)
- **rod:** a unit of length, roughly 12 U.S. customary inches or 30 cm, used primarily for heights of persons or roughly person-sized objects, or movement of persons; this word is the English localization of several similarly-sized measurements used on Atrusius which range from approximately 10 inches/25 cm to about 15 inches/38cm
- **roha:** red, crimson; ultimately borrowed into the Adinjo family from Proto-Indoeuropean _\*h${_₁}$rewdʰ-_ “red”
- **romjia:** Windsday, Skyday; Tuesday of odd-numbered weeks
- **rune chips:** small clay, bone, ivory, stone, or similar hard tablets with numeral (or letter) values, often painted in different colors to added elemental associations; any game played with such chips, depending on the particular “chips” these games may resemble Gaean games such as dominoes, rummikub, mahjohng, or Scrabble

## S

- **sabak:** a point-counting card game where the values of two to five cards are built to a value of “19” using positive and negative values based ont he suits present in the set, the player who is closest to 19 or -19 with the fewest cards is the winner of a hand, and the highest winning hand is two-card 1 and 9 of the same suit (followed by the same, sharing sign but not suit, then using addition of the values); note that while the target score is represented as 19, it is an Adin game using base-twelve numbers, so the actual target score is valued at either 21 or -21 in base-ten, this game is inspired by the Star Wars card game known as sabacc.
- **semeric:** (sometimes spelled _semerik_) a species of anthropomorphic primate-like beings from the world of Atrusius, they are more culturally homogenous than other species, and have developed the ability to still perceive the shape of the world and objects around them without light (though they can also still see in light, they are sensitive to bright lights), and are the Atrusian equivalent of traditional fantasy Dwarves, after reaching adulthood around age 12-13, their further senescence is delayed until their 252-276, and they tend to retire from public life by 432 (and the death of elders is seldom spoken of as a result of age); this word comes from a Semeric language, the Adinjo word is _semerikdin_ or _semeridin_
- **sendalin (harp):** a plucked or picked string instrument of the Kenyu tradition, usually a hollow wooden box which resonates to amplify the sound of the strings, and which is tuned by the tautness of the strings; the name of this instrument comes from Classical Kenyu, the form of the instrument can vary from resembling a lyre to a zither or psaltery
- **seÿlb:** (sometimes _seilb_) an oily secretion produced by late adolescent orks, this secretion causes the skin to toughen into a flexible but durable leathery state; this word is from the Orkish United Tongue
- **seÿlb gelk:** (sometimes _seilb gelk_) the period of life when an adolescent ork is secreting their oily _seÿlb_; this term is from the Orkish United Tongue
- **shak:** (numeral) ten; (meaurement) five-sixths, eighty percent; (scoring) eight out of ten, decently good
- **shi:** they, them; the non-gendered singular pronoun in Adinjo Journalist, the common language in which most meten are printed
- **shif:** a shade of light grey, particularly a matte shade
- **short week:** a seven day period, typically one that begins on either a _kuhajia_ or _neimjia_

## T

- **teljia:** Beastday; Monday of even-numbered weeks
- **tèndi:** the copper tier of currency on Atrusius, a copper coin or a price listed in copper; serves socially as the equivalent to 0.01 pounds, dollars, euros, or 1 yen; economically roughly equates to the buying power of USD 0.018; this word is derived from a Gramurn word meaning “consistent”; any individual copper-tier coin: cent, pence, penny, yen, the term for this tier of coin may be used in figures of speech in the same way as small-value coins in Gaean life (ie: “tèndi for your thoughts” or “two tèndi”)
- **téoljia:** Spiritsday, Godsday; Saturday of even-numbered weeks
- **tóji:** one-thousand seven-hundred-twenty-eight (1728), great gross; serves the numeric role of _thousand_ in social contexts because it is the cube of the base value of the traditional Adin and Kenyu numeric systems; often shortened to _-ji_ or (rarely) _-óji_ in compound numbers
- **tomo:** twelve, dozen; serves the numeric role of English _ten_ in social contexts because it is the base value of the traditional Adin and Kenyu numeric systems; usually shortened to _-mo_ in compound numbers
- **tsel’vark:** (sometimes _celvark, zelvark,_ or _zelbokhian_) a species of sapient, hive-minded assimilatory insect-like beings originally native to Atrusius; this word is originally from classical Kenyu, the Adinjo word is _celvarkdin_ or _celvadin_
- **twarjia:** Watersday, Waterday; Wednesday of odd-numbered weeks

## U

- **underworld:** the underground portion of Atrusius, primarily inhabited by the Semeric and also by certain tribes of Orks, Gramurn, and Hol’tiin. most plantlife in the underworld has developed manasynthesis or thermosynthesis as there is not enough sunlight in the deeper parts of the underworld to keep traditional surface plants alive, and the ecosystem is generally more mana-dense than the surface, however it is not so mana-dense as to simply be a giant dungeon; this is an English translation of terms used across various languages, and is usually not conflated by Atrusians with the afterlife or “land of the dead”

## W

- **week:** see _short week_ and _long week_; when not otherwise clarified, a seven day period
- **win:** (numeral) one; single, solo, sole
- **worgulbÿr:** (sometimes “worg stick”) a staff used by orks for herding and defense, with a hook-shaped bident on one end, and a sheathed blade hidden on the opposite; with a small net attacked to the bident, this is the primary tool used in the game “cross” (versions with shorter handles and no blade are sometimes calls “cross sticks”); the word is Orkish

## X

- **xagijia:** Treeday, Treesday; Wednesday of even-numbered weeks
- **xájia:** Magicday, Magicsday; Saturday of odd-numbered weeks
- **xardon:** (sometimes _khardon_) a continent on Atrusius, east of Aureb and north of Hyarem, this continent has a dense western forest and eastern steppe; it is primarily settled by Kenyu states in the west and Orkish states in the east; the name is of uncertain provenance, though it is likely either Kenyu or Orkish

## Y

- **year:** 528 days, 529 days in a _leap year_
- **yenek:** generation, a particular zenade, the cohort of people within 6 years older or younger than a referenced individual, Atrusian cultures do not generally label generational cohorts in the way recent Gaean English speakers have done in the late 20th and early 21st century
- **yshkaren:** (sometimes _ÿshakaren_) a city in the northeastern central rift valleys of the continent Hyarem, the capital of the United Orkish States and center of unified Orkish culture; the name is from the Orkish Unified Tongue and roughly means “always home” or “eternal home”
- **yukùr:** a trick-taking team card game similar to the Gaean _euchre_, played with a partial deck, the size of which varies based on whether playing with 2 or 3 teams (4 suits for 2 teams, 6 suits for 3 teams), games may be played to 6 or 12 total points, teams earning one point for taking the most tricks or two (three in 3-team games) points for taking all the tricks of a given hand

## Z

- **zenade:** (Adinjo: _tomyore_) 12 years, the set of years with the same dozens place value (like Gaea, the “first zenade” would be the years from 0 to 11)

[^1]: Technically, _kros_ is not lacrosse, but lacrosse is the most fitting real-world equivalent to the sport.
