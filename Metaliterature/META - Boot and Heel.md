# Boot and Heel

_Boot and Heel_ is a mystery series that was originally written in a serial literature magazine around 299 A.A. It centers on the titular Boot, a Gramurn (or sometimes a Kenyu “elf”) dilettante investigator, and a female companion, partner, or housemate named Heel (typically, Heel is an Adin, though in some stories her species is changed). Since the first stories entered the public domain over 50 years ago, the mythos and connections between various versions of Boot and Heel have varied frequently, but the core relationship of the “Consulting Detective” and their “Assistant” has been stable regardless of species.

## Boot

Role in Story: Primary protagonist  
Occupation: Consulting Detective

Physical Description:	A gramurn male of about 28 years (human age). Has dark brown fur, and a curly black mane. Green eyes. Tall for a gramurn, almost seven rods when standing fully upright. Tends to slouch.

Personality:	A genius of detail and labor. Though easily distracted, Boot learns very quickly by study and practice. He often imitates other people to “get in their head.” Boot is heavily introverted, and has little interest in people for the sake of relationships, mostly he sees them as curiosities to study. Very proud at all times, and very energetic when his interest is engaged. The “hunt” for the truth behind a mystery is his greatest motivating force.

Habits/Mannerisms:	Studies people intently. He often tells people who they are before they introduce themselves to him. Imitates those more practiced in certain fields than himself. Plays the sendalin harp to focus his mind, when possible.

Background:	Raised by a wealthy merchant family from Kymara province, Boot speaks naturally with an informal or casual Kymaran accent (refer to the notes “Boot’s Accent” for more details) as well as with the formal accent of elite school (refer to the notes “Formal Tradespeak” for details).

Notes:	Myers Briggs - ISTP

### Details

Boot is the primary protagonist. He is a young adult, perhaps 28 years of age or equivalent. He hails from the Kymara province, which is shown by a Northern accent and noted by many people who meet him.

Boot has been a commissioned investigator for 2-3 years despite his young age, and has studied (albeit lightly) almost all areas of traditional investigation, and dabbles with the use of magic.

Boot is either gramurn or kenyu (depending on the writer), which contributes to his style of investigation, but not to his skills.

PRIMARY SKILLS: Spotting evidence, alchemistry, geology, pattern recognition, sociology.

INTERESTS: Criminals, music, poetry, games of chance.

HOBBIES: Card games, sendalin

PERSONALITY:

- Boot is a genius of labor, who has turned his lack of focus into a strength. Boot likely suffers from ADHD or similar.  
- Boot is asocial. He shuns almost all personal interactions unrelated to cases or studies, though he often leaves his home to study people.
- Boot is a proud person. To question his skills or character is a grave insult that may (depending on his mood) lead to a fierce defense of himself, or a cold vengeance.
- Boot lives for the hunt. He often seems to become depressed when he is between cases, and will sometimes dismiss cases in this state for being too unsporting — which tends to mean they are too easy for him to solve.

### Boot’s Kymaran Accent

There are several accent features of the Kymaran province that are peculiar to the informal speech most often employed by adventurers, commoners, and criminals. Boot tends to show these features in his speech, along with a few mannerisms which are common only among Kymaran gramurn.

#### Vowels

- The words “foot” and “strut” are both pronounced with the vowel /u/. This pronunciation pervades the linguistic tendencies on Kymarans, and any word which rhymes with “foot” or “strut” is pronounced with /u/. 
- Unlike most dialects of Tradespeak, the Kymaran accent rarely expresses the sound /æ/, instead pronouncing those words which use it with the /ä/ sound instead. The Kymaran accent often does this with the sound of /ɒ/ and the other open sounds.
- Kymarans do not glide the letter [y] unless it is actually followed by another vowel. They tend to pronounce it as either /ɪ/ when unstressed, or as the diphthong /äɪ/ when stressed. Kymara itself is pronounced /kɪ mär: ä/

## Heel

Heel spent 8 years as an adventuring medic before meeting boot. Heel was trained as a medic in the Academy of Health & Sciences in Kuharashi (Sunbank) and speaks medically as an aristocrat, but otherwise as a commoner. Heel is a female kenyu (or another species which contrasts with Boot’s; most often an ) and slightly older than Boot.

Heel encounters Boot by chance, as a witness to the first crime in The Magic Investigations, and offers her assistance reluctantly. Heel is actually trained and practiced in the use of magic for healing and investigating injuries. After this case, Boot offers to see to her room and board if she will help him order his thoughts on various matters.

Heel is very much the everyman next to the brilliant deductions of Boot. Though she is a skilled medic and mage, she often overlooks details which Boot finds crucial. As Heel is recording the stories, these details remain hidden from the reader until Boot makes his revelations known.

PRIMARY SKILLS: Medicine (magical and mundane), Geography, Communication

INTERESTS: Poetry, games of chance, music

HOBBIES: Drinking, card games (especially sabak & euchre), writing

PERSONALITY:

- Heel is a practical woman who focuses her pursuits in one area. She does not seem to show any deviations from neurotypical mindset.
- Heel is extroverted and requires more companionship than the company of Boot. She is used to traveling with four or five other adventurers, and feels lonely when shut in for long periods of time.
- Heel defines herself by what she can offer others. She becomes withdrawn and depressed if she is unable to help those to whom she is close.
- Heel is often blinded to the trees by the forest — when confronted with too many details, she focuses on the whole instead of the elements, except with regards to her practice as a medic.
