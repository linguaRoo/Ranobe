# Atrusian Adventuring

Adventuring on Atrusius is a combination of a romantic ideal, local military or militia service, animal control, and infrastructure maintenance, with a dash of ecological management. While many adventurers come into a Guild for their specific career with the hope of wealth and glory, the reality usually crashes into them fairly early on: they are primarily glorified animal control and civic servants who wont likely be able to wander the world, even though they end up drawing comfortable stipends for their work.

The average adventurer only needs about 1 quest every 2-3 months to live a comfortable life, often drawing a stipend of 10-15 gìldèn per month after their dues. Most quests take two days or fewer, but most Adventurers require significant downtime for proper recovery and therapy, repairs to their equipment (if they even own their equipment), and maintaining an actual normal life.

## Professional Guilds

Most Adventurers belong to two (or more) guilds: one guild specific to their particular adventuring career path (such as a guild for Martial Combatants, Technical Experts, Field Magic Users, Healers, etc.) and in most cases, the Guild of the Many Talented (Note that Many Talented is not hyphenated, but is two distinct words), a Guild Union that manages job requests, assessments, and most of the long-term financial stability of Adventurers.

> [!info] Terminology
> 
> For the ease of initial drafting, this document currently uses common English terms for the broad archetypes of five broad classes of Adventurers.

### Fighters’ Guilds

Fighters’ Guilds focus on career melee and missile combat fighters, teaching martial discipline with the necessary flexibility to adapt to irregular conditions.

### Healers’ Guilds

Healers’ Guilds focus on the ecological and support roles of Adventuring, often teaching the use of botanical and magical reagents for healing, buffs, and preventative measures.

### Mages’ Guilds

Mages’ Guilds focus on the specific applications of magic in adventuring life. They are drastically distinct from academic Thaumic Guilds, usually insisting on the physical preparations of their spellcasters to manage their time in the field as well as their magical studies.

### Rogues’ Guilds

Rogues’ Guilds focus on the geography and ecology of the world and dungeons. They are generally not criminal, but are experts with a broad base of knowledge in how the world works, and how to spot dangers, managing and mitigating the greatest threats a party may encounter while in the field. This can include skills associated with thievery, but the stance of Rogues is that the tools of thieves are not themselves immoral—the way they are used determines their moral weight.

### Psychics’ Guild

Psychics’ Guilds focus on the needs and skills of individuals who focus on the use of plasma to sharpen their own senses and skills. Psychics are able to wield their plasma directly without shaping the mana field, and their abilities cannot be replicated by magitech, but they are not as “flashy” as magic. Adventuring psychics often travel to hone their skills and to serve as broad support for the result of their team. They may have abilities like allowing their allies to share sensory experiences, communicate silently, and sensing the presence of spiritual entities.

## The Guild of the Many Talented

The Guild of the Many Talented is the modern name for the _Guild of Adventurers_ chartered in AA 26 (base-10). This Guild has an international license, and so has guild halls in almost every pact-affiliated walled settlement, and often has rest-houses at regular intervals outside of the walls.
