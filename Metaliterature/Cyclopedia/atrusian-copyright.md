# Atrusian Copyright Law & Standards

The standard rule of copyright on Atrusius is that the creation of artistic and practical works grants the Author a legal monopoly over the Work for the remainder of the year in which it is published plus 12 full years from initial publication.

To ensure that every work is protected for 12 full years, the date or year of publication is considered to be year 0, and copyright age is calculated on the New Year’s Day of each year after publication. Copyright expires only when this age would exceed twelve (12). So for an example:

> If you publish a story in January of 374, your work will remain under your monopoly for 374 (0), 375 (1), 376 (2), 377 (3), 378 (4), 379 (5), 380 (6), 381 (7), 382 (8), 383 (9), 384 (10), 385 (11), and 386 (12) -- it will enter the public domain on New Year’s Day in 387.

This international standard is the baseline for worldwide copyright on Atrusius, and all Pact-Affiliated states must recognize these terms for international works, but may have their own laws, whether more or less restrictive, for their own domestic copyright.

Copyright is considered a matter of Civil Law in most cases of violation, though it may rise to Criminal Conduct in cases where damages of 1,728 gilden or more are alleged.

## Possession of Copyright

To hold a copyright, one must be a Natural Person or a Circle of Natural Persons who have formed a contract to share the rights of jointly-produced Works. In the case of a circle, only the members involved in a Work actually possess Copyright in the work, and each such Member has all of the rights secured by Copyright.

## Monopoly Rights

The monopoly rights granted by copyright include the rights of publication, profit, translation, adaptation, and derivation. These rights are generally not transferable, only licensable or revocable.

### Publication

Copyright is granted by the act of Publication, and because few individuals own their own means of Publication, this right is typically licensed to one or more Printing Houses. The Holder of a Copyright is always free to control the licensing for Publication of their works while they hold a Monopoly, including the legal right to break a Publishing contract for Cause or to license the rights to multiple Publishers at the same time (most often this case involves publishing multiple translations).

Publication monopoly includes the moral right to First Disclosure for the duration of the author’s life, though this particular moral right descends to their inheritors (if any). 

### Profit

While a Work is under Copyright protection, the right of profit from that Work belongs entirely to the Holder of the Copyright. While Profit Sharing with publishers is a standard practice, most publishing contracts define the exact rate of the split for the work.

### Translation

While a work is under copyright protection, the right to authorize or permit translations to other languages or scripts belongs entirely to the Holder of the Copyright. This only affects translations made for distribution, and not to translations of mere segments for commentary or for personal usage.

### Adaptation

While a work is under copyright protection, the right to authorize or permit adaptations into other media belongs entirely to the Holder of the Copyright.

### Derivation

While a work is under copyright protection, the right to authorize or permit the creation of novel Works from it belongs entirely to the Holder of the Copyright.

#### Fan Works

Fan Works which do not infringe on the Profit right are generally seen as resting in a legal safe harbor. While it may be strictly outside of the law to produce and share such works, enforcement of Copyright against purely not-for-profit creations is rare.

### Transferability of Copyright

There is one exception for transferability of rights: inheritance in the event of death (or “mortal incapacitation”) for a sole Copyright Holder. This is the result of a legal case, Ellis vs Pact Law, in AA 318, when Parlaio Ellis attempted to leave his copyright to his son, Tamario, allowing him to manage the story for the remainder of its protected term. The nature of the clause caused the will to be brought to court, and concluded the following:

> While copyright is generally not transferable in other cases, we hold that it is in keeping with the intentions of the law to include such transference in the case of the mortal transfer of an estate, so long as it remains within the hands of a sole individual and natural person. Because the late Mr. Ellis did not work with partners to maintain the continuity of copyright, it should be inherited per his mortal wishes.
> 
> This ruling shall not be held to extend the duration of the copyright. The young Mr. Ellis is not the original producer of the work, but having inherited the copyright to the work, he shall be free to do with it anything the law permits any other Holder of copyright to do for the exact duration the copyright term.
> 
> This ruling shall not be held to make copyright a generally contractable right for transfer, nor shall it be held to conflict with the remaining copyright of collaborators in other cases. Had the late Mr. Ellis have had a collaborator who would still hold copyright, we should have ruled differently.
> 
> To be clear: This ruling shall allow the transfer of copyright only in the case of (a) sole copyright and (b) the death or mortal incapacitation of the sole Copyright Holder, and it shall do so only (c) through an inheritance process or will contract.

## Moral Rights and Copyright

Even after copyright expires, certain Moral Rights are still connected to works in the public domain, most notably the right of Attribution.

### Attribution

The author of a work maintains the right to be attributed as the author in perpetuity (even after death) _unless_ they choose to revoke it. This includes Attribution under an alias of their choosing.

This right does not extend to creative derivative works such as re-interpretations of the framework or archetypes used within a story, but does apply to merely transformative derivatives such as a simple translation into a new language, setting, or medium. Note that the translator or adaptor has the same right to attribution as the original author, so if a story by Baeron Lassalen is translated by Thÿrk Mangor, further adaptations of that translation must attribute both Baeron Lassalen and Thÿrk Mangor.

### Integrity

The right to **Personal Integrity**: Any real, existing person depicted in a Work may object to their depiction within the Work on the grounds that it is both false _and_ defamatory.

The right to **Creative Integrity:** In addition, the author of a work may, for their entire life, object to association with depictions of their work which distort or mutate their work in a manner that could, would, or has damaged their personal reputational honor, integrity, and reputation. While they cannot control the publication of such Works from Works that are no longer under copyright, they can still demand their attribution be removed from such works.

### First Disclosure

The author of a work maintains the right to decide _if and when_ a work is published, and therefore enters the protection of copyright. This right persists until the death or mortal incapacitation of the author, at which point it may be inherited by an estate or individual, or even assumed by the material owner of a unique work after its author’s death (such as letters or drafts sent to other people). Posthumous first disclosure is not subject to copyright protection.

### Withdrawal & Retraction

In the manner of Works protected by Copyright, the Holder has the moral right to withdraw from circulation or publication any Work they Hold the Copyright to. This right includes the right to reclaim physical expressions of the Work still held under a contract, and to buy back previously licensed rights and objects for any (or no) Cause.

## Circle Copyright

Because only natural persons are allowed to hold Copyright, circles are a tool that allows a group of artists to collaborate with a simple agreement: Each member of the circle who is involved in producing a work holds _full access_ to the Copyrights of that work. Circles are responsible for managing the identities of individuals involved in individual projects.

For an example, STAR Dandé (Team STAR or Team Star) is composed of four members, Laan Seraa “Seraa” Merolar, Talia Lanta, Achsmul Herlits, and Réliin Myoxe. Each member in that list has the full legal right to enforce the Copyright of the story _To the Stars!!_, independent of, but not against, the others. STAR Dandé is not an intermediate holder of the copyright, it is merely a form of partnership and the legal entity created to symbolize the joint work of its members.

If they wrote a new story without the involvement of Miss Lanta, then Miss Merolar, Mister Herlits, and Mister Myoxe would be the only members of the circle with a claim to the Copyright of that second story.

### Intra-Circle Conflicts

While rare, sometimes a Circle fails to have a consensus agreement on the use of their Copyright. While the first line of defense against copyright abuse by Circle members is internal mediation and documentation, if it falls to a court to resolve such a dispute, the records of the Circle may help determine which fractional elements of a Work the individuals disputing actually hold. For example, if an Illustrator wants to enforce the rights to their imagery _against_ one of the Writers, the court will request documentation on who contributed what to the project to ensure that only the specific images of that Illustrator are subject to the resolution of the case.

## What Can’t be Protected By Copyright?

Not all elements of a Work are necessarily subject to Copyright protection. Under Atrusian law, the following categories are not subject to copyright protection:

### Natural and Historical Facts

Natural facts such as the appearance of a plant, creature, or sapient species that exists independent of the Work are not subject to Copyright. This includes the likeness of individual persons, though there are Moral Rights which prohibit the depiction of an Individual Likeness for profit without their consent.

Historical facts of the world external to the Work are not subject to copyright. This means that the weather on a particular day, the location of a city or military site, or the recorded actions of an individual cannot be protected by copyright, though in the case of Individual actions, they may still be subject to certain Moral Rights.

While the documentation of facts in plain or simple language is not subject to copyright, the work that goes into the expressive documentation of such events is still a creative work subject to copyright. As a general rule, this prevents copyright claims on such media as undirected crystal footage, matter-of-fact transcriptions or descriptions of physical (including magical) actions, and similar such reports.

Note that reporting a speaker as saying specific words in a specific order may be a matter of natural or historical fact even if the words they expressed were composed in a manner that grants their performance _as a whole_ copyright protection (_ex tempore_ speech is generally not protected, however the prepared words of orators are, and due to their training). In the case of such conflicts, favor is given towards protecting the speaker in claims of infringement.

### Historical Documents

As noted above, posthumous publication of writings attributed to an author are not afforded protection by copyright, but part of the reason this specific rule exists due to a dispute between two biographers of the disgraced king Hau’on Rexga the First of the Kingdom of Ul’aux: One published more quickly and claimed copyright over the text of documents from the Palace of Kamelon in order to try to prevent his rival from publishing. The Pact Law courts found that because the documents were a matter of sufficient public interest to be included in a biography, even though they had not been published during the king’s lifetime they were not subject to copyright, and made it clear that this was not specifically true of kings, but of any person about whom a biography might be of interest: personal documents published posthumously are immediately part of the public domain.

### Mathematical, Scientific, and Thaumic Formulae

The fundamental structure of mathematical, scientific, and thaumic formulae are not subject to copyright as they can be independently derived from observation of nature. To balance this and preserve the incentive of novel research, there is a delicate line drawn between research documentation and established formulae or facts.

#### Spell Formulae

Spell Formulae are a form of exception that allows novel thaumic formulae to be protected by copyright, and must meet three specific legal criteria to qualify for protection: (1) the spell may not be a derivative or modification of a known and documented spell, (2) the spell must require material, verbal, or somatic components which are not obvious, and (3) the thaumic researcher claiming copyright must _file_ their research with a recognized thaumic guild for peer review, approval, and protection -- the spell must be published, even if only in the archives of said guild, for the duration of its copyright.

### “Mere Patterns”

A class of information known as “mere patterns” is exempted from Copyright. While the wording is deliberately vague, this classification has been found to include such elements are tropes, archetypes, chords, simple progressions of notes (generally 16 notes or fewer), procedural or random values, color palettes, and simple geometric shapes or concstructions. This list is not exhaustive, and may be amended in future cases if the need to elaborate other “mere patterns” should arise.

## Other Forms of Intellectual Property

While this document is focused on Copyright, it should not be taken to imply that there are no intellectual property patents for novel inventions or ideas. Many mechanical and magitech devices do have patents which protect them from being reproduced before a suitable term of protection has passed. The exact nature of such protections and term lengths is not currently in our research, except that all Intellectual Property on Atrusius is strictly tied to the possession of a Natural Person or, in some very specific cases, a State Entity (meaning it is held by a government.)
