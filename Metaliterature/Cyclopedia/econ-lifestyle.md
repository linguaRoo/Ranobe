# Atrusian Lifestyles

Broadly speaking, there are three standard classes of lifestyles: Urban, Rural, and Outlander. Within these, there are various economic classes based on how much one is able, and willing, to spend on living expenses.

It is important to note that the lifestyles described here use costs assuming generally independent living single adults. Statistically, this is not the majority of the population, especially over the course of a lifetime: most people do marry and eventually raise children, so this independent solo lifestyle is primarily only seen in younger wage workers, especially those who have moved away from their home town or village. Atrusian cultures do not generally consider it shameful to live with family as an adult.

## Urban Lifestyles

These lifestyles are defiend by living or very near the walled cities and towns that define the heart of Atrusian nations. Most nations only have one city, though the largest may support several smaller walled towns and most support a number of unwalled rural villages (based on how much territory they are able to keep secure). The lifestyles here specifically apply to the Cities and Towns, not smaller settlements.

### Poverty

Most nations try to avoid forcing their population into poverty. That said, some people fall through, or descend into, the economic cracks. There is no standard financial position or cost for those in poverty, as many of them beg, panhandle, or live criminal lifestyles. For those who are not criminals, they tend to spend all of their money on costs of living and basic luxuries -- usually food, alcohol, pleasures, and drugs (most often in that order). Those who choose to live in poverty are usually unwilling to work in the normal economy or engage in banking.

The impoverished usually live in cities and towns because urban centers have enough population and money flowing that they can support themselves outside of the formal economy. If they can’t find a place in a city or town, they tend to become outlander “Vagabonds” searching for another city or town.

### Low Income

#### Limited Workers

Limited Work lifestyles tend to be very cheap, often in crowded shared housing arrangements or cheap flophouses, and they usually prepare very calorically dense but simple meals in communal settings too, often with cheap and discounted ingredients. This class is generally more willing to work, but may include people who are unable to work full-time and either refuse, or are not offered, support from their family. 

Low-class living is often in the oldest and most densely developed parts of cities, and often comes with the risks of ill-maintained infrastructure including their courtyards. They are also often in closer proximity to where the impoverished choose to live, so they are at higher risk of being the earliest victims of local monster or dungeon appearances. In some cases, they live in fully subsidized public housing, in others it is heavily discounted based on the income they are able to demonstrate. The fiscal cost of living at this level is usually no more than half the cost of living at a standard level, and this may involve neglecting certain common expectations of living in normal society.

#### Spartan/Minimalist

The spartan or minimalist lifestyle is much more of a conscious choice than the Limited Work lifestyle, and is sometimes chosen for the sake of saving up for a future: they work to earn their keep and contribute to society, but they may live in a lower cost neighborhood or in public housing, and will actively choose to put in more time and effort to lower their fiscal investments.

They may eat just as well as their economic peers, but rather than buying fresh bread, street food, or going to the tavern, they prepare their food for themselves. They may also set aside space in their home to create their own still rooms, doing the necessary chemical and alchemical work to make their own hygienic supplies, preserves, syrups, and simple medicines. Spartan and minimalist individuals will often choose to invest in long-term equipment over the convenience of pre-made usables. While there is not a specific cost of living for this class, they will do everything they can to reduce their cost of living without explicitly causing themselves harm.
