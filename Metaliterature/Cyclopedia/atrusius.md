# Atrusius

Atrusius is a speculative fantasy setting with utopian leanings, though it is not explicitly built to be a utopia. It is a world where the interaction of a magical mana field with living energy fields (collectively called “plasma”) allows for things that are not necessarily strictly in line with real-world physics, though outside of these interactions most of real-world physics is maintained.

Atrusius is the fourth and final rocky planet in its system, orbiting the star Adtresk (in some formal stellar references, Atrusius is referred to as Adtresk IV or Adtresk 4), and the name Atrusius is ultimately adapted from the Orkish Unified Language’s name for the world, _Adtredzias_ meaning “land of three moons.”

## Adin

The Adin are a species of humans from Gaea (the in-setting version of our real world) who achieved magitech superiority in the distant past. They were the inhabitants of Atlantis, and were taken as Gods by various other Gaean cultures. While most of them left Gaea before the rise of Rome, the final two groups of Emissaries (comparable to the Greek and Norse pantheons) left Gaea sometimes in the late 1st to early 2nd millenium CE, traveling on a calculated course for a planet roughly 110 light years away, which is now known as Atrusius.

### Adin Arrival

The Adin originally began to settle a colony on the moon _Gurion_, which they named for being the base of their empire within this star system. They used their generational colony ships and this base to observe the native species on the planetary surface, and noting the similarities and differences between their forms, occasionally sent observers to the surface following strict health and safety protocols. They used this experience to identify what they originally thoughts of as the largest political powers on the world: a selection of about 40 Kenyu, Gramurn, and Hol’tiin nations scattered across the surface.

After initially presenting their proposal for a peaceful colonization (in short: the Adin requested the right to develop lands adjacent to existing states, in exchange for economic returns to those neighbor states from their development), the Adin were also introduced to several Peratan and Semeric nations, and were made aware that the Elressie who had come with them wished to colonize their own nations. Over a number of years, the inclusion of Orkish states was also negotiated, as were a number of other resolutions.

### The First and Second Rules

The Adin have a broad First Rule of contact with other civilizations: They do not introduce technology or cultural ideas to other societies which those societies have not developed. However once a culture develops a technology initially, the Adin are willing to help further development or spread of it.

The Second Rule is more of a response to their own past: Having witnessed the exhaustion of the manafield of Gaea before they left, the Adin have resolved to try and preserve the manafield of any other planets they colonize. They seek to find the gentlest ways to use the manafield without leading to a burnout similar to that on Gaea around the turn of the second millenium CE.

> [!info] Gaean Manafield  
> While the Gaean Manafield was heavily exhausted when the last Adin “Emissaries” departed, it has been slowly recovering. While not directly relevant to Atrusius, Gaea at the turn of the 3rd Millenium CE is finding its manafield developed enough that magic can be used, and dungeons can manifest—allowing _some_ real-world creepypasta style stories to be real events on Gaea.

## The Pact of Eo

The Pact of Eo is essentially the United Nations of Atrusius. It is an international economic and political treaty which seeks to prevent large-scale wars between its member states, and establishes common defense agreements. Every sixteen years, the Pact is renegotiated, updated, and renewed, and the host city for pact meetings rotates across the world periodically after renewals (it does not always change after every renewal).

### Sporting Resolution

The nations of the Pact of Eo have all agreed to use sporting resolution to resolve international disputes before allowing them to escalate to warfare. While this may take any sporting form, the most common is what is called the Arena of Knights, in which professional, highly-trained champions—often called Knights—representing the various nations fight in simulated sporting battles to capture an objective.

To keep the Knights in good form, many exhibition matches are also performed, often involving teams of varying compositions to foster greater international collaboration and to help reduce the amount of bad blood between Knights. It is widely believed that by showing that the Knights can coöperate despite their species and national differences, it helps build the peaceful relations at all levels.

Since the year 260 (base-12; 360 in base 10): Most Skirmishes are recorded on magishow crystals and distributed to major venues so that the world can see them. While the cost of Skirmish crystals is high enough to generally keep individuals from simply purchasing one, they can often be watched in taverns, sporting halls, and dedicated magishow parlors.

## Setting Guide

The setting of Atrusius is a fantastical take on modern society, however there are elements of modern life that simply do not exist within Atrusius. These include, but are not limited to:

- **Motor Vehicles:** While traditional and magitech motors are physically possible, they are currently only likely to be found in very specific regions of the world, particularly areas which also interact with spacecraft.
- **International Broadcasts:** The manafield of Atrusius interferes with traditional electromagnetic broadcast signals. While it is possible to build specific countermeasures or highly specialized magical broadcasting methods, the technology for broadcasting radio or television is generally limited to a very local region.
