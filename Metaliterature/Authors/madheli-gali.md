Gali Madheli is a prominent artist who has entered what she calls “semi-retirement.” Her last major international story was _Odanoicolhom_ or _Sins of Odan_, a romantic drama set between a Farlanna and Ményùwa couple of women who discovered they had both been romanced by the same man, Odan. After Odan’s disappearance at sea, the two women connect and develop a deep and intimate friendship. The eight-year, 300 chapter epic depicts the two of them growing close over a period of nearly 200 years, and ends with them swearing their eternal love to each other and clasping their hands together through their long hair -- a deeply romantic sapphic gesture in both cultures.

She sits on the editorial review board for the Everybody Read and Girls’ Love Life families of Magazines, and founded the contemporary Kenyu Publishing House _Leaves of the Leng’eni_, and its initial self-named magazine. She is a devoted preservationist of Kenyu Culture from any nation, and founded her publishing house to specialize in the printing of Kenyu-language stories.

### _Odanoicolhom_

The story begins with a male kenyu sailor departing from his home and wishing his wife well. At the bottom of the third page, we discover that he is addressing two different women in two different rooms—and he does not name his wife even in the most intimate expression of love.

The scene jumps forward by five years. Using a parallel structure on facing pages, Madheli tells the story of how each woman learns that her husband, Odan, has been declared among the dead after the wreckage of his ship was found. We learn that without a doubt, they were both married to the same man, but each of the women has a drastically different life from the other.

Riëlonna is an upper middle class artist, producing beautiful paintings in tempera, oils, and watercolors, supplemented with fabric or natural materials to bring life and texture to her work. She gracefully accepts the death compensation from the trading company for her husband’s death, and enters a phase of mourning.

Luxaddira is a working middle class carpenter, recently made the head of a workshop under her professional Master, and goes cold when she thinks of Odan. Though she accepts the death compensation, she is firm and distant. She buries her pain in designs and work within her workshop, mourning on the inside.

After two years, the women meet at the “Lost at Sea” memorial for the shipping company and begin to talk about their lost husbands. They agree to visit a meadhouse to share their stories after learning that both of their husbands left on the same night, and no doubt on the same ship. They raise eyebrows as they discuss their lost husbands, until Riëlonna notes a very particular birthmark on her husband. Luxaddira drops her glas to the floor and stares at Riëlonna—“You have just described my husband, Odan.” Riëlonna does not drop her glass, but her jaw hangs slightly loose as she says, “You have just named my husband.”

The next day they approach the shipping company together. They learn that there was, indeed, only one Odan in their employ, and conclude that Odan has played them both for fools. Yet in their misery about being fooled, they find connection to each other, a shared foolishness neither will let go of, and so they agree that they should be friends and let no man fool them so again.

Over years and decades they grow intimately close, eventually Luxaddira even moves into Riëlonna’s home, where they maintain separate bedrooms but shared workspaces for their arts. They are strongly indicated to have become lovers at this point, though it is never said plainly or obliquely in the main text.

By the end of the series, 200 years have passed since the first page, and both women are beginning to show the signs of middle age. As the series ends, they swear that their foolishness in Odan brought them great wisdom, and that the love they have nurtured together over so many years will outlive their bodies. Each placing their hands in their own hair, they reach out, embrace and clasp hands, their cheeks touching, as they swear to love each other forever.
