# Ellis, Parlaio

> [!info] Author Profile
> 
> - **Species:** Kenyu
> - **Sex & Gender:** Male, Man
> - **Pronouns:** _ji_ (he/him)
> - **Age:** Deceased at 248 (A.A. 317)
> - **Nationality:** Farnell
> - **Passions:** Storytelling, Illustration, Celvark
> - **Dispassions:** Sharing, Medication, Losing
> - **Personality Type:** Furak (Lemon; bright and cheerful with a sour flavor)
> - **Affiliations:** _Meten’twanu Miktan_ (Mighty Comic Books)

Parlaio Ellis was a noble Lord of Farnell County and a notable pulp folio author and illustrator during the Golden Age of Atrusian meten publishing. His longest lasting legacy on the culture of Atrusius is his impact on Atrusian copyright law, followed by being the First Author of _Bal Keldin_. Due to his litigious nature, fans became superstitious about saying or writing his name when not strictly necessary, and so established the practice of referring to Bal Keldin authors by numerical epithets, such as Baeron Lassalen, the Fourth Author, or Jerin Romaren, the Fifth Author.

Ellis worked exclusively with Meten’twanu Miktan as one of the initial investors in the Farnell-based House. He also regularly disputed and challenged pact law on copyright, arguing at various times that guilds and houses should be allowed to hold copyright, that copyright terms should be longer for the longer lived species, that they should be universally longer, and that that they should be transferable by contract. While most of his arguments were ruled against, his last will and testament attempted to pass his remaining copyright on to his son, Tamario. Tamario’s case honoring the will, effectively Parlaio’s last case, was narrowly decided in favor of allowing sole copyright holders to inherit copyrights exactly as they stand at the time of death.

### Farnell Nights

This series follows the life of a lower class Kenyu _nashix_ living in Farnell, a romantic burglar who seeks challenges and steals only from other thieves. During the run of the series, he forms a found family of fellow noble thieves who help him steal from bigger and bigger targets, but in the finale, the gang is all caught by the Adin Investigator who has been pursuing them -- only for their cell to be empty in the final panel.

### Baerelon the Strider

A series based on the exploits of a real Kenyu Farstrider and Knight named Baerelon. The series ran for two years and ended with Baerelon abandoning his position as a Knight after the disappearance of his Adin lover, Kitasha Lorebach.

Ellis reportedly contracted the rights to base the story on Baerelon’s private journals, and was forced to stop because the journals ended and he couldn’t bring himself to falsify historical stories to continue it.

### kuCelvark Tanu

In this story, Ellis tries to imagine what life was like for Celvark. He based the design on images found on the walls of Celvark ruins, interpreting them as a lithe, butterfly-like sapient species who could not understand the language or minds of the other people on the planet, but had the best intentions. He interpreted their assimilatory nature as something they considered a gift that would bring peace and understanding to everyone, if they had only not beed chased off by the military alliance of 5 millenia ago.

The story sold moderately well, but was far less successful that Ellis had hoped.

### Black-Card

A story about a Gramurn _nashix_ gambler who brings high stakes to everything in his life. When he discovers a set of magical playing cards, he breaks a losing streak, but discovers that the bigger he wins, the harder the world tries to make him lose. As the stakes rise higher and higher, he keeps trying to get rid of the deck, but discovers it’s cursed. In the finale, he ends up gambling with the Kenyu God of Death, and winning his freedom from the cursed Black-Card deck.

### Blackstar

An adolescent Kenyu wakes up and discovers his skin is turning dark grey and hardening. When he gets attacked by a bully between classes, he also learns that his body is growing hot enough to hurt others when he gets angry. Soon, he discovers that his body is now channeling the power of a star, and the hardened grey skin is his body adapting to keep it in by becoming dense coal -- but he can release it through his eyes, hands and feet. He becomes the superhero Pacirashi (which translates to Blackstar) and fights monsters and criminals, and even the supervillain Anesh Arzol (Blue Lightning).

The series was canceled after only 17 issues due to the death of the author, but Meten’twanu Miktan has published several successive iterations of the character concept starting in 334 until 338 (48 issues), and again starting in 352 until 364 (achieving a full 144 issue run).

## Legal Cases

Parlaio Ellis frequently challenged pact law on the subject of copyright. He argued that copyright protections should be longer, possibly scaling with the expected lifespan of the copyright holder, and that if it is a personal property, it should be transferable like any other, and that guilds and publishing houses should be allowed to hold it. All of these arguments were shot down every time they were brought to court, but he was very persistent. In the end, he did win one meaningful change to copyright law in the case of Ellis v. Pact Law (318), though that case was technically his son arguing over the validity of Parlaio’s last will.

Parlaio also had a tendency to use the threat of civil action in protection of his copyright and moral rights as a weapon against other creators whom he saw as threatening his rights. It is possible that this is among the reasons new creators in the Bal Keldin mythos have taken such significant steps in varying the appearance, genre, and setting of the cast in _Knight of Destiny_, _War of Ages_, _Magical Doppelganger_, etc.
