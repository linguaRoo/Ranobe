# Digimon Adventure Two•Five

_Previously on Digimon:_

_V-mon heard a scream from the bottom of a hill and rushed to help save Floramon, only to find her being attacked by the Ultimate level Dryadmon. Calling Daisuke for help, he received it as, for the first time, their experiment in the Digimental Cross succeeded, evolving V-mon into the Cross Armor form of Stormdramon._

_After saving Floramon, he told her that he was having strange memories from before the time he got sealed up under the Digimental of Courage, and showed her Rift Valley. After he said that he thought it might be impossible for the Digital World to have a prolonged peace, well, just read on to find out!_

## Episode 1<br>Dark Clouds… Awakening of Stormdramon!

“… Maybe there isn’t meant to be peace forever, in which case we need to cherish what we get of it. I think I was remembering things before you screamed, and they were frightening. I used to be a warrior, a vicious one. I think part of that woke up today when I evolved to Stormdramon.”

“A beautiful and impressive form,” she said, smiling at him. “But I was scared… during our peace, you should not fight the way you did just now, because even Dryadmon wasn’t threatening my life, non?”

“You’re right… I can’t explain it, but it’s like I was here before… when I was the vicious warrior, before I was sealed under the Digimental. I want to remember this place as a good one, not a bad memory of the past. I want us to always remember this as the place where I knew it… Floramon, I love you, and I want to love you even more with every minute we spend together.” He sighed, feeling a nervous knot building in his stomach. “Floramon, will you be my mate?”

“I think so,” she said, wrapping her arms around him. “I do believe I will, V-mon.”

From the shadows behind them, a figure slowly departed, taking no interest or concern in the kiss they shared. Her mission was only to find the ancient warriors, those who had been sealed under the Digimentals. Her master was not one to tolerate failures, but Viral Digimon were not rare in showing that trait. Her own black scales allowed her to hide in the shadows, and her sharp ears and eyes were an asset to every aspect of her hunt. She smiled, knowing she now had one of the ancient warriors located, and could report his presence to her master.

Without warning, she found him appearing in front of her. He was a much more powerful Digimon, a dark skinned humanoid type, and he looked down at her. “You have found the dragon warrior, the one of the storms?”

“Yyes, master,” she said, a low hiss in her voice. “He was atop this very hill but moments ago, looking down towards Rift Canyon. I was going to seek out the other two before I reported to you, but it is a great load from me to know that you know his location.”

“Your simpering is not pleasant to hear,” he said, lifting her up. “If I wanted the words of a snake in my ears at all times, I would have simply found a reptile Digimon who was willing. I chose you for a purpose, and I will have you fulfill it. Go seek out the warriors of the earth and the winds, and call for me when you have found them.”

“Stormdramon,” Daisuke said, his eyes widening. “I wish I could have been here for you, buddy. It almost sounds like the old days.”

“No, it wasn’t,” V-mon said, shaking his head. “I think there’s something new happening, something bad, if the Digimental Cross actually worked. And my memories… I remember that I used to be a warrior for someone, and there was a village here.”

“But that can’t be,” Daisuke said, looking around. “There’s no signs of a village ever being here.”

“You don’t know how long I was sealed under that Digimental,” was all the reply that came. “I think I might have been the one who… who destroyed the village here. I need to know more about the past, about who I was and why I was sealed under the Digimental. And I think Hawkmon and Armadimon should come with me while I try to find out, because I think we were all in it together.”

“I guess… that could make some sense,” Daisuke said, looking down where the village had supposedly been. He shook his head and leaned back. “V-mon, you can’t evolve without the Digivice… and it sounds to me like this isn’t a problem we humans are going to be helpful in. Do you think you could Cross Evolve on your own?”

“I might be able to,” he said, walking over to one of the trees. “No, I’m sure that if I need to, I can do it. And if Miyako and Iori give their Digivices and D-Terminals to Hawkmon and Armadimon, we might be able to get them to use it too… without jogress, we need a way to power up.”

“But what about…” He trailed off, knowing V-mon had the same idea as he did. “You know I don’t like it, but if Tailmon can’t use her armor, and Hikari gave it to Takeru… maybe Patamon should go along, too. Don’t you think?”

“Actually, I agree. I don’t want to say why exactly, but I know that Patamon needs to come with us to wherever we’re going, and Tailmon doesn’t.” He walked downhill a way, and then motioned over the horizon. “When we killed, no, obliterated, BelialVandemon, I hoped that we could have an eternal peace here, thanks to Mr. Oikawa’s sacrifice. But that peace is in danger, or else I couldn’t have cross evolved. Beyond that, I don’t think you’ll be a help this time, so it’s a Digimon-only quest.”

“Then tomorrow we need to talk with Miyako and Iori,” Daisuke said, nodding. The darkness was coming quickly, the stars of the Digital world both alien in their beauty and familiar to his eyes.

The children had agreed, and Takeru had reluctantly agreed that if they all felt it best, he would support it. They were leaving the Digital World for what seemed to be the last time, until their partners had finally finished with their soul-searching quest.

As the humans faded and the digital gate closed behind them, V-mon looked around. “I think we need to find Gennai,” he said. “And I think that Patamon should be our leader. He knows more about Gennai than the rest of us put together.”

And so they set out, Patamon leading the four Digimon towards what had been Gennai’s “home” during their first adventure, northwards on Server until they reached the Vira Mountains, home of some of the most aggressive Digimon short of evil in those days, and little different now. A Fuugamon barred their path, the red-skinned oni-like Digimon brandishing his club.

“So a little group of vaccines decided to come all this way just to get beat up, hmm?” He laughed deep in his throat as he looked over them, clearly trying to decide which one to attack first.

Patamon didn’t wait for the others to react. “I don’t think it would be a smart idea to attack my buddy V-mon, he’s always ready for a fight… and if you attack him we’ll attack you in return, and it would just be a whole world of hurt for a big ogre like you. I don’t think the four of us are even worth your effort.”

“Oh is that so,” Fuugamon asked, gripping his club and turning to Patamon.

“Well I mean let’s be honest, you’d win much too easily and all you’d get out of it is the knowledge that you can beat four children up. Where’s the challenge, Fuugamon? Now we have some friends who are adults like you, and if we don’t go back soon, they’ll be coming to find out why. And there are eight of them, just waiting to attack anybody who messes with us.”

“Eight adults, hmm?” Fuugamon grinned and turned. “So I just go due south and I’ll find these eight adults you’re talking about?”

“No, no… south and a bit east, see where the rock spire is over there? Yeah, that’s where they’re waiting for us.”

“Well, I guess you’re telling the truth… after all, you little guys are Vaccines, hmm? I’ll just go take on those eight adults first, then I’ll come back and delete you later!” He headed off down the path, and V-mon blinked in shock.

“Patamon, you could’ve gotten us killed!”

“Nope, I knew what I was doing. I hear people doing it all the time, and it won’t hurt anyone, unless there really are eight adult digimon back by that spire.” He laughed and started moving onwards. “Well, aren’t we going to move on so we can find Gennai?”

They eventually made it, though several of the Viral Digimon had not fallen for Patamon’s little stories and they had needed to evolve. Patamon and Armadimon didn’t want to try a jogress either, since they felt it might be too difficult without the help of their partners, so four Adults had ended up taking on WaruMonzaemon, and just barely winning. They’d ended up forcing a de-evolution, but it was enough.

Now they had finally reached the old house that Gennai had used, now floating several meters above the surface of the lake. “Well, if you’re here, you’ve begun to learn about the past. Yes, all four of you have an important role in the past of the Digital World, and so do several of the others. But this is your story, not theirs.” Gennai was looking over a chipped cup full of tea, not even looking at them. “No, I’m not just a hologram. I have been worrying about this for several months, and I’m honestly so worried I haven’t touched my tea yet.”

“So we do have a past? This isn’t just one of V-mon’s crazy daydreams?” Hawkmon was staring at Gennai, his eyes wide with shock. “I mean… Well, yes. There was something before the Digimentals?”

“Well, it doesn’t surprise me,” Armadimon said, walking over to the table. “But y’know, I am kind of hungry, so if we could have a bite before you tell the story I’d really appreciate that.” He let out a huge yawn just as he finished and chuckled. “Maybe a short nap too.”

Gennai nodded, and rose. He went to the oven he had used to prepare fish the last time he had entertained guests, and brought over tea and fish. “Not all Digimon sealed under Digimentals were like you… some were sealed by… a sort of mistake. However the three of you were quite intentionally placed there, by various people.”

The Digimon began eating, Patamon not asking about his involvement, sure it would be explained at the right time.

In the youth of the Digital World, villages had sprung up, even before the end of the prehistoric ages. When Gennai was not the only Guardian Program designed in the form of a man, they had helped the peaceful Digimon build villages that were defended against the aggressive ones. But there was a strange sort of balance in it, as each village that did fall ended up with one survivor, who dedicated themselves to become a great warrior to avenge their village.

Directory Town had been the largest, and eventually three of the warriors who had sworn to avenge their towns settled in there, feeling their quest accomplished. They had attained great amounts of power, but something blocked their evolution until one day, when something even Gennai could not predict had attacked Directory Town.

It had been called Ashemon, and was a mere Adult Digimon. A humanoid Digimon with ashy black skin, a powerful body, and a power that exceeded his level. He had not attacked the town immediately. His first move was a warning, a strike that split the very earth north and west of the town. Some even said the strike tore a hole between worlds, but none could prove it. Then he attacked the town.

A single warrior had fled to the south, a bold one who had found love within the town, and after placing his lover safely at the edge of the forest, he ran back into town. She witnessed his battle against Ashemon, but Ashemon had servants. They had attacked her, and the warrior had turned, running to save her. All he found of her was a pendant he had found on his journeys and given to her as a gift.

“Rift Canyon… is that really how it was formed?” V-mon was staring at Gennai. “And… I was there, I did lose someone. Who was she, Gennai? What kind of Digimon was she?”

“Calm down,” Armadimon said, listening intently. “Three warriors, huh? So I’d guess all three of us, right Gennai?”

“Exactly correct,” Gennai said, sipping his tea as he looked at V-mon. “Your lover was another dragon Digimon, one of similar form to you, but a different form. Very few of those similar to yourself survived the great purging, and you did only because of the Digimental that you were sealed under. It is also important that you know that in the past, you were not V-mon, that name came from the Digimental. Indeed, all three of you were reconfigured under the Digimentals, into what you are now.”

As Gennai took another sip of his tea, a holographic field surrounded them, showing them almost firsthand the events following Ashemon’s attack on Directory Town. “Ths is the forest where Ahimon searched high and low for his beloved,” Gennai said. A Digimon similar to V-mon, but taller and tougher looking was holding a pendant, and they could all see the pain in his eyes as he clutched it tightly.

The scream he let out was one of the ultimate in anguish, the name of his lover who was now lost, forever. “Vivamon!”

“Viva… Vivamon?” V-mon tried to remember, but he knew Hawkmon was closing his eyes, as if to pray for his comfort. “I don’t remember ever hearing anything about a Digimon by that name, Gennai.”

“You wouldn’t,” he said. “The Digimental had several purposes, but they will be explained as the story goes on. Hush now, and we can discuss later.”

Ahimon had been found that night by the other warriors, Falcomon and Armamon, who did resemble their modern counterparts in ways. In Directory Town they had become friends, and they were now there for their friend. “There has to be a way,” Ahimon said, punching the ground. “There has to be a way for us to evolve, so we can fight Ashemon on equal terms. For taking my love, he will forever know my hate.”

“Calm down,” Falcomon said, placing a feathered hand on his friend’s shoulder. “Anger won’t help us, not tonight. We have survived, and perhaps so has Vivamon. Perhaps she was not deleted.”

“Yeah,” Armamon drawled, butting his head against some trees. Aside from the seemingly thicker plates along his back and a pair of horns on his head, he seemed the least changed from the modern counterpart. As a bunch of fruit fell down, he walked back over to his friends. “First thing we need is to eat, then we can talk about revenge. Yup, I think we all want revenge after today.”

They ate the fruits and then fell asleep. When morning came, they awoke, and Ahimon led them in planning for revenge. “Ashemon isn’t like any other Digimon, he’s stronger, somehow. We need a way to evolve, and maybe we’ll be like him. I mean, we’ve all handled adult Digimon even though we’re only child forms, right?”

“Well, I hate to brag, but I once defeated a trio of Monochromon without any aid at all,” Falcomon said, nodding. “We’re not normal child-level Digimon, but how do you know that if we evolve we won’t be normal adult-levels?”

“I don’t, but I think that we will,” was the reply, a grim tone of voice setting the mood. “Ashemon headed west after he attacked, so we need to head west to find him. But without the power to stand up to him…”

“This was a boring conversation,” Patamon said, looking at V-mon. “Gennai, can’t we skip this and find some action?”

“Well, I don’t see why not, although Falcomon and Ahimon might benefit from understanding their past selves. Armamon, it seems, had little change to his personality under the Digimental.”

“Hey, you can’t change the classics,” Armadimon said, letting out a deep yawn.

“No, I agree with Patamon,” V-mon said, looking at the discussion of revenge. “I want to see when we get the power to evolve… When I became a vicious warrior, not just a mighty one. I remember I wasn’t just strong.”

The show paused and changed to a late night, with a figure in a white robe. The three warriors were watching as a box was opened, revealing three pendants. “I am not supposed to be giving these to you,” he said, giving one to each of the warriors before him. “If Gennai learns of this, he will be very angry. But I believe you three must be the ones to defeat Ashemon, and even if Gennai is displeased, I will help you as much as I can.”

“Thank you sir Gemma,” Ahimon said, looking at the pendant. “What are these?”

“They embody the forces of the Digital world, they are the pendants of Heart, of Soul, and of Mind. They will give you the power to evolve not only to the adult level, but beyond it to the perfect form, the highest level of power in the world. If Ashemon continues to acquire power, we will certainly need three champions capable of attaining the perfect level. Bear in mind that these evolutions are permanent, and you will never be able to reverse them, so save the power until you truly need it.”

The three examined their pendants, taking special notice of the Core Diamonds that had been placed within them to provide power. “Ahimon, you will evolve into the warrior of Storms through the Pendant of Heart, with the powers of lightning and fire at your disposal. I cannot say what you will look like, but you will represent the fury, the unforgiving wrath, of a storm.”

Falcomon slipped his on, the feel of ChromeDigizoid and core diamonds cold against his down feathered chest, and sighed. “Falcomon, you shall be warrior of wind, with grass and the winds in your power, through the pendant of Soul. Be cautious of the power, for the wind can be gentle and caring, yet it can become a hurricane which destroys with no care for what it touches.”

Armamon managed to flip the chain over his head and then let the pendant hang, looking up to the robed figure, Gemma, for advice. “The earth and the seas shall be your power, Armamon, gentle and vicious, violent and peaceful, only with your strong mind can you control these forces warrior of Earth. To the west, and quickly, you three, for Ashemon’s power grows by the moment.”

The three knelt before him for only a moment, saying their thanks before they left. As they did so, Gemma turned away, and headed off to the forest where the guardians lived, from which the three were now watching the defeated Guardian heading back.

He was not greeted warmly when he arrived either. Gennai held a final pendant, and Gemma knew he had been found out. The last pendant was the most powerful, but only Gennai knew where it was hidden. If he had retrieved it, he had also decided the time to use it, and undoubtedly the others, was at hand.

“Gemma, you have given the pendants to the warriors,” Gennai said. “Without the permission of the elders or myself, you have taken the pendants and given them to those you felt deserved them.” The cold stare that followed was enough to hurt even the viewers in Gennai’s home. “As guardians, we do not act upon what we feel, and for doing so there is only one punishment fit for this occasion.

“Though you indeed delivered the pendants where they were meant to go, you have done so prematurely, and you will be stripped of your rank as a Guardian. Furthermore, you shall live as a Digimon from now on, a new Digimon who will be to aid the three in their quest.”

“The elders have sentenced me,” Gemma said, looking up at the pendant. “And I shall accept the punishment. I only beg that I will be of further aid to this world I acted only to protect, should further times arise.”

V-mon stared at Patamon. In fact, all three of them started at Patamon, who had said the line exactly as Gemma had, though several octaves higher. He felt accused, and looked around. “What? What is it, guys?”

Gennai nodded. “Patamon, do you now understand why you are here? Why you must be here, now that the Digimental Cross has been awakened?”

“II think… You mean that II used to be a Guardian? Like Gennai?” His eyes were widening as he realized what had happened. “And… that last pendant, it’s me? What is it?”

“The Pendant of Light, symbolizing Hope for the future and the Light of digital life… After Ashemon was defeated, the pendants were reforged into the Digimentals you are familiar with, and you were sealed under them in order for reconfiguration to occur. This restored you to normal power levels and child levels.

“Well, aside from you, Patamon. You were needed before the reconfiguration could be completed, and so I called your code from the Digimental and formed it into a Digiegg. All but a fraction of your power was saved for you to become who you are now. Just watch.”

As the Pendant was placed around Gemma’s neck he shrank into a child-like humanoid figure with wavy blond hair and a silk robe covering his body. He had six pairs of wings, five on his back and the last atop his head, and his eyes were azure. At each of his wrists and ankles was a metal band, a pronouncement of his cursed eternity, and along the left side of his chest and his left legs was a prophecy written in the digital glyphs, which said only this: “In time all peace shall be restored.”

“You are now Lucemon, bearer of the Pendant of Light,” Gennai said. “Seek out your fellow warriors, and aid them. All of your power and theirs will be necessary to defeat Ashemon once he has evolved.” Lucemon nodded, leaving the forest immediately.

Again Gennai sent things forward, pausing as three warriors stood before him. “I know your memories will return to you over time, and you will remember clearly the battle against Ashemon, and how he swore to return as long as the illusion of peace hung over this world. Undoubtedly he has now returned, which is why the Digimental Cross is working.”

Gennai paused. “When I told your partners of the Digimental Cross, I had hoped that the awakening of the ancient Pendants would never be necessary, but it seems that time has indeed come. You are about to see the destruction of those pendants and the formation of your Digimentals from their remains, a task which only I was able to perform. I realized that in spite of my hopes, there might be a time when the power of the pendants would be necessary, and so I gave the Digimental Armors the ability to fuse, and to create a force nearly as powerful as you were when you defeated Ashemon.”

“You exceeded the power level of any known Perfect level Digimon,” Gennai said, looking at the three armored Digimon in front of him. “Storm Ahimon, Tempestmon, and Galapagomon… your names will be remembered by the elders for all of time, even if the rest of the world should forget them. For defeating Ashemon, we wish to reward you, but what is within our power that you do not have?”

Storm Ahimon, a knight in brilliant blue armor with silvers bolts and golden flames etched along it knelt before him. “I have only two wishes, and one is surely beyond your power,” he said. “Sir Gennai, I wish to have my beloved Vivamon brought back into this new world of peace we have formed.”

Gennai looked upwards, consulting with some unseen entity. “We cannot grant that wish, however we can give you a world of peace in which you may have a peaceful form. But only if all of you will accept it, you three who shall live in legends.”

Tempestmon spoke next, his voice deep, matured beyond even what Hawkmon could imagine himself sounding like, but the red feathers cresting his head and the uniquely avian sort of armor marked Tempestmon as himself in the past. “Sir Gennai, I do not wish to be remembered for a war, whether as the cause of it’s origin or of its conclusion. I would as soon see myself deleted as to live on as naught but a symbol of war. If you can offer me a life where I am peaceful, then I shall accept it without delay.”

“As long as I don’t have to do much work, you can count me in,” Galapagomon said, his drawl a clear mark of who he had become. He let out a yawn easily his equal in size before continuing. “And a chance to forget all of this war stuff too… I don’t want to remember this.”

“Nobody would,” Gennai said. “If you will give me your pendants, I will be able to re-forge them into the tools we will need to grant this. Ahimon, do you agree as well?”

Ahimon took the pendant from his neck and nodded. “Without Vivamon, it is not worth staying in this world recovering from war. Perhaps in the peaceful world and life you offer us, I might find another love, but not in this one.”

Time skipped, certainly not more than a week, but it was obvious, because immediately they saw Gennai holding six familiar objects, each on its own sash. He first took the Digimentals of Courage and Friendship, and held them out to Ahimon. “Storm Ahimon, when you touch these, your power will be drained, returning you to the state you were before the pendant allowed you to evolve,” he said. “You shall also be locked in a stasis while you are reformatted for your peaceful life. But so that none will forget your deeds, I promise you a name to reflect what you achieved. For being the one who brought us victory over Ashemon, you shall be Victorymon.”

“No, that name is horrible,” he said, shaking his head. “It’s long and would remind me of war. I do not wish to remember this war.”

“Then V-mon, though it shall still be in memory of your victory.” Gennai bowed in apology, and set the Digimentals on the ground.

“V-mon… that is a name I believe I could like…” Storm Ahimon said, kneeling and placing one hand upon each of the Digimentals.

“Tempestmon, these will hold your power, and you also shall be sealed. Though you will be changed in form, your attributes will not, and you will remain free to fly in your future life, over the ever-peaceful plains and forests of this world as it shall be.”

“Of course,” he said, kneeling before the guardian and placing his hands upon the Digimentals.
