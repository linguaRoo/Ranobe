# Meta-X: Summer Daze

---

## Prologue

Nathaniel Knight, a 12 year old just out of elementary school, has a secret. He is one of the superpowered people known as Metahuman, and as puberty hits, his powers are Bio-Emerging. Discovering that his best female friend and the new transfer student are also metahumans, he convinces them to work with him, against local people who might either use metahuman abilities for evil, or those who might claim it.

Managing to save their hometown of Grand Rapids from the threat of destruction by Guise, an Epsilon Level transmorph, Nathaniel, as Helios, readily turns the other youngster, now revealed to be his old classmate Charles VanDyke, over the black clothed men who claim his custody. But putting Meta-X in the view of the government may not have been an especially wise choice.

As summer draws near once again, Livwyer has struck again, claiming that he will have vengeance for Guise. And with the Metahuman Registration Act coming before both Congress and the United Nations, Nathaniel is contacted by a Japanese youth named Jenrya. As events are set in motion, Livwyer may not be the only threat for Meta-X this summer.

# Meta-X: Year Two “Summer Daze”

## 16 May, 2002

Dear Diary,

Most of the school year has been uneventful, but now Jason’s struck again. And I’m sworn to secrecy about his powers, so I can’t afford revealing him to Nathaniel. I think, though, the wierdest thing in my life just now is the nightmares I’ve been having for the past week.

It always starts out the same way, pitch black, illuminated by a bolt of lightning. Guise is back, and strangling Jason, but Jason is wearing one of our Meta-X uniforms. And then, Jason turns into Nathaniel, his body literally steaming as rain begins falling from the sky. It changes after that, sometimes he manages to send off a heat wave and knock Guise away, but then Jason jumps on his back and sends too much electricity through him. No matter how I try, I can never change anything. If I keep Jason from getting to Nathaniel, he sends a bolt of electricity, giving Guise the time to get back to him. If I try to warn him, it only delays the inevitable.

I hate to think about it, especially considering how much stock Nathaniel puts in legends and foretellings. I don’t dare tell him about it, but if I don’t, I’m afraid he’ll die as unaware of it as he does in the dream.

Today, however, was a good day. We’re having a spring dance next week Tuesday, and Melvin asked me to go. Considering that I don’t want Nathaniel to think to much of me and him, I decided to accept. I hope it wasn’t the wrong choice. But what should I wear?

@~!~ Julia ~!~@

## Episode 1 - Big Eyes, Small Hopes

Date: Friday, 17 May, 2002

“Nothing like spring in Michigan,” Nathaniel said, coming up behind Melvin. “So, how’s the gossip going these days?”

“After managing to quash the rumour about us being metahumans, you mean?” Melvin sighed, his purple hair looking just dyed enough to pass muster. “Well, there’s a rumour that some girl wants to ask you to the dance.” He hoped it was true, especially as introverted as Nathaniel had been during their seventh grade year. “Haven’t found out who, yet, but once I do, I’ll tell you first.”

“Drats,” Nathaniel said, carefully executing the turn. He was now rollerblading backwards, at roughly the same pace Melvin was walking. “She wouldn’t happen to be dying her hair pink, would she?”

“Actually, I asked her to the dance yesterday,” Melvin said, twitching his nose. “Look, I don’t have time to talk, I need to review those notes for algebra before the bus gets here. Still, anything new from Jenrya?”

“Yeah,” Nathaniel said, doing a full spin as he stopped. “You know, you really should get a pair of blades. Then we could go to school without waiting for a bus here.” He set himself on the ground, unbuckled his rollerblades, and took off his backpack. First, he pulled out a grayscale printout of three kids, two young boys and one girl, then took his shoes off the bottom of the bag.

“Apparently it’s a portrait of him and two friends, all three of them low-profile metahumans. The thing is, Japan’s been using a Metahuman Registration law since before we were born, so even if Jen doesn’t know about it, the government may know about them. I’m going to try hacking into their databases this afternoon, then running a translator program to see if I can find anything about these three.”

“You’re lucky we’re the only people at the bus stop,” Melvin noted, looking at the picture. One boy with wild hair, of a shade of gray he assumed would indicate brown. The girl wore a uniform, unlike the other two, which made him wonder if her school went longer than theirs, or maybe only hers required the uniform. “So, which one is Jenrya?”

“He wouldn’t say,” Nathaniel said, pulling on his left shoe. Tying it, he got up and hooked his skates to his backpack, then looked over it. “But, I expect he’s the one in the middle. Darker hair than the other two, not quite black, but that was probably just the lighting. The guy on the left has brown hair, and the girl has red hair. He wouldn’t tell me their names.”

“Weird,” Melvin said, giving the picture back to his friend. “Well, just keep it safe. People might wonder why you have a picture of three Japanese kids, and start up the metahuman rumors again.”

“Those only stopped because it’s last weeks news,” said a girl coming up to the stop, grinning at Nathaniel. “Besides, with a bookworm like him, you have to wonder how he really spends his free time.”

“Good morning, Jasmine,” Nathaniel said, looking over the red-head. He quickly stuffed the picture into his pocket, and hoped she hadn’t heard about it. She was well built for her age, and well developed too. She was wearing tight shorts, and a t-shirt just loose enough to pass the dress code. “How are you doing?”

“I’m all right,” she said, looking him over. “I think maybe we should start talking about what superpowers you got, though.” She grinned at him, the giggled. “You think maybe you could show me sometime?”

“If I did have any superpowers besides being able to read 800-page novels in two days and eating anything with Insanity Sauce added, I’m sure I would,” Nathaniel said, pointing to the pocket on his denim jacket. “Unfortunately, most people would hardly count those, since the one eliminates popularity, and the other leaves me with gas.”

“Are you kidding,” Kimberly asked, walking up behind him. “You never have gas when I’m around.”

“I have manners,” Nathaniel said, grunting as he turned around. “G’morning Kim.” She was another of the attractive girls, even though she was the tomboy of the group. Her shorts didn’t have to be tight, and her football shirts left little enough to the imagination. No doubt she could get any guy in the school, and yet somehow she seemed to pay attention to a scrawny bookworm.

“Geez, Kim, why do you even pay attention to him,” Jasmine said, looking at her friend. “He’s scrawnier than Julian, and almost half as cute.”

“Hey guys, it’s the bus,” Melvin said, as the yellow bus pulled to a stop in front of the curb. “Nathaniel, stop flirting with those girls, we have to get to school.”

Nathaniel rushed after Melvin, and was lucky to get the back row seat on the bus. The popular girls always sat at the front, or as close to it as possible. “First you ask the girl I want to the dance, then make it seem like I’m flirting,” he said, keeping his voice low. “I we hadn’t been through so much last summer, I swear I would knock your teeth out. I may be ‘scrawny’, but I still have my sparring matches with Jason every week.” He didn’t mention that Kim obviously thought he was cute.

“Today we’re going to be studying some progressive biology, with the help of Mr. Jan-yu Li,” Mr. Willams said, walking over to the door. “Just remember, if his English isn’t that great, don’t make fun of him like you did when we had Mr. Johnson come in.”

“But he talked funny,” one of the kids in the class shouted.

“You can do that in English class,” Mr. Williams said, “or you can go to detention. That goes double for those of you who already earned it.”

“Good enough,” Julia muttered to Nathaniel, as Mr. Williams opened the door. A dark haired man of Asian ancestry, if not personal origin, walked in, and went to the fron ot the class, where he wrote his name, in english.

“Ohayo, kids,” he said, turning back to the class. “I’m Jan’yu Li, one of the original researchers on the Metahuman projects, at least the ones in Japan. Now, with metahumans becoming more prominent in society, especially in your age group, some of us older researchers decided to take off and go around to share what we can safely share, with students.”

Nathaniel just blinked, and pulled out the picture that had been in his pocket all morning. He looked down at the central boy, then up at the man speaking to them. There was no doubt about it, he decided that if that kid was Jenrya, he was certainly related to Mr. Li, and probably as a son.

“Excuse me, young man,” Jan’yu said, looking back. Nathaniel looked up, and said a soft sorry. Jan’yu continued. “As it is, we actually discovered several different types of Metahumans, who can be classified into five levels. Alpha levels are powerful enough in their own right, and are the strongest of the normal levels we use. Of course, by theory, the first generation would have the most Alpha levels, meaning that if any of you in here were to be metahumans, I wouldn’t be surprised at all if you were Alphas.

“Of course, I don’t expect many of you are really metahumans,” he continued, with a chuckle. “Beta levels are weaker than Alpha, the result of an incomplete dominance between the two power-level gene pairs, while Gamma levels are a result of co-dominance between a single pair, rather than both. The other two levels are considered special. Omega Levels are low power Metahumans, often with abilities that may seem to have no obvious advantage, but when harnessed, their power can grow exponentially.”

Nathaniel rose, without even thinking, and Jan’yu stopped. “May I try to explain Epsilon levels, Mr. Li?”

Jan’yu blinked, and nodded, wondering at the boy. Especially as he started breaking down the difference between theory and reality. “First off, the theory was that Epsilon levels would be very rare for several generations,” Nathaniel started, looking straight at Jan’yu. “Epsilons are metahumans whose power is capable of jumping to a higher level very rapidly, especially in moments of stress, when hormones like adrenaline flood the body. Unfortunately, the most public metahuman of our generation was found here in Grand Rapids. And he was also an Epsilon, as it seems, by my calculations, would be most of the first few generations.

“In fact, my calculations show that every few generations, there should be a boom of Epsilons, followed by a period of Alpha and Beta dominance, then Gammas, then Omegas, followed again by Epsilon surges. It’s a constant cycle which would keep the metahuman community going, much the way a community of rabbits keeps growing.”

“That’s enough,” Jan’yu said, looking at the boy. “Perhaps we can talk over your calculations later, so I can show you your errors? But, you do have a firm enough understanding of the levels of metahumans. What’s your name?”

Think fast, Nathaniel thought, trying to think of a fake name that wouldn’t sound too fake. He failed. “Er, Nathaniel,” he said, sighing. “And I’d love to go over my calculations with you.”

As the final bell rang, Jan’yu waited by the door of the school. He stopped Nathaniel as he was headed out, then took him to a nearby classroom. “I have one question. How do you know so much about metahumans?”

“If that’s the only questions, it’s simple enough,” Nathaniel said, walking to the blackboard. He started writing letters, a genetic sequence, on the board, and left off after a while. “You worked on the project, you probably recognize this. I’m a genius, and it took me two weeks to realize that it’s present in my own genetic code as well as in that of several of my friends.”

“Actually yes,” Jan’yu said, looking over the code. He picked up a piece of chalk and continued it, filling the board. “This one we called Li’s Code, the code behind Alpha levels with amazing potential. I also discovered one that was half Alpha and half Epsilon. But I never expected a child would be so intelligent.”

“I’m not,” Nathaniel said, closing the door. “But we should talk.”

“First, I want to know what you were looking at earlier,” Jan’yu said, sitting down. “You seemed to connect me to whatever you were looking at.”

“You just, look like someone from a picture a friend sent me,” Nathaniel said, being careful not to risk being caught in a lie.

“Does this friend have a name?”

“I don’t know if it’s his real name,” Nathaniel said, pulling out the picture. “This is it, maybe you know him?”

Jan’yu looked at the picture in shock, seeing his son and two friends. “Yes, I do,” he said, softly. “I thought I taught him better than that, though.”

“That’s what I thought. Jenrya is your son, right?”

Jan’yu cursed himself silently, and nodded. “And he’s a metahuman. I haven’t done the tests to see what level he is, but I expect that it isn’t one of my codes. And if he realizes it already, I’m certain that he doesn’t have one of mine in his x-codes. Look, you’ve already told me more than I expected, but I have a warning now.”

“Be more careful with sharing my knowledge,” Nathaniel said, half questioning. “I figured you were a safe enough bet. Beyond which, I can convince the student body that my only superpowers are the ability to eat spicy foods and to read quickly. They already think I’m a metahuman.”

“You’re a telepath?”

“Wrong guy,” Nathaniel said, standing by the door. “Give Jen my regards.” He left the room, warping his energy signature to a spot where no one would see him reappear.

Julia had gone home early, with a headache, and was miserable in her room. Her computer beeped, the familiar tone that meant incoming team messages. She held her head as she got up and awakened her computer. She guided the mouse quickly through the menus to the hidden program for reading the messages, and typed in her password.

“Hey hey ho,” the message read. “New kid on the block meets a cute chick and looks her up. Guess who’s got a government file on her, Jules?”

She panicked and loaded the trace function, but it was too late. She started a new message and added double level encoding to it, with a three-try password lock. “Helios, meet me at Calder Plaza at 11. Bring Beta. ~!~@ Saje @~!~”

She went down to the basement, and started medidtating, to think over the problem. She needed to keep herself clear headed, especially if someone was after her. Obviously, whoever it is knows me, and somehow made the connection, but who could it be? And who could send a team message anyway? As she tried to figure it out, she let her mind wander, touching and seeking through various people, with no luck.

Nathaniel returned to his coding after school, occasionally glancing over at his book “Get the Most From Your Code”. A few times he would edit a recent string, and a few times he just kept going, not even bothering to watch what he was putting in. When the beep of a team message came, he blinked and switched programs.

He prepared a response quickly, and sent it out. “I’ll be there, Saje. Learned something about my new friend too. ! Helios !”

“Let’s find out about this,” Yamaki said, glowering at the display. It showed a young male, younger than most first time attacks, with silver hair and yellow eyes. A name came across, and Yamaki tried to think of what they had of the file.

“Kazama Yuuki,” Megumi said, turning towards the screen. Her encyclopedic knowledge of the files was almost complete. “We’d projected he’d be an energy siphon, beta level. But he wasn’t supposed to bioemerge for another 2 years.”

“Sir?” Reika looked at him in shock. “I thought you said he wasn’t ready for use yet? Are you sure this is a wise choice?”

“Almost too late,” Beta said, falling on the floor. “Gotta hurry to the plaza… Oh well. I hope they won’t mind.” He jumped out of the window and headed for the Calder Plaza. He needed to move quickly, but couldn’t risk being caught.

“I need to stop living in empty houses,” he muttered, taking note of the street names. “Burton, College… I’ve got a good ways to go before I get there. May as well risk it.” He turned into a rather large sparrow and took to the air. At least it would be faster.

“He’s late,” Helios said, circling the metal sculpture in the plaza on his roller blades. “He promised he’d be here on time, too!”

{Calm down,} Saje sent, shaking her head to stay awake. {Besides, he’s coming. Just give him a few more minutes.}

“You know it creeps me out when you do that for these kinds of things,” Helios said, stopping and sitting on a thermal construct. “Hurry up, man.”

A large sparrow landed next to Saje, and looked curiously at Helios. “You called,” it said, it’s head changing into Beta’s. “Sorry, I had some trouble getting up on time. The shape changing man has no place to lay his head and all.”

“Whatever,” Helios said, sighing. “I have some news on Jenrya. It is his real name, stupid little kid, and he’s Mr. Li’s son as well. But Saje asked us to come, so what is it?”

“Somebody emailed me today,” she said, looking at them. “It was a team message, and I have a government file. I think all of us do, and he probably knows my identity.”

“Do you know who it is,” Beta asked, looking around. “And is this going to affect our plans for the dance?”

“This is serious,” Saje said, looking at him. “And no, I don’t know who it is. I expect he’s new in the area, but I’m too busy to pay attention to all of my new neighbors.”

“We’ll figure it out,” Helios said, nodding. “But it’s almost midnight, and I need some sleep. See you for training tomorrow afternoon?”

“I have ballet,” Saje said, grinning. “It’s a performance.”

“I’ll be there,” Beta said. “I’ll probably even be waiting there for you, Helios.”

They all laughed as they split up, Beta back on the wing. Helios skated home with a flashlight handy, and Saje used her bike.
