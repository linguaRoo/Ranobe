# Digimon Adventure Side Trek 02

## Episode 1: The First Digimental! A Twist of Fate

_Genki here. A few years passed after the others defeated Apocalymon, and all of them got home. Too bad Ben and I weren’t so lucky. We were in the Digital World the whole time, and even though there wasn’t any evil attacking, we were ground zero when the Digimon Kaizer showed up. Actually, Jukohenmon was almost his first slave, except that Coyotemon and I were able to step in._

_I guess he forgot about us though, because he certainly didn’t have any qualms about the rest of the Digital World. He started releasing his Evil Rings everywhere, but most of the time, we were able to keep them from getting too many Digimon. It wasn’t going to be too much longer, though, until we couldn’t handle it on our own._

---

“What is it now? Almost three years we’ve been stuck here, Jukohenmon?”

“Keep your faith, we’ll get home,” said the taller figure. His flesh had a metallic tinge to it, and his voice sounded like it echoed from the walls of a cave. “I know it.”

“Hmm? Hey! What’s this blip on the Digivice? And why is the light glowing green again?”

“The ‘blip’ is probably the Digimental Armor I’ve been telling you about for the past fortnight,” Jukohenmon said, a sad look in his eyes. He looked around for a moment before continuing. “The green light means it’s safe for Coyotemon to try evolving.”

“Great! Let’s go Genki!”

“Yeah!”

“Coyotemon evolve to,” as he said this, he was enveloped in a fiery orange yellow light and grew larger, more hair on his neck and jowls with less on his legs, and the silky feeling was cool enough that it seemed to fight the heat and intensity of his gaze. With a growl first, he finished. “Hibanamon!”

“So, this armor thing should be near here?”

“That is correct.” Jukohenmon looked to the northeast. “That way.”

So they went, keeping an eye out for this new kid, he called himself the Digimon Kaizer, and for anyone who might be able to help them. They were in the middle of a forest when the hole appeared. It had something in it, and the Crest of Justice was on it.

“Jukohenmon, I can’t take that! That’s the Crest of Justice, it’s yours!”

“No, for now it’s yours. Until the seal breaks down enough that you can reverse my condition, it’s for you.”

Genki shrugged, his slightly overgrown hair falling completely over his eyes. Leaping into the hole, he landed about a meter away from the object, and he could clearly see his Digivice glowing, the pale gold color that meant something was coming, something important, like evolution. Examining the armor, he found that it was shaped like a dog’s muzzle, but perfectly smooth, and only the top part. The bottom was two half cylindrical shapes, and in the front was a pair of fangs. Near the back of it were two horn like blades, and Genki looked dubiously at it again.

\[You gain nothing by just looking,] he heard his grandfather say to him. \[You need the initiative to act, not just analyze.] He put his hands on the sides, and gripped the armor. He only used a small tug, and it came up. He now saw that it had eye holes, in the perfect position for a dog-like Digimon. But he already knew that it wouldn’t come apart on it’s own.

He climbed up the wall quickly, and when he reached the top, he found that Hibanamon was no longer Hibanamon. And Coyotemon was fighting. “No! Coyotemon!” Than, with a moment’s doubt leaving him, he shouted again. “Digimental up!”

“Huh?! Coyotemon, armor evolve,” Coyotemon shifted form, he was dog-like, but humanoid now, and the forms of Metallimon, Kanemon, Judamon, and even Jihadmon were reflected as he shifted. They were all humanoid, it made sense now. His paws were left almost un-armored, but the metal bands with their razor claws looked dangerous enough. His lower legs were shielded, and there seemed to be a cloth around his body, while the helmet had covered his head. “Canimon! The Warrior of Justice!”

“Armor?” The Kaizer shook his head in disbelief. “I can’t allow that!” He held out his black Digivice, it was shaped in a more ergonomic form, and it wasn’t for a moment that Genki realized that both of the ones he had, had changed to reflect a similar shape: one of them with a gold trim and the other was pure white. But nothing happened when he held out the black one. Anubimon stayed, he didn’t reverse evolve.

“You can’t affect Anubimon with your Dark Digivice, Kaizer,” Jukohenmon said. “But you probably don’t care. Justice Flare!” He swung his arm out and the air burned a bright white.

“Dart Claw,” Anubimon shouted, holding out his left arm towards the Kaizer. Airdramon intercepted, blocking the attack with his wing.

“You’re no threat to me,” the Kaizer called out from atop Airdramon’s back. “Always remember that, and that I rule this world!”

“He rules no world but his mind,” Jukohenmon said as the Armor reverted, and Coyotemon stood there once more. “Come, we should seek out the other Digimon. They will be of great help, even if none of us can normally evolve.”

“Huh?”

“Look at your Digivice. It’s a D3 model, activated by the ancient powers of the Armor. But, you can see that we’re not able to evolve here anymore, but at the Kaizer’s whim.” He looked away. “Come, Tailmon is waiting this way.” He headed to the southeast.

Alex stood back a good ways watching the battle unfold and looked down to DemiZeirickmon who nodded his lizard like head in response. They stepped out from their hiding place after the battle was over and Alex held his D3 carefully as they walked to where the battle had occurred, and the boy slid down into the crater to see the egg gone and smiled staring back up at his companion, “He took it…”

The Digimon looked down the hole confused, “And that’s a good thing? I thought we were supposed to get here and grab it, or that’s why we were here.”

“You don’t get it… never mind, but it may be worth our while to do something other than wait around.” he nervously fingered the black crest on his neck, being only one thing, failure. “Besides we still have things to do before we can reveal ourselves anyway.”

“All right, all right! Lets just get moving already!” the small black Digimon turned away and walked back from the edge as Alex climbed up dusting himself off at the top.

“Fine, you care to pick which way we go this time?”

“Yeah, that way.” Unknowingly, they began walking in the same direction as a certain Chosen child.

\[So, Tailmon is out here? It’s been a while since last time I saw her.] Back when the Dark Masters reshaped the Digital World, before Apocalymon reformatted it. At times, he still sometimes wondered how the four, or three, of them had survived? \[Almost like something beyond our control.]

“Hey, Genki.” Jukohenmon, his head hanging heavy. “I think we may have some company. My information shows that there could be any number of Chosen children, the Kaizer may even be one of them turned to the cause of Darkness, so it may be one of them.”

“Huh?”

“All that I can say is that it’s a reptilian Digimon of the Zeirickmon family. Not sure which one, but one of them. But, going for us, we know that if it’s Chosen, it won’t be able to get to Champion, Ultimate, or Mega levels, so we have a fighting chance.”

“Just because we have one human, one Champion and one Ultimate? Gimme a break Ben,” he said, taking two reasonable steps away. He noticed Jukohenmon turning his head away, focusing out of the forest.

\[It’s a couple of Monochromon. They had best enjoy their freedom, while it lasts. Ken will get to them sooner or later. Unless we manage to contact the other Chosen, we may not have a chance.] “Genki! I think you should send out another e-mail for Koushirou.”

“Good idea. Tonight I will.”

As Genki sat out in the night, he pulled out the terminal he’d been left. He quickly loaded the e-mail client, and

> Koushirou,
> 
> We’re having some trouble here, not able to go into details. Sorry if this message is a bit vague, but try to make sure one of you gets in here every week, if only to make sure the Digimon are safe.
> 
> By the way, I got your message about Omegamon. Thanks for letting me know about what happened, I’ll try to figure it out too. Also, I found something weird today, and my Digivice changed. I really can’t describe it, but, next time you’re around, make sure to let me know.
> 
> See you around,  
> Kurata Genki

Yeah, Omegamon is a weird bit of work. War Greymon and Metal Garurumon used, what did Paso call it? Oh, Jogress evolution, that’s right, to become Omegamon. One body, two minds, and more power. I wonder what it could be. He ate the fish that Coyotemon had caught, and thought, but couldn’t do more than narrow it down to a few possibilities. Number one: Jogress occurs between friends? Or is it something closer? Anyways, Number two: Jogress can only happen if there’s enough danger? Yeah, that could work, but… Number three: Digimon are assigned a Jogress partner, and when it’s triggered, they can Jogress? That seems to make a lot of sense, but so do the others. I guess we’ll just have to wait and see.

Alex continued on the path he was traveling, hands in his pockets, “Why do I bother? I mean, to be honest we don’t even know if I’m meant to help ‘em or not.” He sighed and turned his Digivice over in his pocket a few times and muttered to himself.

DemiZeirickmon looked up at him, “Its alright Alex, just because you have the Crest of Failure doesn’t mean you are one.” Alex stopped after that comment, it wasn’t something he was very proud of, and one reason he avoided the other Digidestined if he could. “Sorry…” Alex just smiled and resumed walking, things were getting worse it seemed, and he just may have to join up with them at some point, and only fate knew the answer to that question. And fate seemed to be in a joking mood at that moment, as the two would be unknown passers by suddenly stumbled into Genki’s camp, \[Oh shoot…] Alex thought bitterly.

\[So, it is one of the chosen children,] Ben thought, free to his own thoughts while the Judamon side of him slept. \[Alex Beldark, chosen for the trait of Victory… I wonder why he has been trying so long to avoid us? Unless that strange figure… maybe he has been misled.]

As Alex looked at him, he stood, difficulty entering his thoughts as he tried to control this body. That was when Judamon awoke. Their thoughts, so different as the winds from the north and the south, merged seamlessly as Judamon took on the functions of the body. “Alex Beldark?”

Alex startled at the sound of his name from the moving statue. \[Remember, this isn’t Kansas, so to speak, anymore.] “What do you want? I’ll have DemiZeirickmon stop you if you try to attack!”

“Tell me, then, what crest you have.”

“If you know my name, you should already know I have the crest of Failure.”

Jukohenmon nodded. “Indeed. We will meet again, Alex, and then you shall know the truth. You are not destined to fail, but to rise victorious. Go on, Alex. I won’t tell Genki about you. Now hurry.”

Alex stared a long moment at Jukohenmon for a long moment again before leaving, proceeding on his way, “destined for victory,” what a joke. Thats why his entire life had been one failure after another… perhaps he should do something about that… The Kaizer was no threat to him, but he was one of the Digidestined, and he couldn’t bring himself to face doing what he couldn’t… or could he? Perhaps he could… no, out of the question, find your other egg and move on, only then will you be able to do anything. Besides, interfering was not what he could do at the moment anyway.

They all seemed to agree on it, not only was the Digital World in trouble, but in serious trouble. It didn’t seem too bad yet, only a few “dark towers”, or “control spires” as the American kids called them. Then came the latest message. \[Kurata! It’s been months. I wonder why he waited so long.]

“Hmm, not much I didn’t already know,” he said, recording a message for digital playback on Genki’s terminal.

“Also, I really appreciate your trying to work out this whole Jogress thing, but we don’t need it. I figure it happens because of extreme danger, and that’s it. And I’ll make sure that someone does get in there as often as possible, but the Digital Gates have been popping up less and less often lately. I’d send someone as a more permanent ally but that could be too risky. Actually, if you could try and find out more about this Digimon Kaizer, I’d appreciate that far more than anything you get on Jogress. And… I’ve missed having a similar strong mind around. Tell the Kaizer that if he tries to kill you, Kabuterimon and I will eat him for breakfast. Well, I guess this is our goodbye for now. Try to send me a voice email next time though. See you around, and I’ll check in first. Your friend, Izumi Koushirou.”

He sent it out, then looked up. “Hmm, time for bed. I’ll set this up to search for a Digital Gate, then get some sleep.” Opening his door, he shouted out. “Mom, I’ll be up a bit late tonight, don’t worry about it too much though.”

“All right, son,” she called back. “But do remember that you have school tomorrow.”

“Of course,” Koushirou said, half heartedly. Lowering his voice, his words meant only for himself, he continued. “But whatever it is that Genki found, it seems to be a pretty powerful artifact. I wonder…”

A girl looked through the clear noonday sky, her blond hair falling to her waist. She sketched the symbol that had become so routine to her, a circle with three teardrops, evenly spaced inside of it. “Hagurumon,” she whispered, placing her Digivice in the center.

A gate opened, and she gasped at the once familiar feel of falling upwards, and out of sync with her friends. She grinned as she flew across the rift between the two worlds.

“Kimberly,” Hagurumon shouted, flying up to her. “You’re back, you’re back!”

“Yeah, I guess I am,” Kimberly said, looking herself over. She had grown in the past three years, and her tastes as well. But she could feel that something was happening in the Digital World, something none of the Chosen had expected. “But there’s danger now, isn’t there, Hagurumon?”

“There sure is,” Hagurumon said, sighing. “Come on, I’ll tell you about it once we’re someplace safe. Follow me.”

Taichi slept, but his dreams were very troubled. He awoke with a start, and found that he couldn’t remember what had startled him so much. Not wanting to worry his sister, he just sat in his bed, trying to make sense of what he could remember of the dream.

It was a threat of no value, compared to some of what he’d faced. But obviously it was a greater threat than he wanted to hope. “Agumon, I’m coming,” he said, lying back down. “First thing in the morning.”

“All right, I’m listening,” Tailmon said, looking at Coyotemon warily. “Now explain why you’re suddenly so much more concerned about the Kaizer.”

“Because he’s becoming a real threat,” Jukohenmon said, silencing Genki. “His Evil Rings are spreading far through the world, and he is enslaving enough Digimon to become a threat. Tailmon, we wouldn’t have sought you out if it were otherwise. You need to get everyone here, they need to learn about this threat. Or even just tell them to beware of the Kaizer.”

“Well that’s just common sense,” Tailmon said, wrinkling her nose. “I have to go now. I hope we don’t meet again too soon.”

As she disappeared, Genki stared in disbelief. For a moment, he almost saw her in the way she’d acted when she was in Vandemon’s army. Then he noticed her tail. The ring that had always before hung there was gone, and he didn’t know what to say.

A young boy with crimson red hair fell into the branches of a very strange tree. Blinking several times, he ran his tongue around his mouth, then over his lips, and sighed. “It’s another one of those days,” he muttered, exactly as the branches supporting him broke. He crashed onto the ground and sighed. “How much worse can it get?”

“Oh, wow! Are you a Chosen?” The voice came from a small dog-like creature, with dark brown fur and pink highlights. “Hey, cool! That’s a Digivice, isn’t it?”

“A, a what?” The boy looked at the thing in his hand, and gasped. It was shaped a bit like a bean, with a yellow pad on each side, and a small square screen surrounded by a yellow circle. “Where am I? Who are you?”

“I’m Lopmon,” the creature said, jumping on his head. “And I’m your partner!”

“Yeah, great,” the boy said. “I’m Kevin Allman, from Bozeman. Where is my school?”

“What’s a school?” Lopmon looked very confused. “Is that a food? If it isn’t, I don’t think we have any here in Digiworld.”

“Okay,” Kevin said, looking around. He sighed as he saw that it was late at night here, wherever here was. He managed to make out a cave, and headed for it.

Especially when his hand came out holding a weird bean-shaped toy. “What the fuck?”

## Episode 2: Hagurumon, Armor Digivolve!

_Kimberly here. I was lonely, and hoping to see Hagurumon again, so I drew my Crest in the dirt and set my Digivice in it. Well, I was certainly shocked to find myself in the Digiworld a few moments later, with Hagurumon back at my side. But as I’m about to find out, I’m not the only member of the Bozeman Digidestined who was in the Real World._

“Where the hell are you taking me,” Griff shouted, lifting up his Digimon by it’s head. “Damn, you’re heavier than you look.”

“Put me down,” Tapirmon said, struggling in Griff’s grasp. “

“These are the Digimentals,” Renamon said, standing in the center of the room. “Gabrielle, one of them is yours, and will give me the power to digivolve.”

“One of them for each of us,” Tapirmon said, moving away from Griff. “You have to find out which one is yours, though. We don’t know.”

“Damn shit,” Griff said, looking around the group. “Gabby? How the hell’d you get here?”

“I could ask the same of you,” she said, turning aside. She walked over to one of the Digimentals, and looked it over. It looked like an old-fashioned oil lamp, the kind she had seen in paintings portraying the Revolutionary War. It had a bell shape on it, with one dot inside it. “May as well try it.”

She took a deep breath as Kevin looked around. “This is one weird trip,” he said, walking over to one of the other Digimentals. This one looked like a giant M&M, and was decorated like a strange easter egg. “As good a try as any,” he muttered, tracing the shape of a bowl on the top of the Digimental. He grabbed it by the sides, and prepared to lift it.

“This one must be mine,” Kimberly said, running to the next one, with her crest drawn on it. It was shaped like a jester’s hat, and was even pied like a jester’s costume. Her hands wrapped around it, and she took a deep breath.

“Fuck,” Griff said, going to the last one. “No way this cutesy thing is mine!” It was a pink something. He didn’t even know what it looked like, just that it seemed like something too cute for his tastes. “But I may as well try.” The shape of a sunburst caught his eyes as he grabbed it.

All at once, the Bozeman Digidestined lifted. “It’s mine,” Kimberly shouted triumphantly, holding up the Digimental of Wit.

“Wow,” Kevin said, falling over as the Digimental came off its stand, much lighter than he’d expected it to be. “Weird.”

“No luck,” Gabrielle said, sighing. “All right Griff, let’s switch.”

“Damn straight,” Griff said, stepping to her first choice. He lifted it in one hand, and blinked in shock. “Damn this is light.”

“Then this must be mine,” Gabrielle said, pulling on the last Digimental. “Cool.” It came up in her hands, looking like a fancy cup, sort of like a solid wine glass.

“Evacuate this cave immediately, trespassers,” came a deep voice. “The Digimon Kaizer commands it! If you haven’t evacuated within two minutes, I’ll be sending in my minions.”

“Kaizer?” Kimberly looked at Hagurumon, her grin fading quickly. “What is he talking about, Hagurumon?”

“He came a short while ago,” Hagurumon said, closing her eyes. “He has a new Digivice that keeps us from digivolving. It’s sort of like the ones the new guys… and you, have. Except his is black.”

“So a human is trying to take over? That means there’s a new threat, and these three are supposed to help me stop it.” Kimberly looked around, and nodded. “Kevin, you’ve always loved reading those adventure books, now you get a chance to live ‘em.”

Kevin’s eyes widened. “You… You noticed? But you always acted… a chance to live my dreams… And this Digimental thing is the key?”

Griff snorted. “The dork and the loner girl,” he said, looking at his toy. “Figures that I’m gonna have to save the world. The stories always throw my type in this role… guess I’ve got no real choice. Especially with my little sister at risk too.”

“And I’m definitely in,” Gabrielle said, grinning. “No more homework!”

“Your time is rapidly running out,” the voice of the Kaizer said, contempt in it. “Or maybe you don’t understand me. In that case… Greymon!”

The roof of the cave shook as something hit it, and Kimberly looked to Hagurumon. “Let’s go! Let’s make light of his shadow, partner. Digi-armor energize!”

“That’s the spirit,” Hagurumon said, feeling the energy get through the block, reaching her. “Hagurumon armor digivolve to, Jestmon!” She looked like a court jester from old England, or at least the fantasy books. “You three, stay safe in here. I’ll handle the Kaizer for now.”

“All right, Kaizer,” Jestmon said, stepping out of the cave as though he were a clown. “You’ve invaded my world, and now it’s time to see who really handles things here.”

“Feel free to try me,” the Kaizer said, his eyes narrowing behind his sunglasses. “Meramon!”

With a roar, a dark red and orange fire golem burst through the trees, and looked over the clearing. “Burning fist,” he said in a monotone, throwing a fireball at Jestmon.

“You call that power,” Jestmon said, jumping over the fireball, and rolling towards Meramon. “I’ve seen Babies that were stronger than you, Meramon! Fire cracker!” From the end of Jestmon’s staff came a burst of fire and light, not designed to hurt the enemy, but to distract them. “Goblin grenades!” She began launching orange bombs at Meramon.

“Airdramon, go. Meramon can handle this.” The Airdramon nodded, and left, just as the other children came out. “Teach them what happens to those who defy me, Airdramon!” A shower of needles poured down from it’s wings, and it flew off. “I have business to attend to.”

The bombs were not holding out overly well, though they were keeping Jestmon from taking a single hit. “Hold it,” Gabrielle said, looking at the fight. “If they’re fighting each other… Renamon, do something!”

“Of course,” he said, crouching. With a powerful thrust, he rose above them all, and closed his eyes. “Ko you setsu!” A focused hail of energy projectiles rained down on the Meramon, breaking his concentration long enough for one of the pumpkin shaped bombs to hit.

“Go for that black ring on his arm,” Kimberly shouted, watching Jestmon slide along the ground like a gel. “Hurry!”

“Let’s shake hands,” Jestmon shouted, slamming her right hand on the ring. It shattered under the force of the unnamed attack, and Meramon collapsed to the ground. As he did, Jestmon’s armor faded, shooting as energy towards Kimberly’s Digivice. “Well, that wasn’t so hard. And thanks for the help, Renamon.”

“It was nothing,” he said, looking to the horizon. “It’s not going to be safe here for long, children. The Kaizer will return.”

## Episode 10: The Kaizer in the World of Darkness

Foreword:

Digimon: Side Trek and Digimon: Side Trek 02 are my stories based around the two original seasons of Digimon Adventure, or Digimon: Digital Monsters in America.

This episode takes place after the episode “Ken’s Secret”, where Daisuke discovers, Ken’s secret (in the Anime this is episode 02-08, or episode 62). This is a short episode, and focuses, primarily, on Ken’s actions while in the Dark World, specifically when he enslaved the Hangyomon. If time constraints allow it, I will be merging it with D:ST 02, Episode 09 to make a single episode.

---

Ben: _I guess that it’s my turn to fill you in this time. Well, first, we were attacked by the Bakemon that Ken, the Digimon Kaizer, sent to keep us from reaching the other Digidestined. Genki used the Digimental of Trust and Coyotemon evolved to Kerberomon, who cleared up our ghost problem, but left us in a jam when this dark cave appeared. Genki led us through and we eventually reached the ocean on the other side of the cave, an ocean where everything was inverted! Man, what have we gotten into this time?_

---

Genki is looking at the ocean, Kerberomon standing behind him. As the dark wave of the distant lighthouse passes by, not only does Kerberomon de-evolve, but Ben’s silver D-3 begins to glow. “Huh? Why is my Digivice… Genki! It’s finally time for you to act, you can separate us now!”

“You mean, back to your normal form? All right,” Genki said, grabbing the silver D-3. He held it towards his friend, shouting the command. “Spirit Split Shinker!”

A silver light burst forth, engulfing Jukohenmon, and splitting him into his basic forms. Ben stood there for a minute, his green hair out of place in the dark world, and Judamon immediately collapsed. “Sorry, Ben. I never meant… for this to happen. I’m too weak to stay at this level any more, but maybe someday… Judamon, reverse shinker!” Energy released from his body as he shrank to his normal form. “Metallimon.” He stood there, looking like a metal Gotsumon, and grinned.

“I wouldn’t have it any other way,” Ben said, smiling, as he fell on his hands and knees. “Now, we have to figure out why we’re here. There must be some reason.”

Genki nodded, helping his old friend up. “I’ve missed you, Ben. It’s good to have you back.” As he gave Ben the D-3, he glanced up, spotting Airdramon. “It’s Ken’s slave,” he said. “He’s here on Airdramon… But what can we do about it?”

“Nothing,” Ben said. “Metallimon and I are too weak to be any help, and that dark light is preventing Armor Evolution. All we can do is head back, and try to catch up with one of the warps where the gates are.”

“Yeah,” Genki said, turning around. “I suppose, but… I wish we could do something.” The four turned back, and returned the way they had come.

“So, you won’t serve me? No problem, I don’t need you to agree,” Ken said, looking at the shadowy figure. “That’s why I have these! And first, I will take the Hangyomon, so you won’t have any subjects. Wormmon, release the Evil Rings!”

“Whatever you say, Ken,” Wormmon said, frowning inwardly. Though it was his pleasure to do what Ken wanted, he was afraid for his partner. He hadn’t been the kind boy he used to be, not since he started coming back and proclaiming himself the Digimon Kaizer. Now, not only was he using his dark Digivice and the Evil Spirals, but he was using something with an even darker origin, the Dark Towers.

“How many times must I tell you,” Ken shouted, lashing at Wormmon with his whip. “Always refer to me as Master!”

“You cannot even control your own subjects,” the shadowy figure said. “Or perhaps you should put one of your Evil Rings on him as well?”

“Quiet, Dagomon! I control your former subjects now,” Ken said, sneering. “Now, tell me where the others are!”

“No,” Dagomon said. “You may be able to enslave some Digimon with your Evil Rings, but some of us are too powerful for you. My allies, like myself, are all too powerful for you, so don’t even attempt it. Leave our domain now, Digimon Kaizer.”

“Then I’ll find them myself!” Ken shouted, not caring anymore about the anger he was feeling. Furious, he turned away. “And I’m leaving Airdramon to oversee my domain here.
