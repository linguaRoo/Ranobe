# Bal Keldin – Bal the Superman

## Bal Keldin

A blue skinned alien who uses his superhuman strength, speed, and senses to fight evil forces and criminals. His chest is emblazoned with a red crest which amalgamates the letters “K” and “X”, and he wears a gray suit. His arms and feet are bare.

### Human equivalent: Superman (alien who fights crime and evil)

## Knight of Destiny Bal Keldin (_kuNadir Neim Bal Keldin_)

A demigod who must achieve a heroic destiny among humans before he can ascend to godhood, Bal is a Gramurn knight in this incarnation. This was loosely based on Gramurn myths and legends, and most of the monsters and enemies were taken from native Atrusian mythologies, not Adin legends. He wears a crimson helmet and gloves, and his crest is a gauntlet which has been raked by claws. His squire and lover, Liyer, marks the debut of Liyer in the Keldin stories, and sets the precedent of a female ally.

### Human equivalent: Herakles/Hercules

## Keldin Tales: The War of Ages (_Keldin Kendan: Katan i Apokhe_)

- Sometimes considered a deviation, the Keldin Tales built a rich world of swords and sorcery in which the exploits of Luko, Rhea, and the Bal Rebellion fight against the evil Rak’shao Imperium. Featuring all the races of Atrusius and several invented by the author, this story has spawned more derivative works than any other Keldin story. Liyer is initially a soldier of Rak’shao who turns to the Rebellion along with her commander, Enkidu.

 Our Equivalent: Star Wars.
- Luko = Luke
- Rhea = Leia
- Bal Rebellion = Rebel Alliance. Unlike SW, War of Ages occurs on a single GIANT world.

## Magical Doppelganger: Bal the Superman (_Kha Larngu-Din Bal Keldin_)

An assistant to Professor Luko, Bal volunteers to undergo an experimental magical enhancement after he is injured in an attack by Slag, a magical superman serving the evil Professor Rak’shao. In this experiment, Prof. Luko grants Bal the power to doppel enemy powers. Liyer is Bal’s sister and fellow assistant. Enkidu appears as a rival who declares no loyalty to either mankind or the supermen.

In Magical Doppelganger, Bal’s colors are blue and sky blue, and he has a blank crest, which adapts to reflect the power he is doppelling at the time. The traditional gray and red color scheme for his outfit is, instead, found on both Liyer (lighter gray) and Enkidu (darker gray).

### Human equivalent: Mega Man (original/X)

- Bal = Rock/X
- Enkidu = Blues/Zero
- Liyer = Roll.

## Keldin Adventures: Bal the Mercenary (_Keldin Jotsonu: Bal Ragunes_)

In a world ravaged by time and war, mankind can only eke out a meager living. Those who dare to explore monster-infested ruins for remains of humanity’s former greatness are hailed as heroes and bear great respect. The Mercenaries who seek these out for their own gain all seek the fabled treasure known as the Dragon’s Hoard, and Bal is one of these mercenaries. Liyer, his cousin, and their grandfather Luko operate a small mercenary outfit, traveling between ruins in search of the Dragon’s Hoard.

Often considered a deviation as Bal is a normal person who simply uses normal magic and weapons during his dig-runs. This Bal, however, returns to the traditional colors of red and gray, and is revealed, late in the series, to be a descendant of the Supermen who caused the fall of civilization. After this, Enkidu begins appearing, attempting to use Bal to destroy the remains of humanity so the Supermen can create their own society.

Though small, a dedicated fanbase exists and continues the stories of Keldin Adventures both before and after the Bal era. A number of these attempt to link this world to the worlds from Magical Doppelganger and Cyber Soldier, though the author insists that Adventures is a world entirely unique unto itself.

### Our Equivalents: Megaman Legends, Fallout

## Cyber Soldier Bal Keldin (_Kybrau Lektakh Bal Keldin_)

In a computerized world, humans interact with the internet through mind-operated programs called Avatars, and cyber-battling between Avatars is a recreation almost everybody enjoys. Diveball team leader Bal Kieres rises to the challenge when TerrorNet steals the top secret Avint program designed by his father, and with the help of his own Avint, Tempo, Bal fights to disrupt, and eventually capture, TerrorNet.

Despite making Bal and the Superman into two separate people, few people consider this a deviation of the formula, as Bal and Tempo are very closely tied to each other. Most major characters are doubled up in this series, such as Liyer and her Avatar Lyre, Luko and his Avatar Enkidu, and avatars and their operators pulled from throughout the history of the Keldin series. Also of note is the faact that in Adin, there is only a very minor distinction between the names: the Bahl operates the Avatar Bal and Liyer’s avatar is Liyr; it is only for English that some characters have such different names.

Despite a tournament arc late in the series, the story is incredibly focused on the development of Tempo and Bal, and the consequences of Avints on the network world. The ultimate conclusion appears to say that Avints are as alive as humans, and only by Avint and human societies being in harmony can either side achieve its full potential. This moral conclusion is ignored by many fans who prefer that the stories continue.

### Our Equivalent: Mega Man Battle Network.

Very similar to the Animated Series, right down to a ‘backwards’ salesman (Dogard) running an organization that also combats TerrorNet, though the tournament is downplayed compared to season 1 of the Rockman.EXE anime.
