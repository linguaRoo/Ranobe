# Keldin Tales: The War of Ages & World of Ages

This third iteration on the concept of Bal and supermen focuses primarily on the story of Luko Romarin, a young city guardsman in the independent holdings of Valnort who discovers he has magical and psykickal gifts after meeting the reclusive hermit Farshan. After the Imperium claims sovereignty over Valnort, Luko is dragged into the Bal Rebellion by Farshan, but as a young man who values freedom, he is happy to fight with them.

## Broad Story Outline

### Imperium Rising

For many years, Valnort has been far from the military fronts of the Imperium, but all of that changes when a speaking brass helmet (calling itself Sedryk) falls from the sky onto the road before Luko. Sedryk claims it must deliver a crucial message to Farshan before the Imperium invades. Luko reluctantly abandons his duties on road patrol to bring the helmet to the reclusive hermit.

After meeting Farshan, Luko begins to develop flashes of foresight warning him of upcoming danger, and he begins to feel magic waking in his blood. Farshan warns him that the Imperium regulates magic and psykick powers, but offers to teach him how to wield these new powers if Luko accompanies him.

They flee Valnort just as the first wave of Imperium emissaries arrives, demanding that Valnort surrender peacefully. Luko tries to turn back, believing he can save his lover, but Farshan stops him. The Imperium may be conquering Valnort, but they have no reason to destroy it.

As they travel away from the Imperium forces, Farshan begins to train Luko in the use of his powers, until they find a large Imperium checkpoint ahead. Luko is awed when the Imperium soldiers simply accept Farshan’s suggestion that they have all of their travel permits in order, and Farshan explains that they were lucky there were no high ranking commanders involved. They stop in a trading city and book airship passage to Atemal, the place where Sedryk’s message originated.

When they arrive at Atemal, they find the city full of Imperium soldiers, and are unsure of how to make their way to the palace where Crown Princess Rhea Jenesia is being kept under Imperium supervision, but when they eventually do so, they learn that she is also blessed with gifts like Luko’s and is hiding them desperately from the Imperium. While they are hidden, they overhear the Death Knight, Liyer, taunting Rhea: Liyer is Rhea’s elder sister, and voluntarily renounced the crown of Atemal to serve the Imperium, being trained in necromancy. She says that Lord Enkidu will be arriving soon to formally test Rhea for her potential, and will pressure their mother into allowing Rhea to serve as well.

Farshan gives Luko a map, and offers to fight Liyer and Enkidu as necessary to buy Luko and Rhea the time to escape. If Enkidu arrives, he confesses he will not be able to escape, because even though he once trained Enkidu, Enkidu is more powerful than he is.

## The Path of the Phoenix

Rhea and Luko escape the palace in the middle of the night, knowing that Farshan is sacrificing himself for their sake. They stop near the edge of Atemal long enough to retrieve what initially appears to be a pile of brass scrap, but is actually the rest of Sedryk’s body. Sedryk admits that as a construct, he is not built to fight, but was the instructor of the sisters and possesses vast knowledge of the known lands. Examining the map, he informs them they will be pursuing a path called Karadu, the phoenix path, and that if the map is correct, they will find a rebellion outpost at the end of the map Farshan provided.
