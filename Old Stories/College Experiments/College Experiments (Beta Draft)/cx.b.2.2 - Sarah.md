Sarah left her rugs outside of her bedroom door, and let Melanie deal with the cables. “I’m going out for a walk to get used to the campus,” she said with a smile. “Do you want me to pick up a drink on my way back?”

“Uh, no, I’m good with water,” Melanie answered as she stretched her arms out above her head. “Thanks for the offer.”

Sarah left the dorm and went over her plan. She needed to check in with Kelsie and make sure she wouldn’t interfere. Melanie was a project, and she wanted to enjoy it, but it was going to take time to do this project right. In the meantime, Kelsie would give her room if she could just get the right idea into her. Also, she had to make sure Kelsie knew that her room was allowed to have guests any time Sarah wanted. As long as it didn’t go past their floor, it should be fine.

Maybe the girl from downstairs yesterday should have a few suggestions too? Probably, Sarah told herself. She started by walking the halls, looking for the ruby red hair Kelsie had. When she didn’t see a sign, she started looking at the nameplates on the doors. Most of them were blank, but a few had been filled in with markers. “Holly & Maria” was near the stairway, with heavy block letters for Maria and flowery cursive for Holly. Sarah decided to take the stairs down.

The redhead from the front desk was on duty. Sarah didn’t have a good approach for someone who was sitting at a desk. Rapid inductions meant catching someone off guard, and that was easier when they were less prepared. Someone sitting and waiting to be approached by people was more guarded than someone overworked assigning dorm room and running around.

“You normally down here, then?” she asked.

“They usually have staff here during the year,” the girl said with a smile. “But they like having upperclassmen for the welcoming session. You happy with the dorm so far?”

“Oh, yeah. This is your building, though?”

“Yeah,” she said. “Penny Fox. And you were Mackenzie? Sarah, right?”

“That’s me,” Sarah said. “And there’s a dorm party tomorrow, right?”

“Yeah, dorm life party in the quad and lobbies,” Penny explained. “Starts at like six, I think? I haven’t really bothered with it since I was a frosh, but I guess they’ll want me or one of the RAs here on the desk until staff comes in on Monday. Where ya headed?”

Sarah looked to the door and shrugged. “Nowhere in particular. I just got lost trying to cross the quad yesterday, so I want to get familiar with the campus.”

“I wish more of you froshes would do that,” Penny sighed. “But lost crossing the quad is a new one. What were you after?”

“We wanted to get some food,” Sarah said. “We were halfway across the campus before we realized the nearest union location was just on the north side of our quad.”

Penny laughed at that. “That’s what asking for help is about. Glad you found it, though.”

“Yeah. Guess I’ll be off on my way,” Sarah said. “I hope we see other sometime when you’re off duty.”

With that, Sarah headed outside into the midday sun. It was still very much the middle of summer, and she was glad she’d dressed for the season, with a white tennis skirt and deep purple sports bra and matching tank top, white socks and her purple running shoes. Today’s goal was a run to the north quad and back, and see if she saw any cute subjects around.

The whole trip was about an hour, and she made sure to stay near the various dorm buildings. There were a lot of cute buys that she clocked as open to her advances, but the girls were what really interested her. Redheads, brunettes, raven-hair. College was the place to be to find cute girls who might be useful to her as she developed her techniques for recruiting, or even just to be her subjects as she improved. She paused for a while to stare and watch a trio of goth girls wearing all black, including leather skirts and high-heeled boots, until they went into the north quad’s cafeteria. They would be fun, she thought to herself, making a note on her mental checklist to, “Have fun with goth girls.”

Then she continued her run, turning around and making her way back to the south quad. As she waited at a crosswalk, she heard the sound of a skateboard and she turned to look. It was her new friend from the day before rolling down the sidewalk, but this time, Sarah was ready. She listened carefully and as the skateboard got close, she made her move, stepping backwards and turning with her face away from the skateboarder.

“You crazy cur,” the voice shouted out as the sound of the skateboard came to a stop along with the clack of it being kicked up. “You almost walked right into me!”

“What? Oh my gosh, I can’t believe you,” Sarah said, putting on her best innocent act. She finally turned to face the skateboarder, and found a young man with a shaved head and no helmet. “You almost run into me and act like it’s my fault? Don’t you know it’s illegal to ride that on the sidewalk anyway?”

“Fuck you,” the boy said trying to turn around.

“No, fuck you,” Sarah said, reaching out and grabbing his shoulder firmly. She pulled him with almost superhuman strength and spoke firmly. “Stay and listen, boy. Calm down, relax, and just listen to me for a moment.” As the boy relaxed under her touch and responded positively to her words, she moved into an induction. “Come sit on the bench, and relax. I’m safe, just a harmless girl you almost hurt. You owe me a moment of your time to make it up to me.”

Sarah didn’t push her luck too much. The road wasn’t silent, and while foot traffic was light, it was a risk. She learned the boy’s name was Ryan Rockford, and that he shaved his head for solidarity with his little sister’s cancer. After gently encouraging him to be more respectful to people he nearly hit, Sarah finished with a simple suggestion to try and find her at the library next week. Ending with a suggestion to apologize if he accepted her ideas, she paused and licked her lips. She wasn’t sure how well it would hold, but she had to try, and this boy could be her first real guinea pig. Kelsie was a necessary pawn. Penny, if Sarah could get her, would be the same. Ryan could be someone she pushed her chances with, as long as they could meet in more private, and calm, settings.

“Sorry I almost ran into you,” Ryan said as he looked around blinking. He stared at Sarah for a moment before saying, “Wha, what happened here? I don’t apologize to chicks, are you some kind of witch?”

“Yes,” Sarah said bluntly. “And now that you’ve apologized, my spell is complete. I’ll see you next week, little boy.” She stood up and stepped away from the bench, struggling to hide the smirk she could feel. The crosswalk signal was lit up for her to cross, so she started to resume her run and crossed the street, only allowing her smirk to come out once she heard Ryan yelling after her.

“I ain’t never gonna see you again, you crazy witch!”
