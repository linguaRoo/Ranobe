Saturday was uneventful. Sarah fiddled with her writing on her laptop, trying to make sure it her old fairy tales were actually looking the way they were meant to. “Why can’t this computer just work like the PCs at school,” she muttered more than once as she struggled with the minor changes in the keyboard shortcuts and special key locations.

Eventually, she had asked Melanie to run across the quad to pick up drinks and pastries from the Café du Beagle in the student union. She’d managed to get a copy of their menu during their last trip to the union, and picked out what she thought sounded good. She wanted a vingt thé vert glacé au ginseng and a croissant à la crème, and she figured Melanie could get whatever she wanted. $20 would cover it, and she expected a receipt and change.

Melanie had come back with Sarah’s order and some kind of fruit drink and a bagel for herself, and then she had shut herself in her own room to do Sarah could only imagine what. She hadn’t had such a boring day all summer, and was already regretting her choice to go back to school and actually let some men dictate her studies.

“Sure,” she muttered to herself as she actually started work on a new chapter of Alice’s Adventures in Wondering-Land, deciding to see if at least she could amuse herself. “Technically I’m choosing my courses, but the old guys make the curriculum.”

> Alice continued to wander through the green field borderd with hedges. “This is such a queer place,” she said aloud, though she saw nobody who could hear her. “It’s almost as if someone wanted to keep something in here, but then forgot to let it in at all.”

> She saw a gate at one end of the field, where she had entered, and a tea set arranged on an iron table painted in stark, bright white. Making her way to the table, she discovered it was just the right size for her, though it had no chair, and it was laid out with two biscuits and a pot of tea. “I really shouldn’t,” she told herself as she picked up a cup off the table and examined it.

> “Fill me,” the cup said, and she wondered if she should do it, but she thought back to the last time she’d seen something like this. The small cakes in the room of doors had said “Eat me” and when she’d eaten them she had grown bigger and bigger, until she was pressed against the walls and doors. Then she’d found the bottle and it said, “Drink me” and it made her shrink more and more until she could fit through a crack in the wall.

> Her days had gotten curiouser and curiouser since then, with the white rabbit giving her tea and making her work as his maid, and then when she finally, literally, grew out of his house, she’d been chased down the road until she shrank again. The Duchess had tried to take her in, but after being shrunk to the size of a baby, Alice had just cried and cried until she was sent off with the mailman. Growing and shrinking had her so confused over who she was anymore, and even who she should be.

> So should she fill the cup? It seemed to want to be filled. Given everything else that had happened, she assumed the teapot must be full. But she was also in the looking glass land. Maybe it was like the cake, that had to be served before it could be cut? Which came first, the full cup or the drinking?

> Slowly, beyond her own awareness, the cup had begun to fill up with her thoughts and memories. Everything she remembered or thought became another drop of tea in the cup. She was just staring at it, every time she started to realize what was happening, the realization turned into a drop in the cup, outside of her head.

Sarah was startled by the knocking on the door. She had let herself get wrapped up in her writing, and it was almost 4 PM. “What’s up?”

“Uh, I was wondering if I could use your shampoo,” Melanie said, opening the door just enough to talk clearly. “Apparently the bottle I brought from home kind of exploded in its bag.”

“Sure,” Sarah said, sitting upright and crossing her legs. “So, while you’re here, can I ask you if you’ve ever read… fanfic?”

Melanie blushed and nodded. “Yeah, but I’m kind of picky about what I read.”

“I was just wondering,” Sarah said and looked to her computer. “I’m working on a story I started a few year ago. I just got inspired to add a new chapter, but…” She let out a deep sigh and shook her head. “It’s based on Alice in Wonderland. But… ah, never mind. It’s not important. Take your shower. My shower stuff is under that table.” She pointed to a small table by the door.

Once Melanie had pulled out the bag of Shower supplies, and then gotten the shampoo out, Sarah looked back at her screen. She knew where she wanted to take it, but she couldn’t really publish it now. She was going to start writing out her plans for Melanie in her fanfic, and when she finally did publish it… it would be too late for anybody to undo her work.

> The cup was almost full and Alice was drooling when the Red Queen came along the scene. “Oh, dear,” she said, walking around Alice several times. “You’ve gone and gotten yourself into a mess, young lady. You’re lucky I found you instead of one of the nastier types. You’ll never make it across the board and become a queen without my help.”

> Alice didn’t even register the words she was being told. If she tried to think, the cup just got another drop. If she tied to do anything, the cup filled faster. All she could do was nothing, and it felt horribly pleasant to be blank, empty, and unable to do anything.

> “Well, this is the forgotten tea garden,” the Red Queen mused. “And the tea here is brewed with forget-mes, so you must be quite blank now. White would probably try to sabotage you. You aren’t one of her pawns or mine, really. I have no obligation to help you. But you are making this game so much more interesting than usual. So here’s what I’ll do, little pawn. Alice, right? You have a very important choice coming in just a few more ranks, what kind of piece will you be?”

> The Red Queen looked Alice over intently for a few minutes, careful not to touch the tea cup. She took in the young woman’s appearance, and nodded several times, appreciating that this woman was quite ready for anything right now.

> “Very well. I want you to think about how it feels to be blank like this. Not now, but once you finish your tea. Think about how it feels to belong to one of the sides and just fill your role. You can choose what you become when you reach the palace in the eighth rank, but since you aren’t declared as Red or White, you can also choose to become one of our units. If you become a queen, you can go home, but if you become my soldier, or White’s soldier, you will be able to belong. Freedom or belonging is such a difficult choice…”

> Alice simply continued to drool, her cup starting to spill over as the Red Queen spoke. “Alice, you always make the right choice, because deep down you know what you truly want. Before you even think, you make the right choice. Now… drink your tea before it all spills out and you lose your mind forever.”

> With that, the Red Queen moved Alice’s hand closer to her mouth, so the girl could reclaim her memories from the cup before she lost everything. From the first taste of the tea, Alice began to move on her own, drinking the tea and feeling all of her thoughts flooding back. Once the cup was empty, the Queen very quickly pulled it from her hands and set it back on the table.

> “What happened, your majesty?”

> “This is the Forgotten Tea Garden in Rank 5,” the Red Queen explained. “You picked up a cup that is used for forget-me-tea, and it started to draw your memories and thoughts out. You’re lucky I found you, if your cup had started to spill without anybody here to help you drink it… Well, you’d have been a pawn for whoever did end up finding you, and sent back to the palace on your side for training. And pawns are not a happy lot unless they’re obeying. Only a rare few have what it takes to actually become a queen.”

> “Well, I need to become a queen to return home, don’t I?” Alice blinked a few times as she tried to remember everything. Where she was, who she was, the enchantment of this garden seemed to draw her away from herself.

> “Yes, if returning home is what you want,” the Red Queen said with a coy smile. “Now, I can take you to the next file, and point you towards rank eight, but you’ll need to figure out the rest of your journey for yourself, Alice. Remember, even though you’re filling in for a missing white pawn, you aren’t really part of either side right now, though only we queens know it.”

> Alice nodded slowly. “Thank you, your majesty. I’ll do my best.”

Sarah saved her writing and then closed the window on her computer. She still had a lot to write, but too much anticipation was building up in her. She had to get ready for her own shower, and smooth out how she was going to start actually working on Melanie. After all, forget-me-tea was just a trick in her story. There was no way to trick Melanie into emptying her own mind into a teacup.

That would require actual work.
