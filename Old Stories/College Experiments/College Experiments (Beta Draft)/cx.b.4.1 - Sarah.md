“Hey Melanie,” Sarah knocked on the bedroom door and waited a moment. “I know you aren’t in psych, but I was hoping you could take a look over my essay.”

Melanie pulled her headphones off. She wore them more and more since classes had started. Sarah wondered what she might be listening to, but for now it wasn’t important for her to know. “I guess I could use a break from this tedium,” Melanie said, looking over to her roommate.

Sarah smiled and offered her a notebook and a red pen. “Here. I prefer to do my draft manually, but if it looks good I’ll type it up. Do you want some… privacy?”

“That’s alright,” Melanie said, standing up before she took the offered notebook. “I’ll read it in the common room, get away from the screen for a bit.”

Sarah nodded. “How about I run to the Beagle for drinks while you read it?”

“That sounds nice. They have an apple pie latte, but… not a big one. I don’t wanna be up all night.”

Sarah nodded and slipped her shoes on, a simple pair of maryjanes that seemed to fit with her black pants and pale green button-up shirt. “Apple pie. I’ll get a douze, then?”

“Yeah, thank you. For… everything.” Melanie blushed as she looked towards Sarah at the door, and then nodded. “Oh, and a bagel, please? With cream cheese.”

“Sure thing,” Sarah said with a cheerful smile. There was a psychological principle of reciprocation, and most people never thought about it, but when people are kind to you, or generous, you feel a pressure to return the kindness or generosity. She was making lots of small generous and kind actions to get Melanie to trust her, but her plan would be in motion soon. “I’ll be back in a bit. Don’t forget to mark my paper up.”

Sarah opened the door and headed off before Melanie could respond, but if she’d done her job right, Melanie would be ready for a first test. Sarah had always used her voice until now, but… written word should be just as effective, and her handwriting would be easy to read without distractions.
