Melanie took a deep breath and closed her eyes. She remembered hearing how someone had tried to roofie her before classes even started, and how Sarah had worried about her so much. Reading this paper was the first chance she’d really had to make up even a bit of what she owed Sarah for saving her from… whoever the guy was.

“Alright, let’s get reading.” She fiddled with the pen and made a small test mark on the top of the first page. Sure that the pen would work, she read. “The importance of meditation,” she whispered to herself, starting to read the paper out loud. It was about mental health and well being. She let herself just read through it, marking where it seemed like there might be something to fix. 

She took her time and went back to the start after finishing, to see if there were any other problems. She zoned out pretty heavily as she read through it, making new marks on her second time through, relaxed and focused and just feeling what had to be marked. At the end, she felt like there must be something more, so she read it a third time, slower, taking in the ideas behind every word. She made a few marks, and when she got to the end, she wrote a note on the page, though her precise writing seemed to be incomprehensible.

Within a few minutes of writing the note, she heard the door open, but she was just trying to read the note she’d put on the end of the essay.

“How are you feeling?”

“Confused,” Melanie answered. “I made all the marks I was supposed to, but I’m not sure what I wrote here.”

Sarah lifted her chin and looked into her eyes. “That’s great, Melanie. Its easy to forget things. It’s easier still to remember to forget. And you’ve forgotten what you wrote, but every time you read it it becomes more true even though you remember to forget it whenever you see it.”

“Remember to forget,” Melanie repeated and nodded. “I’m good. I remember to forget.”

“Yes, you are. And for now, all you need to remember to forget is that you’ve ever been hypnotized. You read an essay I wrote using hypnotic language, and so you’ve been hypnotized my me, but you just remember to forget that and everything except for proofreading my essay. You remember clearly that you finished reading it three times and found as many things to mark up as you could. And you remember to forget that you obey.”

“Remember… read three times. Good proofreader. Forget that I obey.” Melanie stared blankly into the eyes in front of her. As she stared, she only knew that they were beautiful green eyes with the tiniest flecks of gold deep inside of them. They controlled her like the words, and then she forgot that she was controlled, that she obeyed.

“Now Melanie, close your eyes and lean back. In a moment, when you hear me click my tongue, you wake up remembering that you’re done with my proofreading, and remembering to forget that anything else happened. You just closed your eyes for a break once you were finished.” The beautiful eyes pulled away just a little bit, showing a face that Melanie couldn’t quite place, and with the words ending, she nodded slowly and leaned back on the sofa. As she did, she felt her body relaxing more and almost going limp.

CLICK.

Melanie opened her eyes with a start. Sarah was standing by the still open door, holding a cup carrier and a bag. “Oh, Sarah! I finish your essay, it was really good…” She looked down at the page and saw her red marks. “I found a few things you could improve. And… what did I scribble here?” There was short red mark at the end that just looked like a scribble, and Melanie blushed slightly, feeling hr breath catch as she looked at it. “It feels familiar, but I can’t place it.”

“I’m sure it’s no big deal,” Sarah said as she closed the door. “You said you needed a break from tedium. I hope proofreading my essay helped.”

“Yeah, it felt really nice to just focus on words instead of code. And your handwriting is really pretty,” Melanie said. “I’ve never liked my handwriting.”

“If you want to improve, I could help,” Sarah said with a smile. “If you wanted, I could even hypnotize you to help you practice improving your handwriting.”

“Uh, no thanks. I’ve never been hypnotized,” Melanie said and smiled. “I’m not sure if you could hypnotize me.”

Sarah just smiled. “Well, why don’t we find something to watch on the TV before our drinsk get cold? I even got you an extra cup of cream cheese since I know how much you like it on your bagels.”

Melanie smiled and nodded. “Would it be alright if I read your essay one more time after we finish watching TV? I think I could find some other improvements you could make.”

Sarah smiled and sat down right next to Melanie. “Id love all the help you can offer me,” she said, pulling a large cup out of the holder, and tearing open the bag that the Beagle had decided to close with a sticker. “Want to be my first reader for all my essays?”

“If they’re half as interesting as this one,” Melanie bit her lip thouhtfully, then leaned forward to brab her bagel and the plastic knife they’d provided for the spread. As she opened the first cup of cream cheese, she spotted the scibble again and let out a sigh. “Sure. I may not understand all the words, but it’s still nice to just focus on the rules of writing.”

Sarah smiled and kissed Melanie’s cheek. “That sounds great. Oh, so I was talking with one of my Yahoo! friends in Germany the other day, and apparently he thinks it’s hilarious that there’s a group just called ‘Eight Please.’ So, sitcom? Drama? What are you in the mood for?”

Melanie thought for a minute, but drew a blank. “I can’t think of anything. You pick, Sarah.”
