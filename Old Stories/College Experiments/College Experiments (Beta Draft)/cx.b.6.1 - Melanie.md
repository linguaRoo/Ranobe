Melanie decided to let Sarah pick all of her drinks. She was still uneasy about drinking in public after August, but the club that Sarah had chosen had a techno DJ and no alcohol. They only served something called “foxtails,” which Melanie discovered were mixed fruit juices that looked like cocktails.

Sarah had even dragged her out on the dance floor after buying both of them some glowing bracelets, and eventually Sarah had pointed out two guys who were watching them. “I’ll go see if they want to get to know us better,” she’d offered before giving Melanie a very light kiss on the cheek.

Melanie was glad that the overwhelmingly blue lights of the club would hide how red she felt from all of this. As Sarah strolled over to the guys, Melanie bit her lip as she tried to reconcile what she was doing tonight with everything she’d been, and also the decision to figure herself out. “Am I this kind of girl? Do I want to go to clubs and hook up with random strangers?”

She was wearing a glowing neon-green gel bracelet on her left wrist. She looked at it and thought about the last time she’d even seen anything like this, when her family went to a fireworks show up north by grandpa’s hotel. She’d had fun that summer. They hadn’t gone up to the hotel very much since then, unless it was to save money on the price of a vacation.

BUt… why was she in a club now? The music was good. Nothing she was familiar with, just a live DJ mixing techno. The foxtail drinks Sarah had gotten her were good. Sweet, just a little bit tangy, and colourful. Melanie wasn’t a club girl. She hated crowds and showing off. But still, she wanted to try new things. Why had she decided to try going to a club tonight, though?

She thought back and her thoughts short circuited when they hit the fuzzy btis of her game with Sarah earlier. Hypnosis had felt good, and interesting. Part of it were pretty clear, like when they started and Melanie repeated what she agreed with. Other parts were vague, like she knew she had a map of her priorities. Some parts, however, were just fuzzy. It was as if they just felt good and she wasn’t sure what they were, but even thinking about them made her feel better.

“Hey Mels, we should talk with these boys for a bit and see if they’re a good fit.” She stepepd aside, the two boys had come over with her, and she made introductions. “This is Greg, and he says he’s going to be a rock star and I’m hot, so he clearly had good taste. And his friend Dave, here, was eyeing you up so I offered to introduce you. Mels, tell Dave something about yourself.”

Melanie froze as the first reflex halted. In her mind, she knew nobody called her Mels. But when Sarah did, it felt friendly and nice and… right. She wanted to try being more sociable. Talking with some random boys would help her with that skill, and maybe help her be better with interviews and stuff. “Hi Dave. I’m Melanie,” she started. “I’m interested in experimenting to find out who I am.”

She raised her hands to her mouth and gasped. Had she really just said that? Why the hell had she just blurted it out like that? Now she sounded like some kind of trashy girl who just wanted a fling. But maybe for tonight that was fine?

“Mels, relax.” Sarah reached out and held her shoulder for a few seconds as Melanie felt her mind calm down and her body loosen up a bit. “I’m here for you. Just talk.”

They talked for a while. Dave was an upperclassman in the mechanical engineering program, and wanted to follow in his fathers footsteps designing robots. He was fascinated by machines, and that included computers. Melanie got really into the conversation when he said that he had been one of Acht Bitte’s first fans, and that he enjoyed heavy industrial techno.

Eventually, Sarah pointed out that it was getting late. “Mels, we should invite them back to our place for a chat.”

“Yeah,” Melanie barely registered what Sarah said, because she was enjoying Dave’s company and she did want to keep talking with him. “You should visit us for a coffee so we can keep talking, Dave.”

Dave agreed all too readily, and then they were off.
