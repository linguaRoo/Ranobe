Melanie was going to be a porject, but she was also going to be Sarah’s masterpiece. Building connections with her would be important, so that Melanie would make most of the decisions on her own in their relationship. That meant taking it slow, and letting Melanie feel like she was Sarah’s equal, for now.

“I’m going to order a pizza,” Sarah offered. “What would you like?”

“Anything except hot peppers is fine,” Melanie said. “How do you feel about deep dish?”

Sarah smiled. “I’m fine with that. I’ll check at the desk to see if they know any good places. You sure you’re okay with whatever I get?”

“Yeah,” Melanie said. “I’m just getting hungry.”

Sarah smiled and went into her room, thinking about her plans. Should she try to get something she’d expect Melanie would appreciate, or something she’d feel obliged to enjoy because she wasn’t paying? It was a delicate game, but this early on it might be best to not force herself on Melanie too much. She pulled on the shorts and top she had worn earlier, then crossed the living space in her bare feet to head to the lobby.

As she got to the elevator, she sighed. She’d been so hoping to secure her influence over Kelsie that she’d even stayed outside longer than she originally planned. Still, Melanie was her main goal. Her first major step was coming soon. Before their first day of classes, she had to offer Melanie her study trick.

A blonde girl was at the counter, though not the stereotypical yellow-blonde, her hair has a hint of gold in it. “Can I help you?”

“Hey, yes.” Sarah acted like she’d been expecting this girl all along, and wasn’t disappointed to not see Penny. “I was wondering if you know any good pizza places that deliver?”

“There’s some menus on the bulletin board,” the blonde said, pointing to the other side of the main doorway where a corkboard was set up with a variety of menus and ads stuck up. “You can take business cards, but please leave the menus for other students. When you call in, most places will put their menu in the order.”

“Okay,” Sarah said, walking over. She looked over the menus and found chinese, sandwiches, even burger places were offering delivery to the campus. Finally, she spottd one that looked promising, Motor City Dish, a pizza and pasta shop. They had a bunch of business cards pinned up by the menu, so Sarah took one and then looked at the menu. She decided to get their Temptation, and she noted that all of their specials were named after Motown acts. She pulled her cellphone out of the pocket of her shorts and looked it over, dialing the number.

“Yes, I’m calling to place an order for delivery,” she said. “I’m on campus at Ferris.” She gave her building name and the guy seemed to think for a moment. “Yes, a new student in the dorms. My name is Sarah. I just want to order a Temptation. Yes, I know what’s on it,” she hissed out. “Sure, add in an order of bread sticks. No, I don’t need a pop! How much? Okay, yes, I have cash. Great. Thirty minutes? Alright.” She gritted her teeth. “Yes, thank you.”

“Sunshine on a cloudy day,” the girl at the counter murmured as Sarah ended the call. “Good choice, but those breadsticks will make you fat.”

“Who asked you anyway,” Sarah said, turning her attention to the blonde. “Sorry, sorry. I just forgot to eat earlier, so I’m really hungry.” She paused for a moment, and then started to walk back to the elevator. “Oh! I forgot to give him my room number. Maybe I should just wait down here. So, can we talk?”

It turned out the blonde was named Sarah Freebower, and as Sarah waited for the delivery, she was able to use conversational techniques to bring her into a receptive enough trance that Sarah F. was drooling as she listened to the induction patter Sarah had developed. The test suggestion was to volunteer the name of her latest crush or romantic partner, only after the pizza had been delivered.

By twenty minutes, Sarah was getting bored of the idle chatter, and her eyes lit up when she saw a man in a red and white chekered shrit come up to the front door. As he came inside, he looked to Sarah F. “I got a pizza here for a Sarah? Didn’t give me a room number.”

Sarah F smirked. “Well, I’m a Sarah and I’m not in a room,” she said as she twirled her hair around one finger. “Must be for me.”

“You got $12.79 for it?” the guy said, almost flirting with Sarah F.

Sarah held back a scoff as she stepped towards the awkward tableau on a man who looked to be at least thirty flirting with a 20-ish year old girl. “That’s my pizza. $20 and keep the change,” she said, reaching into the same pocket she’d stored her cell phone in. “You don’t deserve that kind of tip, but I can’t stand to wait for you to give me change, creep.”

“Whatever,” he said, taking the money and setting the pizza in her hands. As he walked out the doors, Sarah could swear she heard him calling her, “Prude.”

“Ugh. I was gonna get you a discount by flirting with the dude,” Sarah F said, rolling her eyes. “Jimmy Cooper is probably better than the virgin pizza guy anyway.”

Sarah nodded and opened the pizza to take a look at it. At least it was still hot and the cheese hadn’t slid all over. “Who?”

“Star running back on the team,” Sarah F explained. “He made over 500 yards last year? Don’t you pay any attention to sports?”

“I guess not,” Sarah said, taking a deep breath of the pizza. “Sorry to interrupt your great scheme, though. You want a breadstick?”

“No, gross! I told you those things will make you fat,” Sarah F retorted. “Just enjoy your food and don’t bug me again unless it’s an emergency.”

Sarah smiled and shrugged. The suggestion had worked almost too well. “Maybe you should ask Jimmy out, though. See ya!” She headed back to the elevator, and after a quick trip up to her floor, returned to the room with supper for herself and Melanie.
