After a few hours of watching sitcoms, Meleanie let out a big yawn. “I hate Professor D’s assignments. It’s so boring,” she said as she reached for the remote. “Just looking at code and adding comments, or explaining what’s wrong with code. We’re not supposed to write any code at all, it all has to be explained in stupid plain English.”

“I’m sure there’s a reasonf or that,” Sarah said as she stretched her arms above her head. “Like you have an English class, right? Why do you spend so much time reading and writing about essays instead of just your own thoughts?”

Melanie paused and thought for a moment. She looked to Sarah and then raised her hand to her chin. “I’ve never thought about it. I guess so we can see what works and doesn’t?”

“Something like that,” Sarah said with a gentle smile. “Sorry, I did some tutoring in high school, so… I enjkoy helping people connect the dots.”

Melanie nodded. “So Profssor D is is trying to make us think about good and bad programming?”

“Analysis. Plus, if you work in a company,” Sarah led Melanie.

“I would have to work with other peoples’ code sometimes,” Melanie said as she thought about it. “So I would need to be able to understand their work, even if it isn’t commented. I might even need to fix it.”

“See, it might be boring or tedious, but it’s practical.” Sarah sat up and picked up her water glass, swirling it slightly to make the small amount of water left swish about. “I think I’m going to take some personal time now. It’s been fun watching cheesy shows with you, though.”

Melanie nodded and pushed herself up to her feet. “I used to watch these kinds of shows with dad. It’s…” she took a deep breath. “It’s nostalgic to just chill out and watch them. Thank you.”

Sarah just finished the rest of her water and waved her free hand dismissively before standing up and heading into her room.

Melanie sighed, but she got it. Sarah wasn’t affectionate. Even snuggling, Sarah ahd been pretty passive. She talked a big game about sex, but she was… distant. Melanie just sighed and took care of the trash in the living room, then headed back to her room. She still had a bit of coursework left, and Sarah had helped her get a new perspective on why the assignments she had were so different from what she’d expected.

She set a timer on her PC for an hour. She was going to need sleep, after all. With the timer set, she put on her headphones, opened up Calliope, and picked one of her techno playlists. She set to work on her assignments and just let herself zone out until the alarm went off. She made more progress than she’d expected, and as she saved her work, she thought over the weeks since school had started.

---

It had been almost a month since she’d met Sarah, and Sarah had confused her. Sarah had made it clear she was going to bring people over to the dorm, and she had kept that promise. She’d had a few girls and guys over at night, though they were always out by curfew. Melanie didn’t pay them much mind, since Sarah brought them into her own room.

That didn’t mean she hadn’t noticed them. Most of them left looking very pleased and also almost dazed. One of the girls from the reception desk during the move-in weekend, the blonde one, had come by and left giggling like someone after a first date.

“She was flushed too,” Melanie whispered as she thought back over it. She shut her PC down and left her headphones on the desk. “Did she and Sarah… do those kinds of things?” She pulled out her diary and opened it. All she had to do was write something for today. Anything.

She took a deep breath and looked back a month.

> I’m not a lesbian. Am I? I mean, Sarah is… cute. Hot? I mean, why am I even thinking about this? Joan helped me realize that these thoughts can only lead me astray, but…

She remembered that she had gotten up while writing this entry. She’d gone to use the bathroom, and when she got back, she had just written from a different point.

> She can’t make me gay if I already am. But am I? Should I be? Do I want to be? I’ve always just been the girl dad and church said I should be, as true to myself as I could be in that environment. Joan was a church counselor, so of course she’d say that being gay is bad. Becca was gay, though, and she never seemed like a bad person.
> 
> Becca… she was my best friend until 10th grade. I never got any letters from her, even though she promised. I miss Becca. She got me. Eve and Libby too. Why did Becca have to leave in the middle of our campaign? What am I saying, writing?
> 
> Joan always told me that I can’t be gay and a Christian. Do I have to choose? Which one will actually be right?

Melanie flipped back to the first blank page remaining, picked up her pen, and wrote. For the first time since she was 12, she didn’t just write in her quick and precise block script, but in cursive.

> My name is Melanie Dijkstra, and I have to be honest:
> 
> I DON’T KNOW WHO I AM.
> 
> I don’t know what I believe. I don’t know what I want. Im not sure what I need.
> 
> As of this 21st day of September, 2001, I promise to be curious.
> 
> I promise to explore and find my own answers.
> 
> I promise to myself that I will accept whatever answers I find.
> 
> I promise to myself… that I will love myself, whoever it turns out I am.
> 
> Next time you read this, I hope you’re keeping these promises.
> 
> Love,
> 
> Melanie

As she set her pen down, Melanie felt herself flush with the intensity of what she had just felt. It was true, it was right. And it was liberating to tell herself something she’d always wanted to hear.

Joan would never tell her to love herself, “no matter what.” Dad had used to, but ever since Melanie was 10, he’d gotten more distant. Dorothy had never liked her enough to say anything close to support, never mind love. Tonight, she was going to start putting her church and her step-mother in the past and steer her own life for a while.

She noticed an awkward scribble after her name and squinted at it. Closing her diary, she put it away in the locked drawer of her computer desk, and the undressed. Breathing a deep sigh of relief, she turned off the lights and crawled into bed. Tomorrow would be another day.
