Melanie nodded. “I drive, but I don’t have a car.”

“A lot of people drive the same route regularly. It becomes normal to them, and as they get used to it, they can handle other small tasks, like listening to the radio or adjusting their heat. Not enough to distract from driving, but small things.” Sarah paused for a moment. “That driving is sort of like a trance. A relaxed and focused state of mind.”

Melanie nodded. “Okay. So.. You just want me to repeat things I agree with, and do those things if I want to?”

Sarah nodded and smiled slightly. “I see you get the rules. But I need you to be listening to what I say, because if you agree with something before I finish it, I could trick you into something you agree with at the start, but not at the end.”

Melanie paused for a moment. “Would you do that?”

Sarah pursed her lips for a moment before she continued. “Let’s start.”

Melanie blinked once and looked Sarah over. “I’m ready.”

Sarah started off with simple statements, and Melanie only agreed with one out of every few. When she did agree, Sarah was confident that it felt good. As Sarah went further and further, she started saying more and more things that Melanie would agree with. Some of them were simple facts, either in general or in their specific moment.

“You only repeat after you think,” Sarah said.

Melanie paused for a moment before she agreed. She seemed to need to think about it. “I only repeat things I’ve thought about.”

The game went on for a while, with Melanie getting more relaxed and calm, until Sarah was confident she had taken all of Melanie’s focus, and that the other girl was choosing to play along properly.

“You obey the rules,” Sarah said.

Melanie paused. Sarah wished she could tell what was going on in the other girl’s head. “Yes, I follow the rules.”

“You repeat after me,” Sarah said.

Melanie nodded. “I am repeating you.”

“I set the rules.”

“You made the rules.”

“You obey my rules.” Sarah bit her lip nervously. By this point, Melanie had closed her eyes for a few minutes, but this was a difficult one.

“I obey your rules,” Melanie said, not changing anything except the person. This was what Sarah had been waiting for.

“Good. Now you can still repeat what you agree with, but we’re moving to a second part of this hypnosis game. I’m going to ask you to do things. Each time you do something, you decide to do it or to not do it. When you do what I ask, you feel yourself drifting further into our game. And remember, there’s a simple way to end this. Do you remember what happens if you say Cinnamon Drop?”

Melanie paused, processing everything. Sarah just watched eagerly. “Game gets more real when I obey,” Melanie said clearly. “We stop playing if I say those words.”

Sarah nodded. “Now, I’m going to ask you to do ten things. When I ask, you answer me with Agree or Refuse, based on whether you want to do that thing. If you agree, you do it and go deeper into the game. If you refuse, you relax and stay right where you are in the game. Are you ready?”

Melanie’s lips moved without a sound for a few seconds. “Agree.”

Sarah had so many ideas, but she wanted to start by getting an agree. “Number one. Melanie, would you let out one of your pigtails?”

There was almost no hesitation. “Agree.” She reached up and pulled the black elastic band out of her hair on the left side. As her arm returned to rest, Sarah could see the relaxation increasing.

“Number two. Melanie, would you let out your other pigtail?”

“Agree,” Melanie reached up and pulled the band out of her other pigtail. Both of the bands dropped to the floor once her right arm was resting.

“Number three. Melanie,” Sarah paused, wanting a refuse that wouldn’t jar Melanie enough to use the safe word. “Would you call me Master?”
