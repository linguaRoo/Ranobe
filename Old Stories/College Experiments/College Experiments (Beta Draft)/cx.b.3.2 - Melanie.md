Melanie let out a heavy sigh as she flopped on her bed, and then a grunt as she remembered how firm the dorm bed actually was. She was just glad she’d flopped onto her back, and not her breasts. The last thing she needed was to crush those.

“I don’t wanna do this campus life thing,” she moaned as she undid her towel and let herself just lie in the open air. Her body shuddered with the slight chill, but she’d dried herself off pretty well.

What was it Sarah had said? Fanfic, of course Melanie had read fanfic. She even wrote some Matrix stories. And she’d written some for that cartoon where the kids got sucked into the network back in the early 90s. NetGardz, wasn’t it? It had been 3 years since she’d even thought about that cartoon, but she did remember the story she had written about Les and Kim getting stuck in a deep cave, and the things they had done that she had no place writing. Not that it was anything bad, but…

She sighed and let go of the thought. Her therapist had taught her not to get too wrapped up in thoughts like that. They weren’t good for her, they were distracting. “It’s okay, Melanie. You don’t need to think about it. You aren’t…” she swallowed and closed her eyes. She didn’t like having to tell herself these things. 

She’d already joked about it with Sarah, hadn’t she? It didn’t matter if other people were lesbians, why should it matter if she was lesbian? But she couldn’t be a Christian and a lesbian, her therapist had told her so many times that even thinking about it was very close to a sin.

“She can’t make me gay,” Melanie whispered, trying to breathe slowly, but choking for an instant. “I… I want to be my best. She can’t make me gay. Even if she’s beautiful… even if she’s gay… she can’t make me gay.”

She tried a new idea in her head. “She can’t make me gay… if I already am.”
