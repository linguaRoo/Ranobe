Melanie had gotten herself composed, and dressed for this campus life thing, even though she didn’t really want to participate. But, she should meet some other students. There were probably some others in her program in nearby dorms, and… besides, the program she had gotten from the front desk said that one of the night’s acts, in the quad, would be a local techno group called ACHT BITTE . Dorothy never let her go to concerts, but Melanie did enjoy techno.

“Come on,” she said, knocking on Sarah’s door. She had established the next rul for their dorm life: Closed bedroom doors while dressing. Not that she was ashamed, she was just uncomfortable with the idea of having an audience.

“I’m almost ready,” Sarah shouted through the door. “You don’t have to wait for me, though.”

Melanie sighed and walked over to the counter. Looking over the flyer again, she read the entertainment options. In addition to ACHT BITTE, there was a dance DJ called QWIKMAN inside the southern student union, a metal group (unnamed on the flyer) at the north quad, and apparently there was a hip-hop thing called a cipher outside the library. The metal show might be interesting, but there was no way Melanie was going up to the north quad alone. Besides, she knew she liked techno.

Sarah came out and looked at the flyer and she started to work her hair into a braided queue faster than Melanie had imagined possible. “What’s this, “Eight Please?” After clicking her tongue loudly, Sarah looked to Melanie. “Is that supposed to be German?”

“Techno group,” Melanie said, blushing a little bit. She tried not to stare at Sarah, but Sarah dressed like she knew just how attractive she was, which was… well, Melanie imagined most guys would call Sarah an 8, or maybe more. On looks, it was easy to see her being a model or supermodel. In the tight purple t-shirt with it’s low-cut V neck and black leather trousers she was wearing, it was hard to not think Sarah wanted to flirt tonight. “I was thinking about hanging near their act. I actually really enjoy techno.”

“It’s alright,” Sarah said as she finished off her braid and tossed it over her shoulder. “Four acts across campus. Free barbecue and entertainment, get to know your classmates. It almost sounds boring. I wish they said anything more than just metal, though. I guess Techno is probably the best if I don’t even know what kind of metal they’ll be playing.”

“You’re a metal-head?”

“No, I just like metal,” Sarah scoffed. “I really prefer instrumental, but I learned bits and pieces of German from my brothers’ metal collection.”

Melanie relaxed a bit at that. She wasn’t sure she could imagine Sarah actually being a metal-head, but Melanie has heard some decent metal songs at church events. She’d even owned a Griddle album before it went missing sometime in the last year. And she’d also heard a really cool sounding song from some Dragon-themed band online during one of her older brother’s rare friendly moods. “Okay. I mean, metal is fine, but… If you prefer instrumental, the best techno doesn’t have lyrics.”

Sarah smiled and headed over to the door. “Loosen up, Mels.”

“Nobody calls me that,” Melanie interjected immediately.

“Mel?” Sarah scrunched up her face and looked Melanie over, taking in the disapproving look. “Come on, can’t I have a nickname for you? Anything less than three syllables for when we’re just having fun.”

“I don’t really have a nickname,” Melanie said, stepping towards the door to block Sarah’s path. “Nothing I’d want to be called, at least. I guess…”

Sarah smiled. “Alright, Melanie. Melly. No, Melly sounds too much like an airhead,” Sarah said. “But I understand. I wouldn’t want you to call me anything my big brothers would have called me.”

Melanie sighed a bit. “Alright. So… shall we head out?”

“Of course,” Sarah said, offering a very over-the-top bow to Melanie. “After you, miss Melanie.”
