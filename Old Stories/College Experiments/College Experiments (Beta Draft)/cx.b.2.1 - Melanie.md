title: cx.b.2.1 - Melanie
---

Melanie had agreed to let Sarah use her computer for emails and checking her writing, provided that Melanie wasn’t doing her own work on it, and was in the dorm. She didn’t want Sarah downloading any viruses or weird programs on her brand new Gazebo 8890, so she had to supervise. She was getting herself set up with her very own Eagle Online subscription, too.

It was surprisingly easy to set up, and the campus had high-speed internet available in the dorms, but Melanie still needed an actual account to connect through. She’d decided to go with perennial “free hours” distirbutor Eagle Online mostly because she knew them, and already had an EagleChat account. Sure, EagleChat wasn’t technically reliant on Eagle Online anymore, but she was loyal to her brands, and Eagle Online did still offer what they called “easy setup” for EagleChat users.

After running the built-in modem to dial-in to Eagle Online and set up her account, she was offered access to the Eagle Online Student Plan — $4.99 a month as long as her computer was attached to a university or school network, or $7.99 for high speed service through a school network. She got everything set up before going out to the common room where Sarah had made a stunning green salad.

“Help yourself to the salad,” Sarah said, setting up a TV set between the doorway and the sofa that she had moved into the far corner. “Apparently these sofas are pull-outs. And Kelsie told me the dorm rules are okay with having guests, even boys as long as they’re out before midnight.”

“That’s great,” Melanie said, looking through the salad. It was just rich green lettuce, cherry tomatoes, shaved carrots, cucumber chunks, and a lot of white mushrooms. “You’re not a vegetarian are you?”

“No, but I don’t need meat all the time,” Sarah said, looking over the common space as if she was trying to see something that wasn’t there. “Salads are great for lunch. Is that a problem?”

Melanie shrugged. “Wait, how are we going to hook up the cable with the TV all the way in the middle of the room? They give us free cable service, you know.”

“I’ll let you figure that out yourself,” Sarah said. “In fact, would you be a dear and help me get the DVD set up after you eat? I’ve always been more focused on the aesthetics than the technical bits.”

“You’re kidding. You brought a TV and DVD player and want me to set them up for you?” Melanie piled a load of the salad into a bowl, then looked in the fridge. Sarah had picked up a vinaigrette, and while Melanie didn’t have any strong feelings about it, she added some to her salad. She would have preferred a French or Russian dressing.

“I’d rather we both have the opportunity to watch,” Sarah said. “Maybe we can enjoy Alien or something together? And it’s a fair trade, isn’t it? I bring the equipment, you do the setup, we both get to enjoy it.”

Melanie sighed a bit and walked over to the sofa Sarah had fully moved. “Sure,” she said. “But I don’t want either of us tripping over the wires in the dark. You got anything to cover then with once we finish?”

Sarah sighed. “Obviously. I have a few rugs,” she said. She walked over to the counter and made herself a bowl of salad too. “Come on, isn’t it great to be on our own now? Making decisions for ourselves, arranging the whole place however we want.”

Melanie nodded. “Yeah. Middle sisters stand together?”

“Sure,” Sarah said with a grin. “I’ll get the stuff, you do the work, and we’ll be best roomies until we graduate.”

Melanie ate the salad. It was better than she expected, and she decided to say as much. “I’m only used to salads as appetizers,” she said after swallowing a bite. “Where did you learn how to make this?”

“Learn?” Sarah scoffed and shrugged, taking a bite of her salad. After she swallowed, she explained, “I had a friend who liked salads, but I actually made this recipe myself. She wasn’t quite as impressed as you.”

“Maybe because I’ve never had a salad as a meal,” Melanie said, smiling. “Were you… y’know?”

“Oh, she was just a friend,” Sarah said, finally moving to sit down, and choosing to do it next to Melanie. “She was cute, but I didn’t want to be with her or anything.”

“Okay,” Melanie said, blushing a bit at how close Sarah was to her. “Well, do you have a boyfriend?”

“Goddess no,” Sarah said with a laugh. “Boys are too much fun to play with to imagine a commitment. I wanna keep myself free to be with who I like, no ropes on me.”

“Oh, are you a wiccan?”

“Not exactly,” Sarah said, leaning towards the outside of the sofa.

Silence took the room over for a few minutes while they ate. “I’m liberal, not really religious in any sense,” Sarah said softly, turning to look at Melanie. “I just kind of get the feeling the gods don’t care about us, if they are out there. Like they’re… better than us and see us the way we see ants. But… if there’s any worth our respect, they’d have to be women.”

“I’m a feminist too,” Melanie said, blushing and stuttering. “And I’m Christian, but not really that religious. I prefer computers, they make more sense than people.”

“There is something appealing about them,” Sarah admitted. “They do what you tell them. If they mess up, it’s not their own fault, it’s whoever programmed them, right?”

“Yeah,” Melanie said. “Or it’s from pushing them too hard. They still have limits.”

Sarah nodded knowingly. “Well, I want to explore while I’m here. I’m free and I want to be able to have sex. Will it be okay with you if I bring people back here sometimes?”

“I suppose,” Melanie said. “I don’t know if I’ll do that, but if you don’t distract me, sure.”
