Walking into the building, Sarah saw something that looked out of the turn of the 1990s Bright and hot pink carpet covered the lobby of the dorm building, which had wood fixtures and reinforced windows everywhere. The wood had that yellowish stain that had probably been sandy a decade ago, but was now ruined by the finish being loaded with dirt, grime, and probably even some smoke.

She stepped up to the front desk and looked around. Nobody was there at the moment, but there was a sign that said, “Please wait for dorm assignments.”

Sarah let out a loud sigh for show, but there was obviously nothing for it. She would need to wait.

Behind her, a girl with straight brown hair tied up into pigtails knocked on the desk. “Yeah, I don’t think that’s gonna speed things up,” Sarah said, turning to look at the girl. She took in the soft cheeks and rosy lips and almost felt the urge to bite her own. The plain and baggy clothes the other girl was wearing suggested someone who was lazy.

“Well, maybe they should have a bell for service,” the girl said flatly. As Sarah’s eyes roamed over her body, she seemed to get flustered. “Look, I’m just… eager to get moved in. Melanie.” She held out a hand to Sarah.

“Sarah,” she said, raising her shoulders while crossing her arms in front of her. “So you got a major, or starting off undeclared?”

“Oh, I, well,” Melanie stuttered and started a few time before finding her words. “Computer science. Programming, mostly.”

Sarah smirked at that. “Programming? Sounds interesting and useful,” she said, looking around the lobby again. A queue had started forming behind them, but a red-haired older student was approaching the desk now. “Well, maybe we’ll get lucky and be roomies.”

Melanie nodded with a little bit of a blush. “What do you mean useful?”

“I am so sorry for the wait,” the redhead said as she looked over the growing crowd. “There was a… nevermind. Can I see your student IDs, please?”

Melanie got the ID that had been mailed to her ready, and handed it over. Sarah reached into her back pocket to pull it out of her tight jeans. “Mackenzie… and Dijkstra, right? It seems like you’re both on the third floor. The RA for third floor has specifics.”

“Maybe this is my lucky day,” Sarah said with a bemused smile, or maybe it was a smirk, as she headed for the elevator. “Come along, roomie!” Melanie scrambled a bit clumsily, and Sarah just made note of the flustered response. She told herself how much fun she was going to have with Melanie.

Unfortunately, Melanie just missed the elevator, probably because Sarah had held down the button to close the doors as soon as she got in. She’d see more of Melanie soon enough, but she had to be certain she would get what she wanted.

“Student ID,” a woman with ruby red dyed hair said.

Sarah held her ID out, and as the RA reached to take it Sarah grabbed the girl and pulled her close. “Just relax. Calm down, relax, listen for a minute. Can you change room assignments?”

The girl nodded slowly and whispered, “Uh-huh.”

“I need the largest empty room you still have, and my roommate is Melanie Dijkstra. Look here.” Sarah flipped her card around, showing Melanie’s ID to the RA. “When you see her, make sure you assign her to the same room as me. Understand?”

“Yes ma’am,” the RA answered, calmly and flatly.

“Now when I release your hand, forget we had this talk, but my ideas are now your own.” Sarah smiled as she took in how suggestible this girl was in such a very minor trance. “Three, two…” She let go of the girl’s hand and gasped suddenly. “Oh, I’m sorry. You stumbled there for a moment.” Sarah also noted how lucky it was that the halls were empty for the moment, but she told herself it was obviously because most of the students were unpacking.

“I… uh, yeah,” the RA said. “I’m Kelsie Greunwald, third floor RA. If you have any questions or concerns, come see me. And you’re Mackenzie, right? Sarah? I have the perfect room for you.”

Kelsie led Sarah to a corner room on the floor and smiled. “I haven’t seen your roommate yet, so just don’t try to take over everything.”

Take over everything, Sarah thought to herself. “Well, the thought had occurred to me,” she said before Kelsie left. “But I’ll play nice.”
