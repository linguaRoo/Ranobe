# I Don’t Remember… 

## by Irma Vanderbroek

I don’t remember how I got here, how I ended up in this strange den. It looks like a cave, though it’s well decorated with pillows and cushions and sheets and blankets. It looks like a cave turned into a den in haste. Some of the pillows were damaged, feathers or fluff spilling out of them. My head hurt. My neck hurts, like someone had been biting it, a lot. I raise a hand to it, but my fur isn’t matted or stiff. It isn’t warm or sticky either. If I was bitten, someone didn’t want to damage me.

I rise on all fours. It’s cooler than it should be, right? My memory is still all blurry. I’m not even sure what I do remember. It was a hot day. Evening, maybe? The air was heavy with scents, more than I can sort out right now. Broken grass. Hot, fresh blood. A fresh kill from a hunt. But did I make the kill, or was I tracking the kill?

My head throbs and I lower myself on my front paws. This den must have an entrance. Don’t remember, just search for a way out. After a few careful breaths my head settles a bit, and I push myself back up. I can smell the lingering smoke of scentbarks over everything. Whoever lives in this den isn’t entirely feral. But there’s no smell of oil or wood fire. They don’t light this den. The light is weak, but coming from elsewhere, possibly wherever outside is.

I find a darker spot on one side of the cave and pad towards it. My hindpaw catches on something cold. Wet. Soft. Something like a cloth, a rag. A blanket. I choose to ignore it and simply free my hindpaw. But the wet is distinctly thick as I press the blanket down to the floor under my paw. I ignore the twist in my stomach, and the immediate concern as to wondering it’s blood or intimate fluids. I don’t want to know right now. I just want to find out where I am.

I take another sniff at the air and realize it’s not quite still in here. The air is flowing towards the dark spot, which is a good sign. Airflow in caves gets stronger near entrances, so I can find my way out by following it. I notice too late that I’ve stepped on a loose stone, sending it skittering into the dark. I freeze and listen.

All I hear is the air and my own heartbeat. How deep am I? Has my captor gone far? I pause. Captor. Where did that come from. I’m not chained up, I’m not muzzled or collared. I may have no light, but I’m not restrained. I just can’t remember anything. A smell flares up in my memory, something sweet. too sweet. The memory disappears and leaves behind another throbbing pulse in my skull. I don’t remember what it was.

I take a few more breaths, and as the ache dulls I pad forward once more. The tunnel is tall, I could stand up if I wanted, but padding along on all four paws is quieter. I don’t know if I am a captive, and don’t want to alert whomever lives here. That’s all. I try to focus on what’s around me. Watching and tenderly taking each step to remain quiet as a mouse. It’s only a few steps before I need to make a choice. The tunnel from the den goes off to both sides of me, but no further forward. I take a slow glance from my right to my left.

The light seems darker on the left. The air, too, is moving towards the right. So I press my body close to the rough wall, no longer hidden by sheets or blankets or pillows, and follow it. I catch a faint hint of a smell. Hints of florals and oil. Scented bathing oils? In such a crude cave, it was unusual. Right? My head suddenly throbs again, harder than before. I force my eyes closed and wonder. I must have hit my head. I must have a concussion. Maybe I should go back to bed and rest.

But I need to know where I am. I need to find the way out. I can rest once I know I’m safe, once I know where here is. The headache persists. I just need to move. I can’t stay still. I take a deep breath. Then another. And despite the headache, I push forward. I keep my eyes narrowed, moving slowly, seeing a faint flicker further along the walls. A light source!

I pad slowly towards it. Not running. Not even walking, no, as slow and quiet as can be. I begin to hear sounds. Crackling. Bubbling. A pot over a fire. It’s still distant as I round the bend in the tunnel, but the scent. That smell of bathing oils is definitely getting stronger. Another cue to follow, if the light wasn’t enough.

I reflect for a moment. The cave is raw, rough, but it’s clearly been made into a home. The den is safe, secure, and cool. Good for sleeping. Good for mating. The tunnel is probably not as long as it feels, just because I’ve been going so slowly. This is probably some sort of feralist home. Not worked over like… my head throbs. Like… Again. The pain gets stronger. I should have something to compare this with, but all I get when I try is this damn headache.

I freeze as I recognize that fact. I have been captured. I am being kept by someone. I should go back to the den.

No! This is too important. I’ve been captured, and by a mind mage.

I don’t remember what that is. Just that it’s bad. I don’t remember…

The scent of the bathing oils is strong. I fall to my haunches and tilt my chin up. I don’t remember why, I just do. And I feel the touch of blunted claws against my chin. It feels pleasant, even though my mind is telling me to growl, to stand, to keep moving.

“I was lost.” The words slip from my mouth without awareness of why. I can’t turn my head. “I wanted fresh air.”

Suddenly the cloak appears next to me. The paw of the other scritching my chin is not just a sensation, but a presence. I don’t remember this person.

But I know I belong here. I know I love them. I know I’m their mate. And I hear their voice.

“Then come walk with me, pet. I’ll let you outside for a few minutes. And then you can stop remembering so much again. Doesn’t that sound nice?”

I can’t help myself as I bark out, “Yes!”
